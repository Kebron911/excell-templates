# How to Use Your Per-Diem Meal Deduction Tracker

**The STR Ledger · thestrledger.com**

IRS Pub 463-compliant meal tracker. Captures the 5 elements of a deductible meal (date, amount, business purpose, attendees, location), routes between **standard meal allowance (per-diem)** and **actual cost** methods, and applies the correct deduction percentage.

## The percentages (IRC §274)

- **50%** — standard business meals
- **100%** — meals provided to employees (cleaning crew pizza, contractor lunch as compensation)
- **0%** — entertainment (golf, concerts) post-2017 TCJA
- **80%** — DOT hours-of-service workers (rare for STR)

## First-time setup (3 min)

1. Open tab **Settings**:
   - B5 → active tax year (2026)
   - Per-diem M&IE rates by city (defaults: $80 high-cost / $59 low-cost). Update from gsa.gov/travel/plan-book/per-diem-rates if needed.
   - Schedule classification (E or C).

## Every meal you take on the road

Open tab **Meals Log**. Fill a row:
- Date · Property/Trip · Location · Restaurant · Attendees · Business purpose · Amount · Tip · Receipt kept Y/N · Method (Actual/Per-diem) · Deductible % (50/80/100/0)

**The 5-element rule**: if Attendees, Business purpose, or Receipt is missing on a meal ≥ $75, the IRS doesn't recognize it. The tab flags incomplete rows.

## When traveling overnight

Open tab **Per-Diem Log** instead. Fill:
- Date · Travel destination · # full days away · # partial days · M&IE rate

Workbook computes daily $. **Travel must be away from tax home overnight** OR more than 12 hours to qualify.

## Reading the outputs

- **YTD deductible — Actual** + **YTD deductible — Per-diem** = your total meal deduction
- **Per-month chart** — meal $ by month
- **Donut chart** — deduction by purpose

The workbook flags if you accidentally double-counted (same date appears in both Actual + Per-diem).

## At tax time

Sum the YTD deductible amount → enter on Schedule E or Schedule C as Line 24b (Travel) or Line 24c (Meals 50%). Your CPA will route correctly from the Meals Log tab.
