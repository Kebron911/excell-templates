# How to Use Your Partnership Distribution Tracker

## What this workbook does

For STR partnerships (LLC taxed as partnership, not single-member). Tracks: capital contributions, profit/loss allocation by ownership %, distributions paid, ending capital account per partner. Outputs the per-partner figures needed for K-1 prep.

The workbook a managing-partner Sarah hands her tax preparer in February.

## First-time setup (30 min)

1. **Partnership Roster:** partners, ownership %, capital contribution dates + amounts (formation + any subsequent).
2. **P&L Inputs:** annual revenue, expenses, NOI for the partnership (pull from TAX-002 or FIN-006).
3. **Distribution Schedule:** each cash distribution to each partner, with date.

## Reading the outputs

- **Capital Account per Partner:** opening + contributions + allocated profit − distributions − allocated losses = ending capital
- **K-1 Summary per Partner:** ordinary business income (loss), guaranteed payments, distributions, ending capital — the figures K-1 form expects
- **Allocation Reconciliation:** profit allocations should sum to 100% of partnership profit (workbook flags if not)

## At year-end (January-February)

1. Update P&L inputs with full-year data
2. Update distribution schedule with all distributions paid in tax year
3. Verify allocations sum to 100%
4. Print K-1 Summary per partner
5. Hand to tax preparer for K-1 generation + Form 1065 filing

## Important — disclaimer territory

Partnership tax is complex. Special allocations (allocations not strictly proportional to ownership), guaranteed payments, §704(b) rules, capital-account maintenance — all require CPA judgment. This workbook handles the standard case (proportional allocation, no special allocations); for anything more complex, your CPA may need to override.
