# How to Use Your House Rules Builder

## What this workbook does

Wizard that walks you through 6 sections of house rules and outputs a printable, listing-ready rules document. Avoids the "blank textarea on Airbnb" problem — most hosts list 4 generic rules ("no smoking, no pets, no parties, quiet hours after 10pm"); this product produces 25-40 specific, defensible rules organized by topic, with the framing your guests actually read.

## First-time setup (20 min one-time per property)

1. Open the workbook.
2. Walk the 6 sections of the wizard:
   - **Quiet hours + noise** (specific dB thresholds, time windows, neighbor relations)
   - **Smoking + cannabis** (where, when, fees)
   - **Pets** (if allowed — pulls from GST-003 Pet Policy if you own it)
   - **Guests + visitors** (max occupancy, day-guests, overnight visitors)
   - **Property care** (kitchen rules, trash, recycling, pool/hot tub safety, broken-item reporting)
   - **Departure** (checkout time, what to leave on/off, where to put keys)
3. For each prompt, pick from the dropdown OR type custom. ~15 inputs per section = 90 inputs total.
4. Output appears on the Rules Document tab — print-ready, listing-ready.

## Reading the outputs

- **Rules Document tab:** formatted, ready to copy into Airbnb's house-rules section + paste into your Welcome Book (GST-001)
- **Listing-Ready Excerpt:** the 8 most important rules formatted for the listing description (Airbnb's character limit)
- **Rule-Violation Log tab (bonus):** track repeat-offender guests for future booking decisions

## Why specific rules win

A vague rule ("be respectful") doesn't help when a guest plays music until 2am. A specific rule ("no amplified music outdoors after 9pm; we have a sound monitor at the property") gives your cleaner, your neighbors, and the Airbnb resolution team a clear reference point. Damage claims succeed faster when the rule was specific and the guest violated it explicitly.

## When to revise

Most rules are stable. Revise the document when:
- A specific incident exposed an unwritten rule ("the hot tub got drained" → add hot tub rules)
- Local regulation changed (new noise ordinance → update quiet hours)
- You add a feature (pool, hot tub, fire pit, EV charger → add safety rules)
