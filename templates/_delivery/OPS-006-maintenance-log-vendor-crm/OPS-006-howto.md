# How to Use Your Maintenance Log + Vendor CRM

## What this workbook does

Single source of truth for repairs, maintenance work, and recurring services across the portfolio. Combines a chronological work-order log with a vendor CRM (preferred plumber, HVAC tech, handyman, electrician) so the next emergency call is one tab away.

Solves the "guest just called, the AC is out, who do I call?" problem at 9pm on a Saturday.

## First-time setup (15 min)

1. Open tab **Property Roster** — list properties with HVAC type, water heater type, septic vs sewer, breaker panel location, water shutoff location.
2. Tab **Vendor CRM** — every vendor you use:
   - Name, business, contact, after-hours number, after-hours rate uplift
   - Specialty (plumbing, HVAC, electrical, handyman, septic, pool/spa, pest)
   - Properties served, rate, payment terms
   - Performance score (1-5 from past work)

## Every work order

1. Open the Work Order Log. Add a row:
   - Date discovered, property, issue description, urgency (emergency / urgent / scheduled)
   - Vendor assigned, vendor contact log
   - Photos (cloud links to before / after)
   - Cost (parts + labor split)
   - Resolution date, follow-up needed?
2. Mark complete when work is verified by guest or cleaner.

## Recurring services schedule

The Recurring tab pre-loads common STR maintenance cadences:
- HVAC filter swap: every 90 days
- Smoke/CO detector battery: every 12 months (better: spring + fall)
- Hot tub drain + clean: every 90 days (heavy use) / 180 (light)
- Septic pump: every 24-36 months
- Roof + gutter inspection: annual
- Pest control: quarterly

Set per-property cadences. Workbook flags upcoming services in the Calendar.

## When emergency hits

Open Vendor CRM. Filter by specialty + property. The vendor with highest performance score for THIS property gets the call first. If unreachable: the next-best vendor.

If you've never called this vendor before for this property: their rate may be different. The workbook surfaces last-rate-paid and any negotiated discount.

## Quarterly review

- Cost-per-property roll-up — flags properties with rising maintenance burden (renovation budget building)
- Vendor performance — drop or replace anyone scoring <3
- Recurring-service compliance — flag any service that slipped past its cadence
