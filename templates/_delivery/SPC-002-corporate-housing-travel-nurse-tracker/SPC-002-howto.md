# How to Use Your Corporate Housing / Travel-Nurse Tracker

## What this workbook does

For mid-term rental hosts (30-day+ stays) on Furnished Finder, Blueground, AnyPlace, or directly with corporate clients / travel nurses. Different model than nightly STR: monthly rent, longer stays, no daily turnover, recovery deposits, utility reimbursement options. Tracks: tenant pipeline, contract terms, monthly recurring revenue, occupancy gap days.

## First-time setup (15 min)

1. **Property Roster** — properties operated as MTR (mid-term rental).
2. **Channel Settings** — Furnished Finder / Blueground / AnyPlace / direct (corporate / travel nurse / relocation). Each has different fee structures.
3. **Pricing Defaults** — monthly rate, security deposit, utility-reimbursement model (included / capped / pass-through).

## Every booking inquiry

1. Open the **Pipeline tab**:
   - Tenant name, source channel, requested dates
   - Monthly rate quoted, deposit
   - Status: Inquiry / Application / Approved / Rejected / Booked / Active / Departed
2. Move tenants through the pipeline as they progress.

## Active stays

Per active tenant, the **Active Stay tab** tracks:
- Move-in date, expected move-out
- Monthly rent collected
- Utility reimbursement (if applicable)
- Damage deposit held
- Issues / repairs during stay
- Renewal probability (if applicable)

## Reading the outputs

- **Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)** — your portfolio's MRR across all active MTR tenants
- **Occupancy Gap Days** — days between one tenant's departure and the next's arrival. Target: <14 days. Above 30 = MTR pipeline is leaking.
- **Source Channel ROI** — Furnished Finder costs $99/yr per listing; if your bookings from FF generate $4K+/yr each, the math works
- **Renewal Rate** — % of tenants who extend. Higher = lower churn cost = higher net revenue.

## MTR vs STR economics

- **STR:** higher per-night ADR, higher cleaning + supplies cost, daily ops burden
- **MTR:** lower effective nightly rate, near-zero ops burden during stay, occupancy-gap risk between tenants
- **Math typically:** MTR nets 60-75% of STR gross at <30% of the operational time

## When MTR wins

- STR regulation in your market (MTR is often unregulated)
- Operator burnout
- Markets with corporate / travel-nurse demand (military bases, large hospitals, oil + gas hubs)
- Properties that are "fine" but not exceptional STR performers
