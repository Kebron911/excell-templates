# How to Use Your Pet Policy Document

## What this workbook does

Short wizard producing a print- and listing-ready pet policy. 4 sections, ~15 inputs total. Outputs a one-page document covering: pets allowed (yes/no/conditional), weight/breed/count limits, fees, designated areas, damage rules, service-animal exception, behavior expectations.

For pet-friendly properties: this is the document that protects you when a guest's "small dog" turns out to be a 90lb mastiff that ate the couch.

## First-time setup (10 min)

1. Open the workbook. Walk the 4 sections:
   - **Pets allowed?** (Yes / No / Conditional — case-by-case)
   - **Limits** (weight max, breed restrictions if any, count max, age min)
   - **Fees** (one-time pet fee, deposit, additional cleaning fee per stay)
   - **Behavior + areas** (where pets can/can't be: bedrooms, kitchen, pool deck, furniture)
2. Service-animal section is pre-filled with ADA-compliant language (no fee, no breed restriction, no weight limit) — DO NOT remove.
3. Output appears on Pet Policy Document tab.

## Reading the outputs

- **Pet Policy Document:** one-page formatted policy, ready to paste into Airbnb's house rules + print for the welcome book
- **Listing Excerpt:** short version for the property description (250 chars max for Airbnb)
- **Pet Fee Calculator:** if you charge per-pet or per-night, computes total fee at booking time

## Why this exists

"Pet-friendly" without a written policy is a refund magnet. Guests bring undeclared pets, exceed counts, or claim emotional-support exceptions. A written policy makes:
- Damage claims defensible (guest violated written rule)
- Refund disputes winnable (your policy was published)
- Cleaning fees enforceable (additional pet cleaning fee was agreed at booking)

## Service animals — a separate category

ADA + the Fair Housing Act treat service animals as not-pets. Your fees and breed/weight limits don't apply. The pre-filled section on the workbook handles this correctly — don't customize it without legal review for your jurisdiction.
