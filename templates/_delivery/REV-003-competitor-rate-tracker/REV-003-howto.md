# How to Use Your Competitor Rate Tracker

## What this workbook does

Manual weekly rate-snapshot tool. Pick 6-10 comparable Airbnb / VRBO properties in your market, snapshot their nightly rate for the next 4 weekends each week, and track how the comp set is moving. Surfaces "I'm priced 18% below comp set average" or "comps just raised for July 4 weekend — should I?" — the gut-check you'd otherwise pay PriceLabs $20/mo for.

## First-time setup (20 min one-time)

1. Open tab **Comp Selection** — pick 6-10 comparable listings:
   - Same market (within ~5 miles)
   - Same bed/bath count (±1)
   - Similar amenity tier (hot tub if you have one, etc.)
   - Mix of mature (300+ reviews) and emerging (50-200 reviews) listings
2. Tab **Settings** — set your tracking horizon (default: next 4 Fri/Sat pairs from today).

## Every Monday (10 min)

1. Open the workbook. Open each comp listing in a browser tab.
2. For each comp × each of the next 4 weekends: type the nightly rate into the snapshot grid.
3. The Comp Average + your-vs-comp delta auto-computes.
4. Read the verdict column: BELOW (raise rates), AT (hold), ABOVE (price-leadership move — defendable if your reviews + amenities support it).

## What to do with the data

- **You're 15%+ below comp average for 3 weeks running** → raise base rate or peak multiplier
- **Comps just raised for a specific weekend (July 4, Memorial Day)** → match within 24 hours
- **Comps drop a specific weekend** → hold, don't follow down (your booking-rank suffers more than your rate)
- **Comps raised but you're already 20% above comp average** → don't follow; you're price-leading

## Why manual beats automated

PriceLabs / Wheelhouse cost $20-50/mo and pull rates automatically. They're better for portfolios. For ≤3 properties, the 10-min weekly manual snapshot teaches you the comp set's behavior — knowledge you can't outsource. Use this workbook for 3 months, then decide if a paid tool is worth it.
