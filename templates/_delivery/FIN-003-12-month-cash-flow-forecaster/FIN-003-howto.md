# How to Use Your 12-Month Cash Flow Forecaster

## What this workbook does

Forward-looking 12-month cash flow forecast across your portfolio. Combines confirmed bookings + seasonal projection + recurring expenses + one-off planned expenses + quarterly tax payments. Surfaces the months where cash flow goes negative — *before* they happen — so you have time to either move expenses or pull from reserves.

Solves the "ran out of operating cash in February" failure mode that hits seasonal STR operators every year.

## First-time setup (15 min)

1. Open tab **Property Roster** — list every property with its current month's cash position.
2. Tab **Confirmed Bookings** — paste the next 90 days of bookings (Airbnb / Vrbo / direct).
3. Tab **Seasonal Projection** — month-by-month occupancy + ADR estimates for nights not yet booked. Pre-loaded with reasonable seasonal patterns by market type (beach / mountain / city).
4. Tab **Recurring Expenses** — mortgage, insurance, utilities, software, property management. Monthly rhythm.
5. Tab **One-Off Expenses** — planned renovations, furniture replacement, big maintenance. Date + amount.
6. Tab **Tax Payments** — Q1 / Q2 / Q3 / Q4 estimated tax payments (pulled from TAX-005 if you use it).

## Reading the outputs

- **12-Month Forecast tab:** monthly cash flow (revenue − expenses) per property + portfolio
- **Negative-Month Flag:** any month projected negative shows a red bar — that's a planning trigger
- **Cumulative Cash Position:** running total starting from current cash. If cumulative goes negative anywhere in the next 12 months, you have a runway problem to address now

## Monthly check

Open the Forecast tab on the 1st of every month. Refresh:
1. Confirmed Bookings tab — paste new bookings, remove past ones
2. Update any expenses that got rescheduled
3. Re-read the 12-month forecast. Negative-month flags should disappear or get smaller as bookings firm up
