# How to Use Your Commission / Split Calculator

## What this workbook does

For co-host arrangements: you manage someone else's STR for a 15-25% revenue share, OR a flat fee per booking, OR a hybrid. This workbook computes per-month commission owed, tracks payments to the property owner (or to the co-host depending on direction), and produces 1099-NEC-ready year-end totals.

## First-time setup (15 min)

1. **Property Roster** — every property you co-host or own under co-host arrangement.
2. **Per-Property Agreement** — commission model:
   - **% of revenue** (typical 15-25%)
   - **Flat per booking** (e.g., $50/booking)
   - **Hybrid** (e.g., $50/booking + 10%)
3. **Owner / Co-Host Roster** — contact info + W-9 for 1099-NEC purposes.

## Every month

1. Update the Booking Log with that month's bookings + revenue.
2. The Commission Calc auto-applies the per-property model to each booking.
3. Per-Owner Payment Schedule shows what's owed to whom.
4. Pay (Venmo / ACH / check). Mark Paid in the workbook.

## At year-end

The 1099-NEC Summary tab pulls every owner / co-host who received ≥$600 in the calendar year. Outputs:
- Recipient name + EIN/SSN + address (must have W-9 on file)
- Total paid in calendar year
- W-9 status flag

This data feeds directly into TAX-003 1099-NEC Tracker for Form 1099-NEC filing.

## Two directions of payment

The workbook handles both:
- **You manage someone's property → you collect revenue, pay owner their share, keep your commission.** Owner gets the 1099-NEC (you paid them).
- **Someone manages your property → owner collects revenue, pays you commission.** You get the 1099-NEC (they paid you).

The Direction setting per property routes the math correctly.

## Common surprises the workbook catches

- "I'm paying my co-host but they don't have a W-9 on file" — fix before December 15
- "The hybrid commission model is more profitable than I thought" — useful for renegotiations
- "One owner relationship has thin margins after I net out my time" — feeds PAM-004 Multi-Owner Consolidated
