# How to Use Your Insurance Claim Log

## What this workbook does

Tracks insurance claims filed against your underlying STR insurance policy (separate from Airbnb AirCover, which is OPS-002). Major events: storm damage, fire, theft, plumbing failure, liability claim. Records claim lifecycle from FNOL (First Notice Of Loss) through settlement, recovery $ vs deductible, and lessons-learned.

## First-time setup (10 min)

1. **Property Roster** — list properties + each property's policy info (carrier, policy #, deductible, coverage limits).
2. **Settings** — your typical claim-filing thresholds (most carriers don't want claims under $5K; you absorb those out-of-pocket to protect future premiums).

## Every claim

1. **FNOL stage** — incident happens, log:
   - Date of loss, property, type (storm / fire / theft / plumbing / liability / other)
   - Description, photos cloud links
   - First call to carrier date + claim #
2. **Adjuster stage** — carrier sends an adjuster:
   - Adjuster name + contact
   - Inspection date
   - Initial estimate
3. **Settlement stage**:
   - Settlement date + amount
   - Deductible applied
   - Net recovery (settlement − deductible)
4. **Lessons-learned column** — what could've prevented this? What policy gap was exposed?

## Reading the outputs

- **Per-Claim Lifecycle View:** every claim's status (FNOL / Investigating / Approved / Denied / Settled)
- **Days-to-Settlement KPI:** rolling average. >60 days = pressure carrier or escalate to state insurance commissioner.
- **Recovery Rate:** total settled $ ÷ total claimed $. <60% = your evidence is weak; reference photos checklist.
- **Per-Property Claim History:** carriers raise rates after 2+ claims in 3 years — this surfaces which property is "hot" before renewal.

## Quarterly review

- Claims pending >60 days: escalate
- Properties with 2+ claims in 3 years: flag for OPS-008 (Insurance Policy Tracker) review at next renewal — may need to switch carriers
- Lessons-learned: review for OPS-006 (Maintenance Log) preventive cadence updates

## Disclaimer

Filing claims under threshold (e.g., $3K damage with $2.5K deductible) often costs more in future premium hikes than the settlement returns. The workbook helps you decide WHETHER to file — not just how to track.
