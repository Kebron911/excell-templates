# How to Use Your Multi-Owner Consolidated Reporting

## What this workbook does

For Pro Pam managing multiple property owners across multiple properties. Aggregates per-owner reporting (which is PAM-001's job) up one level: Pam's total managed revenue, total commission earned, time allocated per owner, profitability per owner relationship.

The workbook that tells you which owner relationships are worth keeping vs firing.

## First-time setup (30 min)

1. **Owner Roster** — every owner with their properties + commission model + relationship start date.
2. **Time Allocation Tab** — log hours per owner per month (admin, comms, owner-specific issues, distributions).
3. **Per-Owner Revenue + Commission** — pulled from PAM-001 if you have it, or input directly.

## Every quarter

1. Update Time Allocation (rough estimates from your calendar are fine).
2. Open the Per-Owner Profitability tab:
   - Total commission earned per owner this quarter
   - Total hours allocated per owner
   - $/hour effective rate per owner relationship
3. Sort by $/hour. The bottom 20% = candidates for renegotiation or firing.

## Reading the outputs

- **Total Managed Revenue** — your portfolio's gross across all owners
- **Total Commission Earned** — your top-line revenue
- **Per-Owner $/Hour** — effective hourly rate per owner relationship. Below your minimum acceptable rate? Renegotiate or fire.
- **Owner-Relationship Ranking** — every owner sorted by profitability
- **Fire vs Keep Recommendations** — bottom-quartile owners flagged

## Common patterns

- **One owner takes 30% of your time but contributes 8% of revenue** → renegotiate or fire
- **One owner generates 40% of revenue with 12% of time** → your A-list; protect this relationship
- **Mid-tier owner with declining $/hour** → property is underperforming OR relationship is heating up; investigate before firing

## When to fire an owner

Hardest part of the job. Use the workbook's data to make it businesslike:
- Below-target $/hour for 3+ consecutive quarters
- Owner-side communication burden disproportionate to their property's size
- Relationship becoming adversarial (frequent disputes over expenses, distributions)

Firing a bad owner relationship lets you take on a better one — most PMs are revenue-capacity-constrained, not demand-constrained.
