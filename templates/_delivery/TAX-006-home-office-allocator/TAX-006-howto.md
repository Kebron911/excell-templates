# How to Use Your Home Office Deduction Allocator

**The STR Ledger · thestrledger.com**

A wizard that walks you through both home-office methods (Simplified $5/sqft vs Actual-Expense), checks the IRS exclusivity tests, and outputs a print-ready Form 8829 mapping.

**Bonus:** a qualified home office is what makes residence-to-STR mileage **deductible business travel** instead of non-deductible commuting. The two work together.

## Wizard flow (8 tabs + Settings)

Two files:
- **DEMO** — Sarah's example: 2,400 sqft home, 110 sqft office, Actual method, ~$985 deduction.
- **BLANK** — your numbers.

Open **BLANK** and walk the tabs.

## Step 1 — Eligibility (tab 1)

5 yes/no questions. The two non-negotiable ones:
- Office used **regularly**? (Y/N)
- Office used **exclusively** for business? (Y/N)

Both must be Yes. If either is No, the IRS does not allow the deduction — period.

## Step 2 — Space Allocation (tab 2)

- Total home sqft
- Office sqft (regular + exclusive only)

Workbook computes business-use % = office_sqft / home_sqft.

## Step 3 — Method Election (tab 3)

Pick one:
- **Simplified** — MIN(office_sqft, 300) × $5. Max $1,500. No depreciation. No recapture.
- **Actual** — direct expenses + (indirect × biz %). Includes depreciation. Triggers §1250 recapture if you sell the home.

The tab shows both side-by-side and flags the higher one.

**If you plan to sell within 5 years**, Simplified is often better even when smaller — no recapture.

## Step 4 — Actual Expenses (tab 4) — only if you elected Actual

Direct (only-the-office cost) and indirect (whole-house, prorated by biz %): utilities, insurance, repairs, real estate taxes, mortgage interest, depreciation.

## Step 5 — STR Routing (tab 5)

Schedule E (passive) → deduction lands on Line 18a. Schedule C (active/substantial-services) → Line 30. Pick which form you file.

## Step 6 — Form 8829 Map (tab 6)

Auto-built mirror of Form 8829 lines 1–36. **Print this** and hand to CPA.

## Step 7 — Launch (tab 7)

Deduction summary card + print packet.

## Year-to-year flexibility

You can switch methods every year. No election lock.
