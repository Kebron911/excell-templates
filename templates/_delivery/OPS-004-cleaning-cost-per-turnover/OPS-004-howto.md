# How to Use Your Cleaning Cost per Turnover Tracker

## What this workbook does

Logs every turnover cleaning event with cost, hours, cleaner, and supplies used. Rolls up to *true cost per turnover* and *cleaning $ per night* per property and across the portfolio. Surfaces outlier cleaners and properties where the cleaning cost has drifted upward.

The output feeds REV-001 Cleaning Fee Optimizer — you can't price the cleaning fee correctly without knowing what cleanings actually cost.

## First-time setup (10 min)

1. Open tab **Property Roster** — list every property with target cleaning cost (your benchmark).
2. Tab **Cleaner Directory** — every cleaner / service you use, hourly rate or flat fee, contact info.
3. Tab **Settings** — your target $ per night (typically $8-15 depending on market).

## Every turnover

1. Open the Cleaning Log. Log a row at the end of each turn:
   - Date, property, cleaner, hours, hourly rate (or flat fee), supplies cost (separate line), total
2. Optional: notes on issues (extra deep-clean needed, damage discovered, late finish).

## Reading the outputs

- **Per-Property Roll-up:** $ per turn, hours per turn, $ per night (vs. nights between this turn and the previous one)
- **Per-Cleaner Roll-up:** average $ per turn, drift from your target rate, on-time vs late completion
- **Outlier Flag:** any cleaning >25% over your target benchmark gets flagged. Investigate the row.

## Quarterly review

Open the Quarterly tab. Look for:
- A property whose $ per night is creeping up (cleaning cost outpacing your nightly rate — review whether the cleaning fee needs a bump)
- A cleaner whose rate has drifted +10%+ without a conversation (renegotiate or replace)
- A property with high turn-time (long average hours) — may need a checklist refresh (OPS-001) or a cleaner swap

## Feeds REV-001

Once you have 90+ days of data, the workbook hands clean inputs (cleaning $ per turn, average LOS, ADR) to REV-001 Cleaning Fee Optimizer. The optimizer recommends whether to charge separately or bundle the fee.
