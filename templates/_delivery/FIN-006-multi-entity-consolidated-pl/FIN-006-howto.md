# How to Use Your Multi-Entity Consolidated P&L

## What this workbook does

For Sarah/Pam-tier hosts who hold each property in its own LLC. Aggregates per-LLC income statements into a holding-company-level consolidated P&L. Resolves "I have 5 LLCs and 5 separate P&Ls — what's my actual portfolio income?"

Differs from TAX-011 (multi-property single-entity) by adding entity-level allocation, intercompany eliminations, and member-distribution tracking.

## First-time setup (45 min)

1. **Entity Roster:** every LLC with formation state, EIN, members, ownership %, properties owned.
2. **Per-LLC P&L** (one tab per LLC): paste from each entity's bookkeeping or TAX-002 output.
3. **Intercompany Transactions:** loans between LLCs, management fees one LLC charges another, shared expenses allocated.
4. **Member-Distribution Schedule:** when each LLC distributes cash to members.

## Reading the outputs

- **Consolidated P&L tab:** holding-company view across all LLCs after eliminations
- **Per-LLC Summary:** revenue, expenses, NOI, distributions per entity
- **Member-View P&L:** each member's allocated share across all entities they own
- **Intercompany Eliminations Log:** what got removed to avoid double-counting

## At year-end

1. Confirm every LLC's P&L is current.
2. Reconcile intercompany transactions (one LLC's expense = another LLC's revenue, must net to zero).
3. Print Consolidated P&L for your overall picture.
4. Print per-LLC P&Ls for each entity's tax filing.
5. Hand to CPA for K-1 prep + entity returns.

## When you need this vs TAX-011

- **TAX-011:** multiple properties, single entity (sole prop or single LLC)
- **FIN-006:** multiple LLCs, possibly different members per LLC

If you don't know which one you need: ask your CPA whether you file one Schedule E or multiple. One = TAX-011. Multiple = FIN-006.
