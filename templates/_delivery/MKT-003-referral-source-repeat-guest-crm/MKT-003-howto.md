# How to Use Your Referral + Repeat Guest CRM

## What this workbook does

Tracks where each guest came from (Airbnb / VRBO / direct / repeat / referral) and identifies *repeat-stay* and *referral-friendly* guests. Attaches them to a lightweight CRM so you can run a year-end "thanks + come back" outreach to the top-15 highest-LTV guests — pushing direct bookings and skipping the 15% Airbnb host fee.

## First-time setup (15 min)

1. Open tab **Property Roster** — list properties.
2. Tab **Guest Log** — paste your booking history (last 24 months ideally). One row per booking: guest name, email if you have it, source channel, dates, gross revenue, property.
3. Tab **Settings** — your top-LTV outreach threshold (default: top 15 guests by total revenue).

## Every booking

1. Add a row to the Guest Log:
   - Date, property, guest name, email (capture during stay if possible — direct booking is easier)
   - Source: Airbnb / VRBO / Direct / Repeat / Referral
   - Gross revenue
   - Notes (referred-by who? met at what?)
2. The workbook auto-flags repeat guests (same name/email seen 2+ times) and computes per-guest LTV.

## Reading the outputs

- **Source Distribution:** % of bookings + revenue by channel. Direct % is your best operational metric — higher = lower channel fees.
- **Repeat-Guest List:** every guest who's stayed 2+ times. Your re-marketing audience.
- **Top-15 LTV List:** highest total-revenue guests. Year-end outreach candidates.
- **Referral Tree:** which past guests sent you new bookings. These are your evangelists.

## Year-end outreach (December)

Use the pre-loaded outreach template:

> "Hey [name] — checking in to thank you for staying with us in [year]. We're starting to take 2027 bookings; if you're planning a return trip, book direct at [link] and use code REPEAT for 10% off."

Send to top-15 LTV. Expected response: 25-40% rebook within 60 days. Direct bookings save you 15% in channel fees, which on a $2,500 stay is $375 — well worth a 10% discount you'd give anyway.

## Quarterly review

- Source distribution shifting toward Direct = your strategy is working
- Source distribution shifting toward Airbnb only = your direct-booking funnel needs investment
