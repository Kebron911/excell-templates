# How to Use Your Rehab Budget + ROI Projection

## What this workbook does

Detailed rehab budget for a property pre-launch with embedded ROI projection. Per line item: estimate, actual, variance, contractor, payment status. Computes total rehab spend, contingency burn, and a "$ rehab → $ revenue lift" ROI estimate so you know whether the renovation actually pays for itself.

Use this BEFORE the wholesale demo starts. Most rehabs go 30-50% over budget; line-item discipline is the only thing that prevents it.

## First-time setup (15 min)

1. Open tab **Property Snapshot** — name, address, target launch date, total rehab budget, contingency %.
2. Tab **Line Items** — every planned rehab task: paint, flooring, kitchen, bath, exterior, electrical, plumbing, HVAC. Pre-loaded with 50+ common line items.
3. Tab **Contractors** — directory of contractors bidding the work, their categories, payment terms.
4. Tab **ROI Projection** — for each rehab category, an estimated revenue-lift (e.g., kitchen reno → +$15/night ADR). Workbook computes payback period.

## During rehab

- Update the Actual column on the Line Items tab as bills come in.
- The Variance column shows over/under per line. Anything >25% over flags red.
- Contingency Burn-Down tab shows how much of your buffer you've used. Below 50% remaining is a warning.

## At launch

1. Print the Final Cost Summary tab. Save in your deal folder as the basis-step-up record (you'll need this for depreciation in TAX-013).
2. Run the ROI Projection 6 months post-launch with actual revenue. The actual-vs-projected delta tunes future rehabs.
