# How to Use Your Holiday + Event Pricing Calendar

## What this workbook does

Pre-built calendar of high-rate dates: federal holidays, school breaks (regional), local events you add (concerts, marathons, festivals, college games). Per-event lift % applied to your base rate. Surfaces the next 12 events sorted by date so you can verify rates are bumped before peak.

Solves "I forgot July 4 was a high-rate weekend until the 3rd."

## First-time setup (15 min)

1. Open tab **Property Roster** — list properties + each one's market.
2. Tab **Federal Holidays** — pre-loaded with all federal holidays + suggested lift % (e.g., July 4 = +50%, Memorial Day = +35%, Labor Day = +35%, Christmas/NYE = +60%).
3. Tab **School Breaks** — regional school calendar (sets school break by region — Northeast, Southeast, etc.). Pre-loaded with typical break weeks.
4. Tab **Local Events** — add concerts, marathons, festivals, college games, conferences, sporting events specific to your market.

## Reading the outputs

- **Next 12 Events** (sorted by date): event name, dates, suggested lift %, current rate vs target rate, status (RATE BUMPED / NOT YET / N/A)
- **Calendar View:** rolling 12 months with all events overlaid
- **Pricing CSV Export:** dates × rates ready to paste into Airbnb / VRBO custom-pricing OR feed into REV-002 Dynamic Pricing Calculator

## Every Monday (5 min)

1. Open Next 12 Events.
2. Anything in NOT YET status with a date <30 days away → bump rates today.
3. New local event spotted (concert, festival)? Add to Local Events tab.

## Why this exists separately from REV-002

REV-002 Dynamic Pricing Calculator handles base rates + season multipliers + day-of-week. Events are spiky and unpredictable — they need a separate workflow. Most hosts forget local events specifically because they don't show up in any data feed; you have to know your market.

## Common events to add per market

- **Beach markets:** July 4, Memorial Day, Labor Day, regional school breaks, fishing tournaments
- **Mountain/ski markets:** Christmas/NYE, MLK weekend, Presidents Day, spring break weeks
- **Urban markets:** local festivals, conventions, sports playoffs, university graduation, concerts
- **Wine country:** harvest weekends, restaurant week, local fests
