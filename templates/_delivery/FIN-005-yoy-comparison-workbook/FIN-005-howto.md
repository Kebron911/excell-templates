# How to Use Your Year-Over-Year P&L Comparison

## What this workbook does

Side-by-side prior-year vs. current-year P&L per property. Surfaces revenue change %, expense category drift, occupancy trend, and the top-3 favorable + top-3 unfavorable movers. The workbook a host opens at year-end to answer: "did 2026 actually go better than 2025, or did it just feel that way?"

## First-time setup (10 min)

1. Open tab **Property Roster** — list properties.
2. Tab **Prior Year** — paste prior-year P&L per property. Pull from TAX-002 P&L Tracker year-end output.
3. Tab **Current Year** — paste current YTD or full-year P&L per property.

## Reading the outputs

- **Per-Property YoY:** revenue, total expenses, NOI, occupancy — current vs prior, $ delta, % delta
- **Top-3 Favorable Movers:** the three categories that improved most ($ saved or revenue gained)
- **Top-3 Unfavorable Movers:** the three categories that worsened most. These are your action items for next year.
- **Portfolio Summary:** aggregate YoY delta across all properties

## Year-end review (annual, 30 min)

1. Run the workbook in early January with full prior-year + current-year data.
2. Open the Top-3 Movers tab. The unfavorable list = next year's optimization queue.
3. Common patterns:
   - **Revenue up + occupancy down** = pricing power gained, market opportunity to push further
   - **Revenue flat + occupancy up** = comp set raising rates faster than you (run REV-003)
   - **Cleaning expense ↑ 20%+** = renegotiate or replace cleaner (run OPS-004)
   - **Insurance ↑** = shop carriers (run OPS-008 if you have it)

## When YoY doesn't tell the truth

If you acquired a property mid-year, prior year is partial — annualize before comparing. The workbook flags partial-year properties so you don't draw bad conclusions.
