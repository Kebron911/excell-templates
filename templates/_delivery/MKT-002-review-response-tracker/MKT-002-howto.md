# How to Use Your Review Response Tracker

## What this workbook does

Logs every guest review (rating, content, sentiment), tracks whether you responded, captures lessons-learned, and provides 8 copy-paste reply templates organized by review tone. Cuts review-response time from 15 min to 3 min and prevents the "I forgot to reply to that 3-star" failure mode that hurts ranking.

## First-time setup (5 min)

1. Open tab **Property Roster** — list properties.
2. Tab **Settings** — review-response SLA target (Airbnb best practice: ≤24 hours after review posts).
3. Tab **Templates** — pre-loaded with 8 reply templates:
   - 5-star (warm thank, no specifics)
   - 5-star (specific praise — repeat the guest's compliment)
   - 4-star (gentle thank + acknowledge any minor issue)
   - 3-star (apology + improvement commitment, no excuses)
   - 1-2-star (firm but professional, factual correction if needed)
   - First-time guest 5-star (welcome to the community language)
   - Repeat guest (acknowledge return, thank loyalty)
   - Long-stay guest (acknowledge length, mention specific guest behavior)

## Every review

1. Airbnb / Vrbo notification arrives. Open the workbook.
2. Log the review on the **Review Log** tab: date, property, guest, rating, brief content, sentiment.
3. Pick the matching template from the Templates tab. Customize 1-2 lines for the specific review.
4. Paste into Airbnb / Vrbo. Mark Responded = Y.
5. Capture any lesson-learned in the Lessons column (e.g., "guest mentioned no body wash — add to OPS-005 par list").

## Reading the outputs

- **Average Response Time:** rolling 30-day. Target ≤24h.
- **Response Rate:** % of reviews you've responded to. Target 100%.
- **Sentiment Trend:** % positive / neutral / negative per quarter
- **Lessons Aggregated:** by frequency. The same complaint appearing 3+ times = action item

## The 1-2 star playbook

Bad reviews happen. The response is your defense for the next 100 readers:
1. Don't apologize for things that aren't your fault
2. Factually correct anything misleading
3. Acknowledge anything legitimate
4. Stay professional — every future booker reads this
5. Use the template; don't write from scratch in the heat of the moment
