# How to Use Your STR vs LTR Yield Comparison

## What this workbook does

For a single property, compares running it as a short-term rental (Airbnb model) vs a long-term rental (12-month lease). Computes all-in yield each way. STR has higher revenue but higher costs (cleaning, supplies, mgmt, vacancy, regulation risk). LTR has lower revenue but lower costs and far less time.

The workbook a host runs when their city threatens an STR ban — or when burnout has them re-thinking the model.

## First-time setup (10 min)

1. **STR Side:** ADR, occupancy, cleaning fee, OTA fees, supplies, cleaning cost, software, mgmt fees, vacancy %.
2. **LTR Side:** market monthly rent, vacancy %, tenant turnover cost, repairs reserve, no cleaning/supplies/OTA.
3. **Common:** mortgage, property tax, insurance, HOA (same in both).

## Reading the outputs

- **All-in Yield STR:** annual STR net income ÷ total cash invested
- **All-in Yield LTR:** annual LTR net income ÷ total cash invested
- **Hours-of-Work:** STR typically 3-8 hrs/week, LTR 0-1 hr/week
- **Regulation-Risk Sensitivity:** what STR yield drops to if your jurisdiction caps nights or bans entirely
- **Recommended Path:** which model wins on dollar yield AND hours

## When LTR wins

- Strict STR regulations (occupancy caps, registration delays, outright bans)
- Markets where STR comp set is saturated
- Hosts hitting burnout — LTR's hands-off ROI matters more than peak revenue
- Properties in markets where LTR rents are surprisingly strong (university towns)
