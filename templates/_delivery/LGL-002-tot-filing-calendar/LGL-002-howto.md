# How to Use Your TOT Filing Calendar

## What this workbook does

Aggregates monthly STR revenue per property/jurisdiction, computes Transient Occupancy Tax (TOT, sometimes called lodging tax, hotel tax, occupancy tax) due, and flags filing deadlines. Many jurisdictions require monthly filing on rolling deadlines; missing one = penalty + listing risk.

Also surfaces the "Airbnb-remitted vs you-remit" split. Many jurisdictions have Airbnb collecting and remitting on your behalf — but NOT all of them. Some only Airbnb (not Vrbo). Some only state-level (not city-level). The workbook tracks the gap so you know what's still your responsibility.

## First-time setup (20 min)

1. Open tab **Property Roster** — every property with its full TOT-applicable jurisdictions: state, county, city, sometimes special districts (resort, tourism, BID).
2. Tab **Tax Rate Register** — for each jurisdiction:
   - Tax rate (e.g., 8% county + 2% city = 10% total)
   - Filing cadence (monthly is most common; quarterly or annual in some)
   - Due-by-day-of-month after period close (e.g., "by the 20th of the following month")
   - Airbnb-remitted? Y/N (check Airbnb's Collect-and-Remit list for your jurisdiction)
   - Vrbo-remitted? Y/N
   - Direct-booking responsibility (always you)
3. Tab **Settings** — your filing-method default per jurisdiction (online portal, paper form, etc.).

## Every month

1. Open the Filing Calendar tab on the 1st.
2. For each property × jurisdiction × due date in the next 30 days:
   - Compute taxable revenue (gross rents — Airbnb-remitted portion if applicable)
   - Apply tax rate
   - Compute amount due
   - File via the jurisdiction's portal
3. Mark Filed = Y. Save the confirmation number.

## Airbnb collect-and-remit — important

Many hosts assume "Airbnb handles all my lodging tax." In reality:
- Airbnb collects-and-remits in some jurisdictions, not all
- Vrbo may or may not, often differently than Airbnb
- Direct bookings (your own website, email referrals) — never collected by anyone but you
- Multi-jurisdictional rentals (state + city + special district) — Airbnb may handle state but not city

The workbook's Airbnb-remitted flag per jurisdiction lets you net out what's already handled vs what's still on you.

## Audit defense

If audited, the jurisdiction wants:
- Gross rents per period (matches your bookkeeping)
- Tax rate applied
- Tax due vs tax remitted (yours and Airbnb's combined)
- Confirmation numbers / receipts for each filing

The Filing History tab is your audit-defense record. Don't delete old rows — the lookback period for audit is typically 3-7 years depending on jurisdiction.
