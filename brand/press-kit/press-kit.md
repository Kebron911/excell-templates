## One-line description

The STR Ledger is a financial-operations toolkit for short-term-rental hosts — Excel templates, PDF guides, and AI tools that turn Airbnb hosting into a real, well-run business.

## 90-second description

Most Airbnb and VRBO hosts treat their property like a side project — until they realize they're running a small business with a six-figure top line, a 20-vendor payroll, and an annual six-week tax marathon. The STR Ledger exists for that moment.

It started with a chart of accounts and a Schedule E workbook that the founder built for his own four properties. Hosts started asking for copies. Then for adjacent tools — mileage logs, cleaner SOPs, market scorecards, lodging-tax trackers. Today the catalog is 65 production-grade Excel templates plus a growing library of operating playbooks (STR Manuals), seven free calculators (STR Host Tools), guest-communication generators (STR Guests Tools), acquisition tools (STR Buyers Tools), and a compliance-alerts service tracking short-term-rental regulations across all 50 US states (STR Laws).

It is, fundamentally, "what your CPA wishes you came in with."

## Founder bio (long — for written features)

Daniel Harrison started hosting on Airbnb in 2021 with one unit. By his second year he had four — and a tax return that took him 60 hours to prepare because nothing he owned was built for short-term rentals. His CPA was a generalist who'd handled exactly one Airbnb before. His bookkeeping software was QuickBooks Online with a "rental property" template designed for long-term landlords. His mileage was on a sticky note.

The first version of The STR Ledger was a 12-tab Excel workbook he built to file his own 2022 return. It mapped every Airbnb-specific cost (lodging tax, OTA fees, welcome gifts, cleaner passthroughs) to its correct IRS Schedule E line, and auto-generated the full statement at year end. He found $11,000 in deductions his previous CPA had missed.

He shared it with two hosts. They shared it with five more. By mid-2023 he was answering the same five questions over and over in STR Facebook groups, so he started writing them up. By 2024 the writing had become a 17-chapter book on the operator-side of running an Airbnb portfolio. By 2025 the templates and the writing had become a product catalog and a website.

The STR Ledger now serves Airbnb hosts and VRBO operators across all 50 US states. Daniel works on it from his kitchen counter and an STR he sometimes blocks for "research."

He is opinionated about three things: that hosts under-claim depreciation by an average of $4,000 a year, that lodging tax is the most-misclassified line on a small operator's books, and that the best STR is one you forgot you own — because the systems run it without you.

## Story angles (for journalists / podcast bookers)

1. **"The $11K Their CPA Missed."** First-person account of switching CPAs and uncovering a decade of missed depreciation. Useful for personal-finance reporters.

2. **"Why 'Airbnb host' should be on the IRS small-business radar."** STR hosts are operating six-figure businesses but get treated like passive landlords. The tax code's mismatch is widening.

3. **"What 50 states think about your hot tub."** Daniel maintains a regulation database for all 50 US states tracking permit costs, occupancy caps, lodging tax rates, and ban status. Comparative angle on which states are friendliest/hostile to STRs.

4. **"The unsexy economics of a 4-property STR portfolio."** Real numbers (anonymized): revenue, cleaning costs, software stack, what actually moves margin. Pushback to YouTube-influencer-math.

5. **"How a one-person operator built a 65-product templates business in 14 months."** Solo-founder build journey. Tooling, decisions, what failed, what compounded.

6. **"AI in the STR workflow — what actually works in 2026."** Listing copy, review responses, welcome books, guest messages. Real before/after; cost economics.
