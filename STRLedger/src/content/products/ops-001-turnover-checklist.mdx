## The 30-second pitch

You can't tell which cleaner is bad until two months in. By then you have a one-star review and a stained mattress.

This template flips the loop: every turnover has a checklist the cleaner uses, and a scorecard you fill in after. 0–3 per category. Two turnovers and you can see whether the cleaner with the lower rate is actually the cheaper cleaner.

## Who it's for

- Single-property hosts cycling through cleaners trying to find a keeper
- Operators with 3+ properties running multiple cleaners across them
- Anyone tired of "the cleaner's been doing this for ten years" being treated as evidence

## Math that earns the price

One avoided one-star review from a missed-cleaning issue protects ~$400 in next-30-day revenue (rank drop + replacement-booking effort). Workbook costs $12. Returns its cost the first time the scorecard catches a slipping cleaner.
