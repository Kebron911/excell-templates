## How Wisconsin lodging tax works

Wisconsin stacks a **5% state sales tax** on top of **county sales tax** (0.5% in most counties) and a **municipal room tax** of up to 8% added by cities and tourism zones. Combined effective rates land between **5.5% and 13.5%**. Top STR markets:

- **Door County** (Sister Bay, Egg Harbor, Fish Creek, Ephraim, Baileys Harbor) — 5% state + 0.5% county + 5.5% room tax = **~11% effective**. The state's premier vacation-rental market; intensely seasonal (Memorial Day through October).
- **Wisconsin Dells (Sauk + Columbia Counties)** — 5% state + 0.5% county + 8% room tax + 1.25% Premier Resort Area Tax (PRAT) = **~14.75%**. Highest STR tax rate in Wisconsin.
- **Madison (Dane County)** — 5% state + 0.5% county + 9% room tax = **~14.5%**. University and capitol traffic; Badger football weekends.
- **Milwaukee** — 5% state + 0.5% county + 9% room tax = **~14.5%**.
- **Lake Geneva (Walworth County)** — 5% state + 0.5% county + 5–6% room tax = **~11%**. Chicago-overflow weekend market.

Stays of **30 consecutive nights or longer** are exempt from state sales tax and from municipal room tax.

## Platform collection

Airbnb and Vrbo both collect and remit the **5% state sales tax** and **county sales tax** in Wisconsin under marketplace facilitator rules. **Municipal room tax collection varies widely** — Madison, Milwaukee, and Wisconsin Dells are typically covered by Airbnb; Door County coverage is partial; many smaller tourism zones still require host self-filing.

This is the biggest compliance trap in Wisconsin: hosts assume the room tax is covered when it isn't.

## What this means in practice

- **State and county sales tax** — handled by both platforms.
- **Municipal room tax** — check your listing's tax disclosure carefully. If your tourism zone or city isn't on the platform's collection list, register with your municipality and file monthly. Door County hosts especially: verify your specific town (Sister Bay vs Egg Harbor vs Fish Creek) — they handle room tax independently.
- **30+ night stays** — exempt from sales tax and room tax.
- **Door County seasonal closure** — if you only operate Memorial Day to October, your room-tax permit stays active unless you formally close it for the season.
