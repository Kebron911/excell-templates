## How Illinois lodging tax works

Illinois charges a **6% state Hotel Operators' Occupation Tax (HOOT)** on stays under 30 nights, plus **local hotel taxes** up to roughly 7.5% added by counties, municipalities, and special districts. Combined effective rates land in the **11.5% to 17.5%** range depending on location. Top STR markets:

- **Chicago** — 6% state + 4.5% city + 1% MPEA + 1% Illinois Sports Facilities + a **4% Vacation Rental and Shared Housing Surcharge** layered on top for STRs specifically = effective **~17.4%**. Chicago STRs carry the country's highest single-city stack.
- **Galena** — 6% state + 5% city = **11%**. Major weekend-getaway market from Chicago/Milwaukee.
- **Lake Geneva Region** (Illinois side near the WI border) — 6% state + ~5% local = **11%**.
- **Springfield, Champaign-Urbana, Peoria** — 6% state + 5–7% local = **11–13%**.

Stays of **30 consecutive nights or longer** are exempt from the state HOOT.

## Chicago's Vacation Rental Surcharge

Chicago is special: in addition to the standard hotel tax stack, STR listings carry a dedicated **4% Vacation Rental Surcharge** that funds homelessness services. This applies on top of the regular hotel taxes. Airbnb and Vrbo collect this from guests automatically for Chicago listings — you don't file it, but it shows up on the guest invoice.

## Platform collection

Airbnb and Vrbo both collect and remit the **6% state HOOT** in Illinois under marketplace facilitator rules. For **Chicago**, both platforms also collect the full city stack (4.5% hotel, 1% MPEA, 1% sports facilities, 4% vacation rental surcharge). Outside Chicago, **most local hotel taxes are NOT collected by the platforms** — that falls on the host.

## What this means in practice

- **Chicago hosts** — both state and city tax stack are handled by the platform. But you must hold a current City of Chicago Shared Housing License before listing. Compliance enforcement is aggressive.
- **Outside Chicago hosts** — state HOOT handled, but you'll likely need to register with your municipality for the local hotel tax and file monthly or quarterly.
- **Galena hosts** — register with the City of Galena for the 5% local lodging tax.
- **30+ night stays** — exempt from state HOOT.
