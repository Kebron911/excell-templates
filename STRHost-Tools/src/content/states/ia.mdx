## How Iowa lodging tax works

Iowa charges a **5% state hotel and motel tax** on lodging stays under 31 nights, plus a **local hotel/motel tax of up to 7%** at the city level. Combined effective rates run **5% to 12%** depending on jurisdiction. Top STR markets:

- **Des Moines** — 5% state + 7% city = **12% effective**.
- **Iowa City** — 5% state + 7% city = **12%**. University of Iowa game-day market is strong.
- **Cedar Rapids** — 5% state + 7% city = **12%**.
- **Lake Okoboji / Arnolds Park** — 5% state + 7% city = **12%** for the major resort towns; small surrounding townships drop to 5%-only.
- **Dubuque** — 5% state + 7% city = **12%**.

Stays of **31 consecutive nights or longer** are exempt from both the state and local hotel/motel tax.

## Platform collection

Airbnb and Vrbo both collect and remit the **5% state hotel/motel tax** and the **local hotel/motel tax** in cities that have signed marketplace facilitator agreements with the platforms. Iowa expanded marketplace coverage in 2019, and most major Iowa cities (Des Moines, Iowa City, Cedar Rapids, Dubuque, Davenport) are now in scope.

That said, smaller resort communities and townships often still require **host self-filing** for the local share. Check your listing's tax disclosure for what's collected versus what falls to you.

## What this means in practice

- **State tax** — handled by Airbnb and Vrbo. No state filings required.
- **Local tax in major cities** — also handled. No filing required.
- **Local tax in smaller towns / townships** — register with the Iowa Department of Revenue under a Local Option Hotel/Motel Tax permit and file quarterly.
- **31+ night stays** — exempt; ensure the booking is documented as 31+ from the start.
- **Lake Okoboji seasonal hosts** — verify your specific township; Dickinson County has multiple taxing jurisdictions with different rates.

## How to use the calculator above

1. Enter your booking subtotal (nightly × nights + cleaning fee, before tax).
2. Enter the local add-on rate that applies to your address. Des Moines, Iowa City, Cedar Rapids, and Okoboji-area cities are typically 7%; unincorporated areas may be 0%.
