## How Delaware lodging tax works

Delaware has **no state sales tax**, but it does levy an **8% state lodging tax** on short-term stays under 120 days. On top of that, beach towns are authorized to add up to a **3% local accommodations tax**, so the typical effective rate in the southern beach corridor is **11%**. Top STR markets:

- **Rehoboth Beach** — 8% state + 3% city = **11% effective**. The state's premier summer market; weekend rates spike from Memorial Day through Labor Day.
- **Dewey Beach** — 8% state + 3% town = **11%**. Higher-density rental zone, party-town demographic.
- **Bethany Beach** — 8% state + 3% town = **11%**. Family-friendly, slightly quieter than Dewey.
- **Lewes** — 8% state + 3% town = **11%**.
- **Wilmington and inland counties** — generally **8% only** (no local add-on).

Delaware's exemption threshold is unusual: stays of **120 consecutive nights or longer** are exempt from state lodging tax (most states use 30 or 90). The local accommodations tax usually mirrors the same rule.

## Platform collection

Airbnb collects and remits the **8% Delaware state lodging tax** under a voluntary collection agreement with the Division of Revenue. **Vrbo does not currently have a state-wide collection agreement** in Delaware — Vrbo hosts are responsible for collecting and remitting state lodging tax themselves. Local accommodations taxes (Rehoboth, Dewey, Bethany, etc.) are **not collected by either platform** — those land on the host.

## What this means in practice

- **Airbnb hosts** — state tax handled; local tax is your responsibility. Register with your beach town's finance department.
- **Vrbo or direct-booking hosts** — register with the Delaware Division of Revenue for the state lodging tax AND with your local town. File monthly.
- **Beach-town permits** — Rehoboth, Dewey, and Bethany all require a separate rental license on top of tax registration; renewal is annual.
- **120+ night stays** — exempt from state tax. This is generous compared to most states.

## How to use the calculator above

1. Enter your booking subtotal (nightly × nights + cleaning fee, before tax).
2. Enter the local add-on rate that applies to your address. Rehoboth, Dewey, Bethany, and Lewes are each 3%; most inland addresses are 0%.
3. The result shows the effective rate (11% beach / 8% inland), total tax, and what the guest sees.
