## How Georgia lodging tax works

Georgia stacks **three** tax components on short-term rental stays:

1. **4% state sales tax** — applies to the room charge.
2. **$5 per night state hotel-motel fee** — flat fee added on top, regardless of nightly rate. (Yes, even a $40 cabin in Blue Ridge owes $5 per night.)
3. **Local hotel-motel tax** — counties and cities add up to **8% on top**, often dedicated to convention/tourism authorities.

Effective rates in the top markets:
- **Savannah** (Chatham County): 4% state + 8% local + $5/night = roughly **12% + $5/night**
- **Atlanta** (Fulton County): 4% state + 8% local + $5/night = roughly **12% + $5/night**
- **Blue Ridge / Fannin County**: 4% state + 5% local + $5/night = roughly **9% + $5/night**
- **Tybee Island**: 4% state + 6% local + $5/night = roughly **10% + $5/night**

## Platform collection

Airbnb and Vrbo collect and remit the **4% state sales tax** in most cases. They also remit the **$5/night state fee** on bookings that go through their platform. For **local hotel-motel tax**, coverage is jurisdiction-specific — Atlanta and Savannah are typically covered; smaller counties often are not.

The Georgia DOR publishes the [marketplace facilitator list](https://dor.georgia.gov/) confirming which platforms collect for which jurisdictions. Always verify against the latest list before assuming local tax is handled.

## What this means in practice

- For **state sales tax + $5 fee** — usually fine; platforms handle it.
- For **local hotel-motel tax** — if your county/city isn't on the platform list, you need to register with the local tax-collection authority, file periodic returns (usually monthly), and remit yourself.
- For **direct bookings** — none of this is handled for you. Register and file all three layers.
- **30+ night stays** are generally exempt from the state hotel-motel fee and from local hotel-motel tax in most jurisdictions, but sales tax may still apply. Verify locally.

## How to use the calculator above

1. Enter your booking subtotal (nightly × nights + cleaning fee, before tax).
2. Set the local add-on rate. Savannah and Atlanta = 8%. Blue Ridge = 5%. Tybee = 6%. Check your county.
3. Read the effective rate and tax total. Remember: the $5/night fee is a flat add — most calculators omit it; budget it separately.
