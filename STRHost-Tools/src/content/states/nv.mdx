## How Nevada lodging tax works

Nevada has **no state-level lodging tax**. Every dollar of room tax is collected at the county level under the **Transient Lodging Tax** statute, and the rates are among the highest in the country:

- **Clark County / Las Vegas Strip**: 13.38% combined transient lodging tax
- **Clark County / Downtown Las Vegas**: 13% combined
- **Clark County / unincorporated**: 12% combined
- **Washoe County / Reno / Sparks**: 13% combined
- **Douglas County / South Lake Tahoe**: 13.5% combined
- **Carson City**: 10% combined

These rates are NOT add-ons to a state tax — they're the whole tax. Nevada's hotel-room industry pays the entire lodging-tax burden through counties and dedicated tourism districts.

## Platform collection

Airbnb and Vrbo collect and remit the **Clark County, Washoe County, and Douglas County transient lodging tax** as marketplace facilitators. For smaller counties (Elko, Nye, Lyon, etc.), coverage is incomplete — verify before assuming collection.

The [Nevada Department of Taxation](https://tax.nv.gov/) and each county's room-tax authority publish marketplace-facilitator status. Always check the latest list.

## What this means in practice

- For **major STR markets** (Vegas, Reno, Tahoe) — platforms handle it.
- For **smaller counties** — verify; you may need to register with the county's room-tax authority, file monthly returns, and remit.
- **Clark County is strict on STR licensing.** Tax compliance is separate from the STR business license, which is required separately and renewed annually.
- **30+ night stays** — generally exempt from transient lodging tax in most Nevada counties. Verify locally.
- **No state sales tax on lodging** — Nevada doesn't stack a state sales tax. The transient lodging tax is the only tax on the room charge.
