## How Pennsylvania lodging tax works

Pennsylvania stacks **three** components on short-term rental stays under 30 days:

1. **6% state hotel occupancy tax** — applies to the room charge (parallels the sales tax, but dedicated to lodging).
2. **1% local sales/occupancy tax** — Allegheny County (Pittsburgh) and Philadelphia each add 1% on top.
3. **County hotel tax** — most counties levy a separate hotel room rental tax of 1–10%, dedicated to tourism promotion.

Effective rates in Pennsylvania's top STR markets:
- **Poconos — Pike County** (Milford, Hawley, Lords Valley): 6% state + 5% Pike hotel = **11%**
- **Poconos — Monroe County** (Stroudsburg, Tannersville, Long Pond): 6% + 5% Monroe = **11%**
- **Philadelphia**: 6% state + 1% Philadelphia sales + 8.5% Philadelphia hotel = **~15.5%**
- **Pittsburgh** (Allegheny County): 6% + 1% Allegheny + 7% Allegheny hotel = **~14%**
- **Gettysburg / Adams County**: 6% + 5% Adams hotel = **11%**
- **Lancaster** (Lancaster County): 6% + 5% Lancaster hotel = **11%**

Pike County's 5% hotel tax catches a lot of NYC weekend renters off guard. Philadelphia's 8.5% city hotel tax is among the highest in the Northeast.

## Platform collection

Airbnb and Vrbo collect and remit the **6% state hotel occupancy tax** and the **1% Philadelphia/Allegheny local sales** in most cases. The **county hotel tax** is more variable — Philadelphia and Allegheny are platform-collected; Pike, Monroe, and Lancaster have had agreements for years; smaller counties (Wayne, Sullivan, Forest) are often the host's responsibility.

The [Pennsylvania Department of Revenue](https://www.revenue.pa.gov/) and individual county treasurer offices track current coverage. Confirm each filing period.

## What this means in practice

- For **state and city sales/occupancy** — handled by Airbnb and Vrbo.
- For **county hotel tax** — register with the County Treasurer (Pike, Monroe, Wayne, etc.), file monthly returns, and remit yourself if the platform doesn't cover your county.
- For **direct bookings** — handle every layer yourself.
- **30+ night stays** are exempt from the 6% state hotel occupancy tax. County hotel taxes generally follow the same rule but verify locally.
- Philadelphia and Pittsburgh both require **STR permits and licenses** separate from tax registration. The City of Philadelphia's STR rules are particularly strict — only owner-occupied or licensed limited-lodging operators qualify.

## How to use the calculator above

1. Enter your booking subtotal (nightly rate × nights + cleaning fee, before tax).
2. Set the local add-on rate. Pike or Monroe (Poconos) = 5%. Philadelphia = 9.5% (1% city sales + 8.5% city hotel). Pittsburgh = 8% (1% county sales + 7% county hotel). Lancaster or Adams = 5%.
3. Read the effective rate. State 6% is the foundation; your local add-on layers on top.
