## How Nebraska lodging tax works

Nebraska stacks **three layers** on every short-term stay: a **5.5% state sales tax**, a **1% statewide lodging tax**, and a **local lodging tax** of up to 4% added by counties. Combined effective rates run **6.5% to 10.5%**. Top STR markets:

- **Omaha (Douglas County)** — 5.5% state + 1% statewide + 4% county lodging = **10.5% effective**. The state's largest STR market by booking volume; College World Series weekends spike demand.
- **Lincoln (Lancaster County)** — 5.5% state + 1% statewide + 4% county lodging = **10.5%**. Nebraska Huskers football weekends are the entire annual market.
- **Grand Island, Kearney, North Platte** — typically 5.5% state + 1% statewide + 2–4% county = **8.5–10.5%**. Limited STR markets — most overnight demand is hotel-served.

Nebraska's STR market is one of the smaller in the country by total active listings. Most non-CWS, non-Husker-game demand is short business trips that traditionally book hotels.

Stays of **30 consecutive nights or longer** are exempt from the 1% statewide lodging tax and from county lodging tax.

## Platform collection

Airbnb and Vrbo both collect and remit the **5.5% state sales tax** AND the **1% statewide lodging tax** under marketplace facilitator rules. **County lodging tax collection is inconsistent** — Douglas County (Omaha) and Lancaster County (Lincoln) have agreements with Airbnb but coverage on Vrbo is narrower. Check your tax disclosure for what's actually collected.

## What this means in practice

- **State + statewide lodging tax** — handled by both platforms. No filings required for those layers.
- **County lodging tax** — for Omaha and Lincoln Airbnb hosts, typically handled. For Vrbo hosts or direct-booking hosts, register with your county and file monthly.
- **CWS / Husker game weekends** — ADR spikes 3–5x; tax dollars scale accordingly. Many hosts only operate during these windows.
- **30+ night stays** — exempt from the 1% statewide and from county lodging tax.
