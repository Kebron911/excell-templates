## How Oregon lodging tax works

Oregon has no general sales tax, but it does levy a **state transient lodging tax** plus optional local lodging taxes:

1. **1.5% state transient lodging tax** — applies to all overnight stays.
2. **Local lodging taxes** — cities and counties may add 1%–11.5% on top.

Effective rates in the top markets:
- **Portland**: 1.5% state + 11.5% city/county = **13%**
- **Bend** (Deschutes County): 1.5% state + 10.4% combined = **11.9%**
- **Cannon Beach**: 1.5% state + 8% city = **9.5%**
- **Seaside**: 1.5% state + 8% city = **9.5%**
- **Eugene**: 1.5% state + 4.5% city = **6%**

## Platform collection

Airbnb and Vrbo collect and remit the **1.5% state transient lodging tax** as marketplace facilitators. For **local lodging taxes**, coverage is jurisdiction-specific. Portland, Bend, Cannon Beach, and most coastal cities are typically covered. Smaller unincorporated areas may not be.

The [Oregon Department of Revenue transient-lodging page](https://www.oregon.gov/dor/programs/businesses/Pages/Lodging.aspx) confirms platform-facilitator agreements. Always verify before assuming local tax is handled.

## What this means in practice

- For **state lodging tax** — platforms handle it.
- For **local lodging tax** — if your jurisdiction isn't on the platform list, you must register with the city/county lodging-tax authority, file quarterly (most common in Oregon), and remit.
- For **direct bookings** — both layers are your responsibility regardless of jurisdiction.
- **30+ night stays** are exempt from state and most local transient lodging taxes. The stay must be 30 consecutive nights with the same guest.
- **Portland operators**: the city's STR rules require a permit AND an STR business license separate from tax registration. All three are required to operate legally.
