## How Arizona lodging tax works

Arizona doesn't call it "sales tax." It's the **Transaction Privilege Tax (TPT)** — technically a tax on the seller's privilege of doing business — and short-term rentals get hit three ways:

1. **5.5% state TPT** — the Arizona Department of Revenue's share.
2. **County excise tax** — typically 0.5%–2%, varies by county.
3. **City/town transient lodging tax** — varies wildly. Phoenix adds 5.3%, Scottsdale 5.5%, Sedona 3.5% city + 0.5% bed tax.

Effective rates in the top markets:
- **Phoenix** (Maricopa County): 5.5% state + ~0.7% county + 5.3% city = **roughly 11.5%**
- **Scottsdale**: 5.5% state + ~0.7% county + 5.5% city = **roughly 11.7%**
- **Sedona**: 5.5% state + ~1.3% county + 3.5% city + 0.5% bed = **roughly 10.8%**
- **Tucson** (Pima County): 5.5% state + ~0.5% county + 6% city = **roughly 12%**
- **Lake Havasu**: 5.5% state + ~1.1% county + 5% city = **roughly 11.6%**

## Platform collection

Airbnb and Vrbo collect and remit the **5.5% state TPT** as marketplace facilitators. They typically also remit county and city transient lodging tax in major jurisdictions (Phoenix, Scottsdale, Tucson, Tempe, Sedona). For smaller cities and unincorporated areas, coverage is spotty.

The [Arizona Department of Revenue TPT page](https://azdor.gov/business/transaction-privilege-tax) is the authoritative source. Always verify against the current marketplace-facilitator list.

## What this means in practice

- For **state TPT** — platforms handle it.
- For **city/county lodging tax** — if your city isn't on the platform-collected list, you must register for a TPT license, file monthly returns, and remit yourself. Arizona's TPT license is per-location and per-business-activity (rental of lodging is activity code 025).
- For **direct bookings** — none of this is handled. Register and file all three layers via [AZTaxes.gov](https://aztaxes.gov/).
- **30+ night stays** are taxed differently — long-term residential rental is activity code 029 with different rates. Confirm classification with the AZ DOR.
- **Sedona** has additional STR permitting requirements; tax compliance does NOT replace the permit obligation.
