## How it works

Builds a printable PDF that walks guests through arrival: a summary card with door
code, parking, and your phone, then numbered steps with optional photos, then a tail
page with Wi-Fi credentials. Photos stay in your browser — nothing is uploaded.

## How to use this generator

1. Fill in the property name, optional address, door code, parking, and host phone.
2. Add Wi-Fi credentials (these go on the last page, so guests find them once they're
   inside).
3. Add arrival steps — usually 3 to 6. Be specific. "Lockbox is mounted on the right
   wall, painted to match the trim" beats "find the lockbox."
4. Optionally attach a photo to each step. JPEG or PNG. Files stay client-side.
5. Click **Download PDF** when the preview looks right.

## Why arrival photos matter

Hosts who include 1–2 arrival photos report a measurable drop in "where is the
lockbox?" texts. The first 30 minutes of a stay are when guests are most stressed —
clear visuals reduce that friction more than any amount of polish elsewhere.

## FAQ

### Are photos uploaded to a server?

No. Photo files are read in your browser via the FileReader API and embedded directly
into the PDF. They never leave your device.

### What photo formats are supported?

JPEG (.jpg, .jpeg) and PNG. HEIC files (typical iPhone exports) need to be converted
first — most macOS and iOS apps will export to JPEG when saving.

### How big can the PDF get?

Each photo adds 100KB–500KB depending on size. A typical check-in PDF with 5 steps
and 2 photos lands around 1MB. Email attachment-friendly.

### Can I send this PDF to guests automatically?

Not from this tool — there's no automation layer. Save the PDF and attach it to your
booking platform's pre-arrival message, or upload to your guidebook software.
