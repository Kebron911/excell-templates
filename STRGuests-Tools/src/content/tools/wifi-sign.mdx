## The first thing every guest does

Connects to the wifi. Make it the smoothest interaction of the whole stay.

A printed Wi-Fi sign with a QR code is the difference between "let me find the password" and "we're online." Most modern phones — iOS 11+, Android 10+ — scan a Wi-Fi QR straight from the camera app and connect on tap.

This generator builds a single-page PDF in your browser with a scannable QR + readable credentials. Pick from three design templates depending on the property's tone.

## The three templates

- **Minimal** — quiet typography, lots of whitespace, no flourishes. Looks at home next to a black-and-white photograph or a designer kitchen.
- **Hospitable** — warm terracotta accent, "Welcome" header, recommended for most STRs.
- **Fun** — big QR, playful "Wi-Fi's over here →" header. Vibes for a vacation property or family rental.

## How to use the printable

- **Frame it.** A 5x7 frame on the kitchen counter beats a Sharpie-on-paper note 100% of the time.
- **Print on cardstock.** Standard printer paper warps when it gets near a kitchen sink. 80–110 lb cardstock is sturdy for the price.
- **Test the QR yourself before printing.** Generate the PDF, open it on your phone, scan it with a different phone. Confirm it joins.
- **Re-print when you rotate the password.** Best practice: rotate Wi-Fi password every 12 months and after any incident.

## Frequently asked

**What's the QR format?**
Standard `WIFI:T:WPA;S:...;P:...;;` — every modern smartphone OS recognizes it.

**Does it work for hidden networks?**
The QR generator supports the hidden flag, but the form doesn't expose it yet. Drop a note in the contact form if you need it.

**WPA vs WEP vs open networks?**
Default is WPA (the standard). If your router uses WEP (rare and insecure) or runs an open network, contact us — that's a low-priority feature.

**Why is the QR navy instead of black?**
Brand consistency. Navy reads as cleanly as black on parchment-tinted paper, and matches the rest of the strguests cluster.
