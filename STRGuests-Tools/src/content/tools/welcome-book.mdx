## How it works

The Welcome Book Builder produces a multi-page printable PDF — cover, table of contents,
your sections, local picks, and emergency contacts. Built entirely in your browser. No
upload, no signup, no watermark.

## How to use this generator

1. Set the property name and your host name. These go on the cover.
2. Optional: add a short host bio (1–2 sentences) and Wi-Fi credentials — they get a
   highlight box on the cover.
3. Add as many sections as you need. "Getting in", "House notes", "Heat & A/C",
   "Trash & recycling" are the most-used.
4. Add local picks — coffee, food, attractions. Three to five is plenty.
5. Add emergency contacts — fire/police is 911, plus your contact and the property
   manager if separate.
6. Click **Download PDF** when the preview looks right.

## What good guidebooks share

The best welcome books answer 80% of the questions guests would otherwise text you
about. Be specific: "lockbox 1234, push the door firmly while turning" is better than
"door can be tricky." Lead with the moment they need each piece of info — arrival,
day-one orientation, before they leave.

## FAQ

### Can I add photos?

Not in v1. Photo uploads are on the roadmap for v2. For now, lay out your text-only
guidebook here, then drop in photos with any PDF editor (Preview on macOS, Acrobat
on Windows) before printing.

### How long should the welcome book be?

Six to ten pages is typical. Anything longer guests don't read. The cover, TOC, and
emergency contacts are non-negotiable; everything else is value-add.

### Can I print this and leave it in the rental?

Yes. The PDF is sized for US Letter and prints cleanly on home printers. Spiral
binding works well for a polished look — local print shops will do this for under $10.

### Will my information be stored anywhere?

No. Generation is fully client-side. The PDF is built in your browser; no data leaves
your device.
