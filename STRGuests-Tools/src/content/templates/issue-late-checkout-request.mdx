## Why this template works

Late checkout is the most common request hosts get — and the most likely to be denied
when guests phrase it badly. The template above leads with a specific time, attaches a
real reason (the flight, the meeting), and explicitly accepts the no. That structure
tells the host: "this is a person planning their day, not someone trying to negotiate
a free hour."

## When to send it

48–24 hours before checkout. Same-day asks read as desperate; a week-out ask reads as
presumptuous. Mid-stay is the sweet spot.

## What to swap

- **Reason** — be specific. "Flight at 4pm" beats "kind of need it." Hosts say yes
  more when they know the constraint.
- **Time** — ask for one or two hours, not three. Same-day cleaning windows are
  usually 11am–3pm, so anything past 1pm is a hard ask.

## What hosts won't say yes to

A same-day turnover (most listings have one). If the property is booked for the night
of your checkout, the cleaner needs the full window. Asking is fine; pushing isn't.
