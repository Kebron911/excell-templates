## How to make the charge stick

Most damage charges fall apart not because the damage didn't happen, but because the
host's first message was emotional, vague, or aggressive. The template above is built
around three principles that hold up at the Resolution Center:

1. **Lead with the facts** — what was found, what it costs to replace.
2. **Skip the moralizing** — no "I can't believe you did this." The guest already
   knows.
3. **Open a brief window for context** — "if there's context I should know" makes the
   message feel fair and gives the guest a chance to admit fault privately.

## Documentation matters more than wording

Before you send this message, make sure you have:

- **Time-stamped photos** of the damage taken during turnover
- **Replacement quote** from a real source (Amazon link, vendor invoice)
- **Pre-stay photo** showing the item undamaged (this is the missing one for most hosts)

If you have all three, the Resolution Center claim is straightforward.

## Timing

File within 14 days of checkout. Airbnb's documentation policy gives you that window;
miss it and you're paying for the damage out of pocket.

## When to skip the resolution center

Damage under ~$50 with a previously great guest. The cost of a 4-star "host hard to
work with" review will exceed the recovery. This is judgment territory — but lean
toward eating small damages from otherwise ideal guests.
