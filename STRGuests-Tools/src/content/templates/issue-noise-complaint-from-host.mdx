## Why this is a hard message to write

Noise complaints land somewhere between a customer-service issue and a confrontation.
Get the tone wrong on either side and you've made things worse: too soft and the
guest doesn't take it seriously; too firm and they feel attacked and dig in.

The template above does three things that work in our experience hosting:

1. **Names the source** — "I got a complaint" puts the issue on a third party, not on
   you-vs-them.
2. **References the rule** — "quiet hours per the house rules" anchors the conversation
   in something agreed-upon, not your judgment.
3. **Opens an exit ramp** — "reply here if there's something I should know" gives the
   guest space to explain (a baby crying, a one-off issue) without escalation.

## When to send it

As soon as you reasonably can after the complaint. Waiting 24 hours feels passive-
aggressive when the guest realizes the timing later. Same evening or next morning is
right.

## What hosts overcorrect on

Sending screenshots, neighbor names, or "I have video evidence." Almost never helpful.
Reserve those for the **second** message, if there's a second incident.

## When to skip the template and go straight to escalation

Active party, repeated violations, neighbor police call. At that point you're moving
to the [party violation template](/templates/issue-party-violation), and possibly the
[stay termination template](/templates/issue-stay-termination).
