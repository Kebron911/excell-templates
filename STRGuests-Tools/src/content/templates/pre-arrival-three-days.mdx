## The most important message of the entire stay

The 3-days-out pre-arrival is the single highest-leverage message a host sends. Get
this one right and you cut day-of questions by ~60% and prevent most of the issues
that turn into 4-star reviews.

## Why 3 days

It's the right balance between "early enough to be useful" and "close enough that
guests will actually act on it." 5 days out, guests forget. 24 hours out, they're
already in transit and can't process new info. The 72-hour mark is the planning
window.

## What to never include

- Long pleasantries — guests skim
- Photos of the property — they've seen the listing
- House rules in full — link to the welcome book PDF instead
- Information that wasn't on the listing — surprises read as bait-and-switch

## What guests actually want at this point

The 7 fields in the template above, in this order. Anything else is noise. If you
have something special — a season-specific tip, a roadwork warning — add a single
short note at the end.

## A note on door codes

Some hosts hold the door code until day-of, citing security. That tradeoff isn't
worth it: about 4% of guests forget the code on arrival and have to dig through old
messages. Send it 3 days out, and again on the morning of arrival as the
[day-of message](/templates/pre-arrival-day-of). Two touches, no missed codes.
