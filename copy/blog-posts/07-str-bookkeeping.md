## Outline

### H1: STR Bookkeeping: Why QuickBooks Generic Setup Costs You $4,000/Year (And the Fix)

### Intro (200 words)
- Hook: QuickBooks Online has a "rental property" template. It's built for long-term landlords with one rent check per month. For STR ops with 96 stays/year, 8 contractor types, 12 expense categories, and a lodging-tax remittance schedule, it falls apart.
- Promise: a purpose-built STR bookkeeping system that auto-maps every dollar to Schedule E and saves you $4,000+/year in missed deductions + categorization fixes.

### Section 1: Why generic bookkeeping setups fail for STR
- Long-term landlord assumption (1 rent check, 1 expense check, repeat)
- Lodging tax mistreated as revenue (it's a liability passthrough)
- Cleaner fee collected vs paid — single-line instead of two
- OTA fees coded as advertising (should be Line 11 management)
- No clean way to tag personal-use days (mandatory if any)

### Section 2: The 60-account STR Chart of Accounts
- High-level outline of category groups (Assets → Liab → Equity → Income → Expense → Depreciation)
- Naming convention: `<number> - <Account Name>` for QBO compatibility
- Schedule E line mapping in a hidden column
- Reference: pre-built file at /free/chart-of-accounts

### Section 3: The monthly close routine (10 minutes flat)
1. Bank reconciliation (Mercury/Relay/Chase Business)
2. Card reconciliation
3. Categorize uncategorized transactions
4. Verify lodging tax liability balance
5. Verify cleaner passthrough netting to $0
6. Run Schedule E preview report
7. Lock the period

### Section 4: The annual close + tax handoff

### Section 6: 5 common bookkeeping disasters
1. Commingled funds (personal credit card used for STR expenses + vice versa)
2. Lodging tax sitting in revenue for 18 months
3. Year-old uncategorized transactions
4. No bank reconciliation since Q3
5. Cleaner passthrough net was -$3K (you paid cleaner more than guest cleaning fee — money lost)
