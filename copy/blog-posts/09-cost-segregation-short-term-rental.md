### Intro (250 words)
- Hook: A typical $400K STR property → naive depreciation = ~$14K/yr on the 27.5-yr building schedule. Cost segregation breakout → ~$25K first-year deduction (almost double). The extra $11K is sitting there for hosts who know to ask.
- Promise: the rule, the methodology, when DIY makes sense, when to pay for a study, and the dollar-by-dollar example.

### Section 1: What cost segregation actually is
- Default depreciation: entire purchase price (minus land) → 27.5-yr straight-line
- Cost seg: break out shorter-life components (5/7/15-yr) → accelerate the deduction into early years
- IRS-blessed: this is an audit-tested methodology, codified in Rev. Proc. 2014-16 and Reg. §1.263(a)-3
- Why short-term rentals matter (more than long-term rentals): STR income is often *active* via material participation, so the deductions offset other income (not just rental income)

### Section 2: The 4 cost-seg categories
| Category | Recovery period | What goes here (STR) |
|----------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Personal property (5-yr) | 5 years | Appliances, carpets, removable cabinetry, decorative lighting |
| Personal property (7-yr) | 7 years | Furniture, fixtures, decorative items |
| Land improvements (15-yr) | 15 years | Driveway, fence, landscaping, exterior lighting, irrigation |
| Building structure (27.5-yr) | 27.5 years | Everything else |

Bonus depreciation overlay: phase-out from 80% (2023) → 60% (2024) → 40% (2026) → 20% (2027) → 0% (2028). Adds dollars to year 1 for items not at full bonus.

### Section 3: The dollar example (the showstopper)

$500K STR purchase:
- $100K land (non-depreciable)
- $400K depreciable basis

**Without cost seg:**
- $400K / 27.5 yr = **$14,545/yr** for 27.5 years (straight-line)

**With cost seg (typical breakdown):**
- 25% to 5-yr personal property = $100K → $20K/yr for 5 yrs (with 40% bonus in year 1: $40K + $12K = **$52K year 1**)
- 10% to 7-yr personal property = $40K → year 1 with bonus: $16K + ~$3K = **$19K year 1**
- 10% to 15-yr land improvements = $40K → year 1 with bonus: $16K + ~$1.6K = **$17.6K year 1**
- 55% to 27.5-yr building = $220K → $8K/yr straight-line

**Year 1 total deduction:**
- With cost seg: $52K + $19K + $17.6K + $8K = **$96.6K**
- Without cost seg: $14.5K

**Year 1 extra deduction: ~$82K** (which at 32% combined tax = ~$26K of actual tax savings)

(Note: bonus % needs to be current-year-verified before publishing — these numbers assume 2026.)

### Section 5: The 4-step DIY methodology (for the $200K–$500K segment)
1. **Get the purchase appraisal** (or build one) — break out land vs. depreciable basis (typically land = 15–25%, varies by area)
2. **Photograph and inventory every item in the property** at the placed-in-service date
3. **Map each item to its MACRS class** using the [Depreciation Asset Quick-Lookup](thestrledger.com/free/depreciation-lookup)
4. **Run the math** in TAX-006 Cost Segregation Worksheet — outputs Form 4562-ready numbers

### Section 6: The "material participation" multiplier
- For STR hosts who **materially participate** (500+ hrs/yr OR meet other tests), STR rental losses are NOT passive
- Non-passive losses can offset other income (W-2, business income, etc.) — not just rental income
- Combined with cost-seg-accelerated deductions, this creates the "STR tax loophole" you see on YouTube
- Caveat: material participation is a specific test and audit-defensible records matter (use TAX-008 Material Participation Log)

### Section 7: The "look-back" study (rescuing prior-year cost seg)
- If you bought your STR 3 years ago and never did cost seg, the **Form 3115 change-in-accounting** lets you catch up the entire missed depreciation in the current year
- No amended returns required — single year catch-up
- This is the "secret weapon" for hosts who've owned for years and just learned about cost seg
- Worth a CPA conversation — Form 3115 is form-heavy

### Section 8: The 4 traps
1. **Allocating too much to land** (auditors push back if your land allocation is < 15-20% in most markets)
2. **Classifying built-in items as personal property** — kitchen cabinets that are permanently attached are 27.5-yr, not 5-yr
3. **Skipping the placed-in-service date** — you can only depreciate from when the property was rentable, not when purchased
4. **Trying to apply cost seg without the right records** — IRS asks for the cost-seg study or methodology document; "I just kinda broke it out" doesn't survive audit

### Section 9: Combined with the STR loophole — what hosts are claiming
- Year 1 deduction $96K (cost seg) + material participation = offsets $96K of other income
- At 32% combined marginal rate = **$30,720 of year-1 tax savings**
- For someone who paid $500K for an STR with 20% down ($100K), that's effectively recovering 30% of the down payment in year-1 tax savings

This is the "STR tax loophole" that YouTube influencers shout about. It's real. It requires correct execution.
