## Post construction standard

Every post:

- **Word count:** 1,500–2,500 words
- **Structure:** H1 → 3–5 H2 sections → H3 subheadings within H2s
- **Internal links:** minimum 3 (to other blog posts, lead magnet page, and a product page)
- **External links:** 1–2 to authoritative sources (IRS.gov, Airbnb help docs, relevant court cases)
- **Featured image:** 1200×630 Canva design, brand-colors, clean
- **SEO title:** keyword front-loaded, under 60 chars
- **Meta description:** 155 chars max, answers "what will I get by reading this?"
- **Call-to-action:** embedded twice (mid-post + end)
- **Lead-magnet prompt:** every post offers the `/47` magnet in the first 300 words AND at end

## Pin multiplier

For each published post, create **3–5 Pinterest pins** with different angles:

- Pin A: "tip list" style ("12 Airbnb Tax Deductions You Might Be Missing")
- Pin B: "quote card" style ("The $8,427 Deduction Hosts Miss")
- Pin C: "infographic" style (visual breakdown)
- Pin D: "question" style ("Are You Filing Schedule E or Schedule C for Your Airbnb?")
- Pin E: "before/after" style (shows spreadsheet preview)

All pins link back to the blog post. Schedule via Tailwind (see Task C2).

## What not to write

- Generic "What is Airbnb?" posts (no buyer intent)
- Pure listicles without a conversion goal
- "10 things I love about hosting" lifestyle content (wrong brand)
- Anything that could be mistaken for legal or tax advice (disclaimers required: "This is general information, not tax advice. Consult your CPA.")
