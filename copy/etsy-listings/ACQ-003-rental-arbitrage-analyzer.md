Run your rentals before they run you. Rental arbitrage deal analyzer for STR operators leasing units to sublet on Airbnb. Lease math, stress test, MTR fallback. Excel.

Read this first. Rental arbitrage is legal only when your lease permits subletting AND your jurisdiction permits short-term rentals. If your lease says no sublet, or your city's STR registry is closed, the math doesn't matter — the deal is illegal. This workbook flips the verdict to PASS automatically when either flag is missing. Consult an attorney before signing any master-lease agreement.

Now the math.

The reason most arbitrage units fail is not Year 1. It is Year 2, when the landlord raises the rent 7 to 10 percent at renewal and the operator has $14,800 of furniture in a unit that no longer cash flows. This analyzer stress-tests against that exact scenario before you sign.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
FAQ
═══════════════════════════════════════════

Q: Is rental arbitrage legal?
A: It depends on your lease and your city. If your lease permits subletting in writing, and your city's STR ordinance permits non-owner-occupied short-term rentals, yes. If either is missing, no. The workbook flags both. Consult an attorney before signing.

Q: What is the Landlord Pitch Page?
A: A one-page printable master-lease proposal — landlord-friendly framing (guaranteed rent, professional management, no tenant turnover). White-label, no STR Ledger marks. Add your own logo.

Q: Why MTR fallback?
A: Real arbitrage operators have a Plan B. If STR demand collapses in your market, mid-term rentals (corporate housing, traveling nurses, 30+ day stays) often cover rent at 90 percent occupancy. The workbook benchmarks this against Furnished Finder rates.

Q: How does the stress test work?
A: Three scenarios. Base = your Year 1 inputs. Stabilized = same numbers with a 5 percent renewal rent hike (Year 2). Worst = 7 percent rent hike, 10 percentage point occupancy drop, 15 percent supplies inflation. Each returns DEAL, MARGINAL, or PASS.

Q: One workbook per unit, or one for the whole portfolio?
A: One per unit. Arbitrage units stand or fall on their own lease terms.
