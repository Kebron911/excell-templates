Run your rentals before they run you. RevPAR, ADR, and Occupancy dashboard for STR hosts. YoY comparison, comp benchmark, sensitivity simulator. Editable Excel.

Your friend with three cabins keeps quoting his RevPAR at $187. You nod. You look at gross revenue every month but you genuinely cannot tell whether you are winning per night or just charging more, or whether last quarter was a real lift or a hot streak.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHY THE BLOCKED-NIGHT METHOD MATTERS
═══════════════════════════════════════════

Most templates do one of two wrong things with blocked nights. They ignore the blocks entirely (overstates your occupancy in your favor — useless when you need to compare to market). Or they count blocks as "available" (understates occupancy and depresses your real number).

The correct method, used by PriceLabs and AirDNA: blocked nights are excluded from the denominator. If you pulled a property for two weeks of personal use, those nights do not count against you. Your occupancy figure becomes comparable to industry benchmarks — and you can finally say what your friend with three cabins is saying.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHY THIS PAYS FOR ITSELF IN ONE PRICING DECISION
═══════════════════════════════════════════

Run the sensitivity simulator on a $200 ADR property at 65% occupancy: lifting ADR $10 yields about $2,030 a year. Raising Occupancy 5pp on the same property yields about $4,470 a year. That ratio — Occupancy moves more revenue than ADR at most price points — is the single most useful piece of information for setting your minimum-stay rules and weekend pricing.

One pricing decision informed by this dashboard typically returns 30-100x the workbook cost in incremental revenue.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
FAQ
═══════════════════════════════════════════

Q: I do not have an AirDNA subscription. Will the dashboard still work?
A: Yes. The comp-benchmark line auto-hides if Settings is empty. The dashboard then shows your YoY comparison only. AirDNA is optional context, not required input.

Q: What is RevPAR exactly?
A: Revenue Per Available Room (or in STR-speak, per available night). RevPAR = Gross Revenue ÷ (Total Nights − Blocked Nights). It mathematically equals ADR × Occupancy. The advantage: it captures both rate and occupancy in one number, so you cannot game it by pricing high and selling little.

Q: How do I get prior-year reference data if I never tracked this before?
A: Pull total gross revenue and total booked nights per property per month from Airbnb / VRBO host dashboards. Enter on Settings rows 40-100. About thirty minutes of data entry for a full prior year of one property.

Q: Multi-year tracking?
A: One workbook per tax year, by design. Re-run annually using prior year as the reference table. Trying to track multiple years degrades performance with the daily calendar pivot.

Q: Does this forecast pace pickup or do demand modeling?
A: No. Out of scope. This is a backwards-looking KPI dashboard plus a forward-looking what-if simulator. Pace pickup analytics is a separate SKU on the roadmap.
