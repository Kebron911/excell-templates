═══════════════════════════════════════════
FAQ
═══════════════════════════════════════════

Q: I already own TAX-002 (single-property P&L). Do I throw those out?
A: No — keep them as the prior-year archive. This workbook replaces them going forward. Categories match exactly so you can paste prior totals into the YoY reference table on Settings.

Q: How does shared-expense allocation work?
A: On the Expense Log, set Shared? = Yes and list the property names in the Allocation column (comma-separated). The workbook splits the amount equally across those properties at calculation time. For square-footage-weighted allocation, enter as separate rows with the math done by hand — a footnote on Settings explains.

Q: Multi-year tracking?
A: One workbook per tax year, intentionally. Trying to track multiple years in one file degrades formula performance above 5,000 expense rows. Re-run the workbook annually using prior-year as your YoY reference.

Q: What if a property changes ownership mid-year?
A: Use the ownership % column on Properties Register and document the effective date in the notes. For deeper mid-year ownership shifts, allocate the months in two passes and hand both to your CPA.

Q: Does this work for international properties?
A: No — US Schedule E only. Foreign rental properties have separate tax treatment.
