## Description

```
Close your year before April does. Schedule E P&L for Airbnb and STR hosts — IRS line-mapped categories, built-in 27.5-yr depreciation calculator, CPA-ready Excel.

Your CPA hands back your Schedule E draft and says "your categories don't map — I had
to rebuild it." Three hours billable. Or you skip depreciation because the calculator
felt complicated, and leave $10,000+ on the table.

This template fixes both. Every expense category maps directly to a Schedule E line
number. The built-in depreciation tab calculates straight-line residential rental
(27.5 years, mid-month convention) — auto-fills Line 20 for you. Hand the Schedule E
Summary tab to your CPA and they copy your numbers straight into the form.

───────────────────────
WHAT'S INCLUDED
───────────────────────

📋 Property Info
   · Address, purchase price, closing costs, loan terms
   · In-service date, days rented, personal-use days
   · Auto-flags Pub 527's 14-day / 10% personal-use threshold

📋 Revenue Log (1,000-row capacity)
   · Date, guest, channel (Airbnb/VRBO/Booking.com/Direct/Other)
   · Gross + platform fee + cleaning fee collected + net
   · Nights column drives the STR loophole eligibility check

📋 Expense Log (2,000-row capacity)
   · 17 expense categories, all Schedule E line-mapped
   · Dropdown-enforced — no miscategorized rows
   · Date, vendor, category, amount, payment method, receipt flag

📋 Monthly P&L (auto)
   · 12 months × 17 expense categories
   · Net income row with green/red conditional formatting
   · Donut chart: expense composition

📋 Depreciation Calculator (NEW — auto-fills Schedule E Line 20)
   · Straight-line 27.5-yr residential rental, mid-month convention
   · Pulls cost basis from Property Info
   · Land-value input + depreciable basis + annual + current-year amounts
   · Prior-year accumulated override + implied-prior helper

📋 Schedule E Summary (CPA hand-off)
   · IRS Schedule E Part I line totals (Lines 3–26a)
   · Audit-defense block: STR loophole eligibility (IRC §469),
     14-day personal-use test (Pub 527), QBI safe-harbor flag (§199A)
   · Schedule E vs Schedule C routing footnote
   · Print area set — letter portrait, ready to print

───────────────────────
WHY STR HOSTS NEED THIS
───────────────────────

Two problems every single-property host has at tax time:

1. CATEGORY DRIFT — your spreadsheet uses "internet," "Wi-Fi," "comcast,"
   and "ISP" all in different rows. Your CPA sees 200 expense rows
   that don't roll up to Schedule E lines. You pay them to clean it up.

2. SKIPPED DEPRECIATION — the biggest deduction most hosts miss.
   A $400,000 property with $75,000 in land = $11,800/yr in depreciation.
   At a 24% federal bracket, that's $2,832/yr in tax savings you don't
   take because the IRS depreciation rules feel complicated.

This template solves both: enforced Schedule E categories on every
expense row, and a depreciation tab that does the 27.5-year math for you.

───────────────────────
WHO THIS IS FOR
───────────────────────

· Airbnb / VRBO / direct-booking hosts with ONE rental property
· Hosts whose CPA charges by the hour to "clean up" their spreadsheet
· Anyone leaving depreciation off their Schedule E because the math felt scary
· First-year hosts who need to file Schedule E in April
· Side-Hustle Sam (1 listing) and Semi-Pro Sarah's first property

───────────────────────
HOW IT WORKS
───────────────────────

1. Setup (10 min): fill the Property Info tab — purchase, loan,
   in-service date. Most fields auto-pull into the Depreciation tab.
2. Every booking: log a row in the Revenue Log (30 seconds).
3. Every expense: log a row in the Expense Log, pick a category
   from the dropdown (10 seconds).
4. Anytime: open the Monthly P&L for live revenue/expense view.
5. April: print the Schedule E Summary tab. Hand it to your CPA.
   They copy numbers into Schedule E Lines 3–26.
```
