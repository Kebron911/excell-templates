## Description

```
Hand your CPA a finished Schedule E. Year-end tax-prep workbook for Airbnb and STR hosts — every Schedule E line pre-mapped, depreciation included, audit-defense block built in.

Your CPA opens your spreadsheet in March. Two hours later, she texts: "your categories
don't map to Schedule E — I rebuilt it." Three hours billable. Or you do your own taxes
in TurboTax, hit Line 5 (Rents received), and stare blankly at the screen because your
Airbnb summary, Vrbo summary, and Direct-booking spreadsheet don't agree.

This workbook is the bridge. It turns a year of bookings, expenses, and depreciation
into a single Schedule E summary tab — with line numbers, audit-defense flags, and a
ready-to-print page your CPA copies straight into the form.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHAT'S INCLUDED
═══════════════════════════════════════════

📋 Year-End Workbook Wizard
   · Walks you through every Schedule E line in order
   · 17 expense categories pre-mapped to Schedule E Part I lines (3-26)
   · Multi-channel revenue rollup (Airbnb / VRBO / Booking.com / Direct)
   · Personal-use day tracker — auto-flags Pub 527's 14-day / 10% threshold

📋 Depreciation Worksheet
   · 27.5-year straight-line residential rental
   · Mid-month convention for the in-service year
   · Auto-fills Schedule E Line 20

📋 Schedule E Summary Tab (CPA hand-off)
   · IRS Schedule E Part I line totals (Lines 3–26a)
   · Print-ready, single page, letter portrait
   · Net income with green/red conditional formatting

📋 Audit-Defense Block
   · STR loophole eligibility (IRC §469) — avg stay ≤ 7 days flag
   · 14-day / 10% personal-use test (Pub 527)
   · QBI safe-harbor flag (§199A) — 250+ service hours
   · Schedule E vs. Schedule C routing footnote

📋 Settings + Archive
   · Active tax year (drives every date filter)
   · Schedule E vs Schedule C classification dropdown
   · Year-end archive ritual — duplicate this file, mark "filed", start fresh

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHY STR HOSTS NEED THIS
═══════════════════════════════════════════

Two specific problems every Schedule E filer hits in March:

1. CATEGORY DRIFT — your spreadsheet uses "internet," "Wi-Fi," "comcast,"
   and "ISP" all in different rows. Your CPA bills you to clean it up.

2. AUDIT-DEFENSE DEAD ZONES — STR loophole? Personal-use threshold?
   QBI safe harbor? Most CPAs ask, most hosts don't know, and you both
   end up assuming it doesn't apply (it usually does — these are big).

This workbook handles both. Categories are dropdown-enforced. Audit-defense
checks fire automatically based on your numbers. Hand the file to your CPA
in one piece.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHO THIS IS FOR
═══════════════════════════════════════════

· Airbnb / VRBO / direct-booking hosts filing Schedule E
· Single-property and small-portfolio hosts (1-3 rentals)
· Anyone whose CPA charges by the hour to "clean up" their spreadsheet
· DIY filers using TurboTax / FreeTaxUSA who need clean Schedule E numbers
· First-year hosts who've never filed Schedule E before

═══════════════════════════════════════════
HOW IT WORKS
═══════════════════════════════════════════

1. Open the workbook in January after year-end. Pre-loaded with sample data.
2. Set the active tax year on the Settings tab.
3. Paste your year's bookings into the Revenue Log (or use TAX-002 if you tracked there).
4. Paste your year's expenses into the Expense Log, picking from the dropdown for each.
5. Open the Depreciation tab — confirm your in-service date and land value.
6. Open Schedule E Summary. Print. Hand to CPA, or copy into TurboTax line by line.
```
