## Description

```
Front-load this year's depreciation. Legally. Section 179 planner for Airbnb and STR hosts. Deduct furniture, appliances, business vehicles in year-1 instead of over 5/7/27.5 years. Editable Excel.

You bought $18,000 of furniture for the new cabin in February. Default IRS treatment
spreads that deduction over five years — $3,600/year. Section 179 lets you deduct
the full $18,000 this year. At a 24% bracket, that's $4,320 in tax saved THIS
April instead of $864 spread over five Aprils.

The catch: §179 has rules. Active-business test. Annual cap. Property type limits
(no real property — that's bonus depreciation territory). Vehicle weight thresholds.
This workbook walks the rules, shows what qualifies, and produces the Form 4562
numbers ready to drop into your tax return.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHAT'S INCLUDED
═══════════════════════════════════════════

📋 Eligibility Decision Tab
   · Active trade-or-business test (Schedule C: yes; pure Schedule E: usually no)
   · Active-participation Schedule E exception
   · Asset class eligibility (furniture, appliances, equipment, vehicles ≥6,000 lb GVWR)

📋 Asset Inventory (50-asset capacity)
   · Date placed in service, cost basis, asset class
   · MACRS recovery period (5/7/15-year) baseline
   · §179 vs bonus depreciation vs straight-line — three scenarios per asset

📋 Three-Way Depreciation Comparison
   · §179 (immediate full deduction up to cap)
   · Bonus depreciation (currently phasing — verify current-year %)
   · Straight-line MACRS (default)
   · Side-by-side year-1 deduction $ comparison per asset

📋 Annual §179 Cap Tracker
   · Current-year cap (verify against IRS — changes annually)
   · Phase-out starts when total assets exceed threshold
   · Workbook flags when you hit the cap

📋 Vehicle Worksheet (special rules)
   · 6,000 lb GVWR threshold (SUVs, trucks, vans)
   · Business-use percentage (must be ≥50% in year 1)
   · "Heavy SUV" annual cap ($30,500 in 2025 — verify current year)
   · Listed-property recapture rules

📋 Form 4562 Output
   · Line numbers populated
   · Print-ready, ready to hand to CPA or copy into TurboTax

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHY STR HOSTS NEED THIS
═══════════════════════════════════════════

Section 179 is one of three depreciation accelerators (the others are bonus
depreciation and cost segregation). It's the one most STR hosts can actually
use without hiring a specialist — IF they're on Schedule C or have material
participation on Schedule E.

The dollars: $18K of new furniture × 24% bracket = $4,320 in year-1 tax saved
vs. $864/year for 5 years on default MACRS. Workbook is $27. Year-one ROI on
a single furnishing buy: 160×.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHO THIS IS FOR
═══════════════════════════════════════════

· Schedule C STR hosts (active business)
· Schedule E hosts with material participation (active-participation exception)
· Hosts who furnished a property in the current tax year
· Hosts who bought a heavy SUV (6,000+ lb GVWR) used for the business
· Multi-property operators standardizing furnishings across the portfolio

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHO THIS IS NOT FOR
═══════════════════════════════════════════

· Pure-passive Schedule E filers (§179 generally not available — use straight-line MACRS)
· Hosts looking to depreciate the building itself (real property — see TAX-010 cost seg
   or TAX-002 P&L tracker for 27.5-year SL)

═══════════════════════════════════════════
HOW IT WORKS
═══════════════════════════════════════════

1. Open the workbook. Start with the Eligibility tab.
2. Confirm you're Schedule C or Schedule E with material participation.
3. Add every depreciable asset acquired this year to the Asset Inventory.
4. Open the Three-Way Comparison — see year-1 deduction under each method per asset.
5. The workbook recommends §179 for assets where it's the highest deduction,
    bonus or straight-line otherwise. You override if needed.
6. Print the Form 4562 Output. Hand to CPA or copy to your tax software.
```
