## Description

```
K-1-ready capital accounts. Per-partner allocations done right. Partnership distribution tracker for STR LLCs taxed as partnerships. Capital contributions, P&L allocation by ownership %, distributions paid, ending capital per partner. Excel + Google Sheets.

You partner-up to buy a cabin. Three partners, 50/30/20 split. Year 1 ends —
who gets what cash distribution? What's each partner's K-1 figure for tax
filing? Without a system, partner relationships get tense fast: someone
thinks they got short-changed, the K-1s don't reconcile, and your tax
preparer charges 3 hours to clean it up.

This workbook tracks: capital contributions by partner + date, profit
allocation by ownership %, distributions paid per partner per date, ending
capital account. Outputs the per-partner figures needed for K-1 prep on
Form 1065.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHAT'S INCLUDED
═══════════════════════════════════════════

📋 Partnership Roster — partners + ownership % + capital contributions
📋 P&L Inputs — annual revenue, expenses, NOI for the partnership
📋 Distribution Schedule — every cash distribution per partner per date
📋 Capital Account Per Partner — opening + contributions + allocated profit − distributions − allocated losses = ending
📋 K-1 Summary Per Partner — ordinary income, guaranteed payments, distributions, ending capital
📋 Allocation Reconciliation — flags if profit allocations don't sum to 100%

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHO THIS IS FOR
═══════════════════════════════════════════

· STR partnerships filing Form 1065
· Multi-member LLCs (2+ partners)
· Family or friend-group STR investments
· Anyone whose tax preparer asked "where's the partner capital schedule?"

═══════════════════════════════════════════
DISCLAIMER — IMPORTANT
═══════════════════════════════════════════

Partnership tax is complex. Special allocations (allocations not strictly
proportional to ownership), guaranteed payments, §704(b) rules,
capital-account maintenance — all require CPA judgment. This workbook
handles the standard case (proportional allocation, no special allocations);
for anything more complex, your CPA may need to override.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHO THIS IS NOT FOR
═══════════════════════════════════════════

· Single-member LLCs (no partnership filing — use TAX-011 instead)
· Partnerships with special §704(b) allocations (consult CPA)
· S-corp filers (different filing regime — Form 1120-S)

═══════════════════════════════════════════
FILE COMPATIBILITY
═══════════════════════════════════════════

✅ Excel 2016+ · ✅ Excel 365 · ✅ Google Sheets
```
