## Description

```
Run your rentals before they run you. A career-transition planner for STR hosts asking the real question: when can I quit? Calculates your quit date — optimistic and conservative — based on your portfolio's actual numbers. Excel.

This is not a hype workbook. It will not tell you to quit tomorrow. It will tell you, based on your W2, your portfolio's current Schedule E numbers, your savings rate, and your household expenses, how many years and months until the math actually works. The answer is rarely what you hoped. It is usually further out than the gurus claim and closer than you fear. The spread between optimistic and conservative is the conversation you have been avoiding with your partner.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHAT'S INCLUDED — LITE ($27 ETSY)
═══════════════════════════════════════════

Excel workbook (3 tabs):

- Start — THE quit date. Optimistic and conservative dates side by side. The spread between them. Four critical numbers: properties needed, cash needed at quit, healthcare bridge cost, projected Y1-after-quit net.
- Current State — W2 income, STR portfolio (gross + Schedule E line 26 net), household expenses, liquid runway, partner W2, employer benefits value (401k match, health insurance, life, disability).
- Target State — replacement income, buffer multiple (default 1.5×), reserve target, properties needed at current per-property economics.

Lite calculates years-to-quit. Full adds the acquisition schedule, healthcare bridge math, partner risk-share, and the printable Conversation Doc.

The cost of an extra year at a job you want to leave is not measured in dollars. It is measured in mornings. But the cost of quitting too early — running out of runway before stabilization, taking a 401k loan to cover a roof repair, going back to W2 at 50 percent of your prior salary — that has a number, and the number is large.

Most "when can I quit" math is napkin math. This workbook runs the spreadsheet version. Healthcare bridge alone — 18 months of COBRA at $14,400/yr family of 4 plus 6 months of ACA — is roughly $28,800. Most planners forget this entirely. We do not.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
FAQ
═══════════════════════════════════════════

Q: What does Optimistic vs Conservative actually change?
A: Y1 occupancy assumptions, stabilized occupancy, acquisition pace (3 properties/year vs 1.5), appreciation rate, buffer multiple, and how aggressively partner risk-share is applied. Optimistic uses your portfolio's actual numbers extrapolated cleanly. Conservative haircuts everything.

Q: Why is healthcare so prominent?
A: Because it is the W2-quit blocker no one talks about. COBRA at 102 percent of premium for 18 months is roughly $14,400/yr family of 4. ACA after that is roughly $9,600 to $14,400/yr depending on income (subsidies phase out). Total bridge cost: typically $25,000 to $35,000. Most quit-the-W2 plans forget this entirely.

Q: What is the Conversation Doc?
A: A one-page printable for the kitchen table. Quit dates, four critical numbers, three swing factors, action plan, two partner sign-off lines. Calm voice. No hype. Designed to be forwarded to a CPA or financial advisor without embarrassment.

Q: Is this tax advice?
A: No. The workbook flags tax-relevant items (Schedule E, COBRA, ACA marketplace) but does not advise. Bring the workbook to your CPA.

Q: Lite vs Full — which do I need?
A: Lite gets you the quit date and the current/target gap. Full gets you the path (acquisition schedule), the risk (healthcare bridge + partner buy-in), and the kitchen-table doc. If you have a partner, get Full.
```
