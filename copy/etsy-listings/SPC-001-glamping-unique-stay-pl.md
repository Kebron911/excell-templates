## Description

```
Yurts, A-frames, domes, treehouses. Off-grid economics tracked properly. Glamping / unique-stay P&L for operators of yurts, tiny homes, A-frames, treehouses, cave stays, domes, vintage trailers. Excel + Google Sheets.

A standard cabin P&L doesn't fit a glamping operation. You don't pay for
city utilities — you pay propane refills and generator fuel. You replace
linens 3x more often (outdoor wear). You take weather-related cancellations
the cabin doesn't see. You have alternative revenue lines (firewood sales,
hosted dinners, sauna access) the cabin doesn't have.

This workbook is the P&L tailored to the glamping reality: heavy seasonality,
off-grid utilities, frequent linen replacement, weather cancellations,
propane-tank tracking, alternative-revenue lines.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHAT'S INCLUDED
═══════════════════════════════════════════

📋 Property Snapshot — type (yurt / dome / tiny home / A-frame), capacity, season
📋 Off-Grid Utility Costs
   · Propane tank refills (winter heat)
   · Generator fuel
   · Water delivery (if any)
   · Septic / port-a-potty pump-outs
📋 Specialized Line Items
   · Linen replacement (more frequent than cabin — outdoor wear)
   · Weather-cancellation refunds
   · Heating alternatives (wood stove, propane heater)
📋 Bonus Revenue
   · Firewood sales
   · On-site experiences (hosted dinner, guided hike, sauna access)
   · Pet fees specific to glamping (often higher %)
📋 Heavy Seasonality Model — 60-80% revenue concentration in 4-6 peak months
📋 Off-Grid Cost as % of Revenue (typically 8-15% vs 3-6% for cabins)

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHO THIS IS FOR
═══════════════════════════════════════════

· Glamping operators (yurts, domes, A-frames, tiny homes)
· Treehouse / cave / vintage-trailer hosts
· Off-grid Hipcamp / GlampingHub operators
· Anyone whose listings are categorized as "Unique stays"

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHO THIS IS NOT FOR
═══════════════════════════════════════════

· Standard cabin / home rentals (use TAX-002 instead — same Schedule E framework, simpler costs)
· Hotel-style operators (different ops model)
· Long-term rentals (different tax + ops regime)
```
