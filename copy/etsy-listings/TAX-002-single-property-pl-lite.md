## Description

```
Close your year before April does.

Single-property P&L tracker for Airbnb and VRBO hosts, pre-mapped to IRS Schedule E.
Monthly + YTD rollups. Editable Excel.

Your CPA gave you the look last April — the one that says "these categories don't map
to Schedule E, I had to rebuild the whole thing." This template fixes that permanently.
Every expense category in the dropdown matches a Schedule E Part I line number. At
year-end, you print one tab and hand it over. Your CPA copies numbers into boxes.
That's it.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHAT'S INCLUDED (7 tabs)
═══════════════════════════════════════════

Tab 1 — Welcome
   · Schedule E mapping note + IRS Pub 527 reference
   · Step-by-step setup guide
   · Upgrade path to Multi-Property Master

Tab 2 — Property Info
   · Address, purchase date, purchase price, closing costs
   · Loan amount, interest rate, loan term
   · Business start date + days rented YTD

Tab 3 — Revenue Log (1000-row capacity)
   · One row per booking
   · Booking channel dropdown: Airbnb / VRBO / Booking.com / Direct / Other
   · Gross, Platform Fee, Cleaning Fee Collected, Net (auto-calculates)
   · IRS-correct: gross = Line 3; platform fee = expense Line 19

Tab 4 — Expense Log (2000-row capacity)
   · One row per expense
   · Category dropdown pre-mapped to all 17 Schedule E lines (Lines 6-20)
   · Payment method dropdown: Venmo / Zelle / Check / Cash / ACH / Credit Card / Auto-deduct
   · Receipt? (Yes/No) for audit trail

Tab 5 — Monthly P&L (auto-calculated)
   · 12-month × 17-category matrix — no manual entry
   · Green net income / red net loss conditional formatting
   · YTD column auto-sums January through December

Tab 6 — Schedule E Summary (print-ready CPA handoff)
   · Lines 3-26 pulled from Monthly P&L totals
   · Depreciation placeholder — enter your YTD figure directly
   · Print area set for 1-page letter portrait
   · Property name + tax year auto-populate from other tabs

Tab 7 — Settings
   · Tax year (auto-detects current year)
   · Category dropdown source list (editable if IRS adjusts)
   · IRS Publication 527 reference note

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHY THIS (not generic bookkeeping)
═══════════════════════════════════════════

Generic expense trackers use categories like "Cleaning" or "Utilities." The IRS doesn't
care. Schedule E has specific line numbers — and if your categories don't map, your CPA
remaps them manually, or worse, misses deductions entirely.

This template uses only Schedule E categories in the dropdown. Every dollar you log
lands on the right IRS line automatically.

Platform fees: not netted from revenue (the common mistake). They go in as an expense
on Line 19. Gross revenue stays on Line 3. The IRS wants gross — this tracker gives it
correctly out of the box.

═══════════════════════════════════════════
WHO IT'S FOR
═══════════════════════════════════════════

· Airbnb and VRBO hosts with 1-2 properties
· Hosts whose CPA asked for a Schedule E-ready summary and got a blank stare in return
· Side-by-side hosts who want clean books without QuickBooks overhead
· Semi-pro operators testing a single property before upgrading to multi-property

═══════════════════════════════════════════
HOW IT WORKS
═══════════════════════════════════════════

1. Setup (5 min): fill Property Info tab — address, purchase price, loan details
2. Revenue comes in: log one row per booking on Revenue Log (2 min per booking)
3. Expense hits: log one row on Expense Log, pick category from dropdown (1 min)
4. Monthly review: open Monthly P&L — green row = profit, red row = loss
5. Tax time: print Schedule E Summary tab, send PDF to CPA
```
