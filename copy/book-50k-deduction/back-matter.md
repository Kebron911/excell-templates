## Section 3 — Appendix C: Glossary

*Two columns. JetBrains Mono 10 pt term in Muted Gold; Inter 400 11 pt body in Graphite.*

**Adjusted basis** — A property's original cost basis plus capital improvements minus depreciation deducted (or allowable). Used to compute gain or loss on sale.

**Augusta Rule (§280A(g))** — The rule that excludes rental income from gross income when a dwelling unit is rented for fewer than 15 days in the year.

**BAR test** — The improvement test under §1.263(a)-3: betterment, adaptation, restoration. If any one applies, the expenditure must be capitalized.

**Basis (depreciable)** — The portion of a property's purchase price that is depreciable, usually purchase price plus capitalized acquisition costs minus the value of the land.

**Bonus depreciation (§168(k))** — A first-year deduction equal to a statutory percentage of the cost of qualifying property with a recovery period of 20 years or less.

**Capitalize** — To record an expenditure as an asset on the balance sheet rather than as a current-year expense, recovering the cost through depreciation over the asset's recovery period.

**Cohan rule** — A judicial doctrine permitting reasonable estimates of deductions when records are imperfect, *not* applicable to §274(d) categories.

**Cost segregation** — The practice of separating components of a real-estate purchase (furniture, fixtures, land improvements) onto faster MACRS depreciation schedules than the building itself.

**De minimis safe harbor (§1.263(a)-1(f))** — An election allowing tangible property under $2,500 per item or per invoice to be expensed regardless of useful life.

**Depreciation** — The annual deduction representing the wear-out of an asset over its useful life, computed under MACRS for most rental property.

**Dwelling unit** — Under §280A, a house, apartment, condominium, mobile home, or similar property used or available for use as a residence.

**§481(a) adjustment** — The cumulative catch-up adjustment taken in the current year when changing a method of accounting (e.g., to correct missed depreciation).

**Form 3115** — IRS application for change in accounting method. The form used to recover missed depreciation, apply cost segregation retroactively, or change other methods.

**Land improvement** — Improvements to land (driveways, decks-on-grade, landscaping, exterior lighting, fences) — depreciable on a 15-year MACRS line.

**Listed property** — Property defined in §280F as subject to additional substantiation. After TCJA (Pub. L. 115-97, §13202), substantially limited to passenger autos and certain entertainment property; computers and peripheral equipment were removed from listed-property treatment for property placed in service after 12/31/2017.

**Material participation** — Active involvement in a trade or business meeting one of the seven tests under §1.469-5T(a). Distinguishes passive from non-passive activity treatment.

**MACRS** — Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System — the standard tax depreciation system for property placed in service after 1986.

**Mid-month convention** — The depreciation convention applied to residential real property: the property is treated as placed in service or disposed of at the midpoint of the month.

**Non-passive activity** — An activity in which the taxpayer materially participates. Losses can offset other non-passive income, including W-2 wages.

**Ordinary and necessary** — The §162 standard for business deductions: ordinary (customary in the trade) and necessary (appropriate and helpful).

**Partial asset disposition** — The election under §1.168(i)-8(d) to write off the remaining basis of a replaced component (e.g., the old roof when a new one is installed).

**Passive activity** — An activity that is a rental or in which the taxpayer does not materially participate. Losses are limited under §469.

**Per diem** — A daily allowance for meals or lodging during business travel, used in lieu of actual receipts under Rev. Proc. 2019-48.

**Placed-in-service date** — The date a property is "ready and available" for its intended use; the start of the depreciation schedule.

**Principal place of business** — Under §280A(c)(1), the location where the taxpayer conducts the administrative or management activities of the trade or business, when there is no other fixed location for those activities.

**Real estate professional** — Under §469(c)(7), a taxpayer who meets the 750-hour and more-than-half-of-personal-services tests. REP status only deactivates the §469 per-se rental presumption — the taxpayer still has to materially participate in the specific rental activity (or aggregate under the §469(c)(7)(A) election) to take losses against ordinary income. "REP" is not a synonym for "automatic non-passive."

**Recovery period** — The number of years over which a depreciable asset's cost is recovered: 5, 7, 15, 27.5, or 39 years, depending on asset class.

**Routine maintenance safe harbor (§1.263(a)-3(i))** — An election allowing recurring maintenance to be expensed even if it would otherwise be a betterment or restoration.

**Schedule C** — IRS Form 1040 Schedule C, "Profit or Loss from Business" — for sole-proprietor trades and businesses; income is subject to self-employment tax.

**Schedule E** — IRS Form 1040 Schedule E, "Supplemental Income and Loss" — for rental real estate, royalties, partnerships, S corporations; income is not subject to self-employment tax.

**Section 179** — Annual election to expense up to a statutory cap of qualifying property purchased for use in an active trade or business.

**Self-employment tax (SE tax)** — The 12.4% Social Security and 2.9% Medicare tax on net earnings from self-employment, plus the 0.9% Additional Medicare on high earners.

**Small taxpayer safe harbor (§1.263(a)-3(h))** — An election allowing buildings with unadjusted basis ≤ $1M to expense improvements up to the lesser of $10,000 or 2% of basis annually.

**Standard mileage rate** — The IRS-published cents-per-mile rate that, applied to business miles, substitutes for actual vehicle expense tracking.

**Strict substantiation** — The §274(d) requirement that travel, meal, vehicle, and listed-property deductions be supported by contemporaneous records of amount, time, place, purpose, and (for meals) relationship.

**Substantial services** — Services "primarily for the convenience of the customer" of a type "not expected in connection with the rental of real property for occupancy as a residence." Substantial services trigger Schedule C treatment.

**Tax home** — The taxpayer's regular or principal place of business, regardless of personal residence. The reference point for "travel away from home" rules.

**Trade or business** — A regular and continuous activity entered into for profit. Distinct from a passive investment activity for §162 and §179 purposes.

**W-9** — IRS Form W-9, "Request for Taxpayer Identification Number and Certification" — collected from contractors before payment to enable 1099-NEC filing.

## Production notes for the print designer

- The Code Reference Card (Appendix A) and Workbook Map (Appendix B) are the two pull-out pages — set both as **two-page spreads** so the reader can fold the book open and see them in landscape.
- The Glossary (Appendix C) sets in two columns; aim to fit on a single recto page if possible (it's tight at ~30 entries).
- The "About the Publisher" page is the last text page before the inside back cover — give it generous whitespace; it should feel like a masthead.
- The Inside Back-Cover Insert is the highest-stakes page in the entire book for the funnel — treat the QR code as a brand element and verify scanning at the print proof stage.
- Page numbers begin on the foreword (roman numerals through front matter, Arabic from Chapter 1). Appendices revert to Arabic continuing the chapter sequence; the inside back-cover insert is intentionally unnumbered.
- All cited page numbers in the Workbook Map and other cross-references should be verified at final layout — placeholder counts during drafting will be wrong.
