## 4. Seven keyword slots

Each slot is a phrase. Amazon ranks on phrase-match, so 2–4 word strings outperform single words.

| # | Keyword | Why this slot |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `airbnb tax deductions` | The blog content plan's #1 keyword (~1,500–2,500/mo). Highest commercial-intent volume in the niche. Direct phrase match in the subtitle. |
| 2 | `short term rental taxes` | Captures the "STR" abbreviation crowd plus the "short-term-rental" longform crowd. Mid-volume, persona-perfect (no LTR landlord noise). |
| 3 | `airbnb schedule e` | Blog post #2 target. Lowest-competition tax keyword in the niche. The phrase a host types after their first failed TurboTax attempt. |
| 4 | `vacation rental tax guide` | Captures the VRBO half of the audience that doesn't self-identify as "Airbnb." "Tax guide" is the format match — Sarah is searching for a book, not a SaaS. |
| 5 | `airbnb host bookkeeping` | Steals SERP real estate from the QuickBooks tutorial corner of YouTube and Etsy. Nobody's written the book here yet — wide-open shelf space. |
| 6 | `str tax write offs` | The exact phrase Sarah's W2 partner types when he's curious what she's been doing all weekend. Long-tail, low-competition, conversational match. |
| 7 | `cost segregation airbnb` | The $8,427 anchor keyword. ~100–250/mo per the content plan but high-intent — anyone searching this is ready to buy. Almost no books rank for it. |

**Reasoning summary:** Slots 1–3 are the fat-head SEO plays. Slots 4–6 are persona-language captures (VRBO, partner-search, host-bookkeeping). Slot 7 is the anchor-figure hook — low volume, near-zero competing books, and aligns the listing with the book's hero case study.

## 8. Pre-launch checklist (KDP)

Work top to bottom. None of these can be skipped without cost.

- [ ] **Author Central account** for `The STR Ledger` registered and verified (§2 above)
- [ ] **USPTO TM quick-search** complete on "The STR Ledger" — already on brand-decisions §11 open-items list; close it before this book ships
- [ ] **ISBN** — accept the free KDP-issued ISBN for the **paperback** (Amazon imprint only — no Ingram distribution Year 1, which is fine for a tripwire). **Kindle does not require an ISBN** — KDP issues an ASIN automatically. If you anticipate ever distributing the paperback off-Amazon, buy a Bowker ISBN ($125) instead; otherwise free is correct
- [ ] **Audiobook (ACX) — skip Year 1.** Tripwire economics don't justify the $1,500–$3,000 narrator cost. Revisit at Q4 2026 if Kindle units > 500/mo
- [ ] **KDP Select enrollment — yes.** 90-day Amazon eBook exclusivity is fine because we're not selling Kindle elsewhere. Unlocks: 5 free-promo days per 90-day cycle, Kindle Countdown Deals, Kindle Unlimited page-read royalties (bonus revenue, no funnel cost)
- [ ] **Free promo schedule** — burn 2 of 5 free days in launch week (Day 3 + Day 4 to maximize "Hot New Releases" momentum). Hold the remaining 3 for tax-season Q1 push (one per week, weeks 10–12 of the cycle)
- [ ] **Pre-order vs. publish-direct** — **publish direct.** Pre-orders accumulate sales over a window but spread the rank velocity; publishing direct concentrates Day-1 velocity for category-rank achievement. Pre-order is the right call for fiction and audience books. This is a tripwire. Direct.
- [ ] **Back-of-book CTA QA** — confirm the `thestrledger.com/47-book` redirect exists, the email-gate form tags entries `source:book-buyer`, and the 21-day nurture variant fires. Print one paperback proof and click-test the QR code on a phone before approving
- [ ] **Reader discount code** — confirm `STRBOOK30` is provisioned in Influencersoft (or current cart), valid 90 days from email-entry timestamp, single-use, applies $30 off the $147 Tax Bundle
- [ ] **Print proof copy** — order a paperback proof through KDP, read out loud cover-to-back, check spine alignment, gutter margins, and the back-cover CTA legibility
- [ ] **Cover review** — confirm cover meets brand-decisions §3 (Cormorant Garamond display, Harbor Navy + Parchment + Muted Gold, monogram on the spine and back-cover bottom-right)
- [ ] **Description HTML** — paste the §3 block into the KDP description field; preview on desktop AND Amazon mobile app; verify `<h2>`, `<b>`, `<i>`, `<ul>`, `<li>` all render and the `<br>` after the "For:" line holds

- [ ] **Pricing** — Kindle $2.99 (qualifies for the 70% royalty tier — under $2.99 drops to 35%), paperback $9.99 (KDP min for the page count is roughly $4–$5; $9.99 leaves $2–$3 royalty after print cost, which is fine because royalty isn't the goal)
