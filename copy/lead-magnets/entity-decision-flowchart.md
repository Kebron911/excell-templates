## Why this magnet exists

Most STR hosts file the wrong schedule. Some file Schedule E when they should be filing Schedule C and miss the QBI deduction. Others file Schedule C when they should be on Schedule E and pay 15.3% SE tax they never owed. The decision turns on three rules — average rental period, substantial services, and material participation — that fit on one printable page if you arrange them right.

This magnet is that page.

It captures email at the top of the funnel (host has not necessarily bought the book; may have shared the link). The capture is tagged `source:entity-decision` so it can be sequenced separately from the buyer nurture in Phase 7. Until that dedicated sequence is built, captures drop onto the weekly Friday broadcast list with the tag preserved for retroactive segmentation.

Question text inside in Cormorant Medium 500, 22 pt, Graphite. Branch labels (`Yes` / `No`) in Inter 500, 14 pt, UPPERCASE, 0.10em tracking, Harbor Navy.

> What's your average guest stay (days rented ÷ number of rentals)?

Branches:

- **8–30 days** → side-branch to Box 2-A
- **30+ days** → side-branch to **Outcome A: Schedule E (passive long-term rental)**

**Box 2 — Question 2** *(only if Box 1 = ≤ 7 days)*
> Do you provide "substantial services" — concierge, daily housekeeping during stay, meals, transportation, sightseeing?

Branches:
- **Yes** → side-branch to **Outcome C: Schedule C (active trade or business)**
- **No** → continue down to Box 3

**Box 2-A — Question 2-A** *(only if Box 1 = 8–30 days)*
> Do you provide substantial services?

Branches:
- **Yes** → side-branch to **Outcome C: Schedule C**
- **No** → side-branch to **Outcome A: Schedule E (passive)**

**Box 3 — Question 3** *(only if Box 2 = No, average ≤ 7 days)*
> Do you materially participate in the rental activity? (At least one of the seven IRS material participation tests — see Page 3.)

Branches:
- **Yes** → side-branch to **Outcome B: Schedule E (non-passive)**
- **No** → side-branch to **Outcome A: Schedule E (passive)**

## Page 3 — Reference cards

Two cards, side by side. Same design language as the cover.

### Card 1 — "Substantial services" (left half)

**Header band:** Harbor Navy, 100 px tall, with white Cormorant Medium 32 pt headline:
> "Substantial services" — what the IRS means.

**Body (Inter 400, 14 pt, leading 22 pt, Graphite, on Parchment):**

> Per Treasury Reg §1.469-1T(e)(3)(ii)(A), "substantial services" are services *primarily for the convenience of the guest* and *of a type that would not be expected in a long-term rental.* The presence of substantial services pushes a rental out of Schedule E and into Schedule C — and into self-employment tax.

**Two columns below: "Counts as substantial" / "Does not count":**

| ✓ Counts | ✗ Does not |
|---|---|
| Daily housekeeping during the guest's stay | Cleaning between guest stays |
| Meal service or breakfast provision | Pre-stocked welcome basket |
| Concierge, sightseeing, or transportation | Local recommendations in a welcome book |
| Linen changes during stay | Linens provided at check-in |
| Personal greeting + tour of the property | Self check-in via smart lock |
| Tour-guide or chauffeur services | A printed neighborhood guide |
| Equipment rental staffed during stay | Bicycles or kayaks left for guest use |

**Footer note (italic, smaller, leading 18):**
> When in doubt, the question is: would a hotel concierge do this, or would a long-term landlord? If the former, it's substantial. If the latter, it isn't.

### Card 2 — "Material participation" (right half)

**Header band:** Muted Gold, 100 px, Graphite Cormorant Medium 32 pt headline:
> "Material participation" — the 7 IRS tests.

**Body intro (Inter 400, 14 pt):**
> You materially participate in an activity if you meet **any one** of the following seven tests in the tax year. Track hours contemporaneously — log them as you go, not from memory in March.

**Numbered list (Inter 500 numerals in Muted Gold, body Inter 400 in Graphite, 14 pt, leading 22 pt):**

1. **500-hour test.** You worked 500+ hours on the activity.
2. **Substantially-all test.** Your participation was substantially all of the participation by anyone (including non-owners) in the activity.
3. **100-hour, more-than-anyone test.** You worked 100+ hours AND more than any other individual.
4. **Significant participation test.** The activity was a significant participation activity (>100 hours) AND your aggregate significant participation across all such activities exceeded 500 hours.
5. **5-of-10 test.** You materially participated in any 5 of the prior 10 tax years.
6. **Personal service activity test.** *(Rare for STR — applies to personal service activities like law, medicine.)*
7. **Facts-and-circumstances test.** Continuous, substantial, regular involvement, with at least 100 hours. Hardest to defend; treat as a last resort.

**Footer note:**
> For STR hosts with average stay ≤ 7 days, meeting any one of these tests + the average-stay rule produces the **non-passive Schedule E** outcome — the so-called "STR loophole." Without material participation, you remain in passive Schedule E and your losses are §469-limited.

## Page 4 — Outcomes & next step

The page that converts the magnet into an action.

### Layout
- Top: Harbor Navy band, 220 px, with "**Now do this.**" — Cormorant Medium 64 pt, Parchment, gold period.
- Body: three outcome panels, vertically stacked, each ~700 px tall.

### Panel A — Schedule E (passive)

**Left third:** the Outcome A box from page 2, miniaturized.
**Center two-thirds:**
- Headline: "Schedule E. Passive activity rules apply." — Cormorant Medium 32 pt, Graphite
- Body (Inter 400, 16 pt, leading 24 pt):
  > File on Schedule E (Form 1040). Depreciate the building over 27.5 years. Losses are limited under §469 unless you qualify as a real estate professional under §469(c)(7). No self-employment tax. This is the default outcome for hosts whose average stay is over 7 days without substantial services, and for all 30+ day rentals.
- Action line (Inter 500, 14 pt, Muted Gold):
  > **Workbook:** *Schedule E Tax Prep (TAX-004)* — drop your numbers in, generate a per-property Schedule E. `thestrledger.com/cap/03`

### Panel B — Schedule E (non-passive)

Same structure, with the gold Outcome B box.

- Headline: "Schedule E. Non-passive activity. The STR play."
- Body:
  > File on Schedule E (Form 1040). The activity is reported on Schedule E *but is treated as non-passive* — losses can offset W-2 income, active business income, and other non-passive sources. No self-employment tax. The combination of average stay ≤ 7 days plus material participation is what produces this outcome. Document your hours.
- Action line:
  > **Workbook:** *Schedule E Tax Prep (TAX-004)* + the Material Participation Hour Log (Tab 6 inside TAX-004). `thestrledger.com/cap/03`

### Panel C — Schedule C

Clay Rose Outcome C box.

- Headline: "Schedule C. Active trade or business."
- Body:
  > File on Schedule C (Form 1040). Net income is subject to **self-employment tax (15.3% on the first ~$168,600 of net earnings, 2.9% above that, plus 0.9% Additional Medicare on high earners).** You may qualify for the qualified business income (QBI) deduction under §199A. Substantial services trigger this outcome regardless of average stay. Bed-and-breakfast operators, full-service vacation rentals with concierge, and hosts providing meals all land here.
- Action line:
  > **Workbook:** *Schedule C Workbook* — coming Q3 2026. For now, *Schedule E Tax Prep (TAX-004)* covers the income/expense capture; the schedule choice is a filing-form swap. `thestrledger.com/cap/03`
