## A/B test rationale

**Subject line variants tested in 10% holdouts before main send:**

- *"Twelve workbooks we built between tax seasons"* (primary) — leads with cadence and craft. Curious-but-calm, matches the editorial-finance frame. The "between tax seasons" phrase signals seasonality without urgency.
- *"The twelve gaps in our own portfolio (now closed)"* — first-person plural, problem-first, frames the launch as auto-dogfooding rather than a release. Strongest match to §6.1 "problem first, product second."
- *"New in the Ledger: twelve workbooks, five problems"* — magazine-issue framing ("New in the Ledger" reads like a column header). Tests whether brand-as-publication outperforms brand-as-shop.
- *"Twelve new workbooks — and the host-stage map for which one first"* — utility-forward. Tests whether the recommend-by-stage callout pulls more clicks than the announcement frame.

Pick the winner on opens at the 90-minute mark; full broadcast follows.

## Cluster framing rationale

Twelve SKUs as a flat list is unreadable on mobile and forces the reader to do their own taxonomy. Five clusters do three things:

1. **Echo the catalog architecture** — the same five domains the navigation uses (Acquisition, Operations, Revenue, Tax, Portfolio Planning). Familiarity signal.
2. **Map cleanly to host stage** — Acquisition is Year 1, Tax/Portfolio Accounting is Year 2-3, Portfolio Planning is the exit horizon. The "if you're stuck" callout cashes in on this.
3. **Let one cluster carry one tone** — Operations & Compliance is the "things go wrong" cluster, Revenue & Marketing is the "stop guessing" cluster, Portfolio Planning is the calm-authority cluster. Each cluster's intro sentence does one job.

Five-cluster grouping was chosen over four because Acquisition and Portfolio Planning serve different host stages — collapsing them blurs the host-stage recommendation that the callout depends on.
