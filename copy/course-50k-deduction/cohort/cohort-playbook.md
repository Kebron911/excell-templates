# Cohort Playbook — *The 47-Deduction Operator*

**Format:** 4-week cohort schedule + group call agendas + CPA panel script + facilitator notes
**Course module:** Cohort tier delivery (paid $997)
**Audience:** Up to 25 enrolled cohort students
**Run cadence:** Twice per year — typically January (pre-tax-season) and August (post-extension)

> **The cohort is what makes the $997 tier worth $997.** Self-Study delivers the same lessons; the cohort delivers accountability, real-time judgment, and the room. This playbook is the room's operating manual.

---

## Why a cohort

The Self-Study tier completes at a rate of ~22% (industry benchmark for self-paced courses). The cohort tier should complete at 70%+. The structural difference is:

- **Calendar-locked cadence** — students show up at the same time each week
- **Visible peer presence** — completing in front of others is harder to skip
- **Live judgment access** — the chance to ask "what would you do here" with your own facts on the table
- **Decision-grade momentum** — a 4-week timeline forces decisions that "I'll get to it" never makes

The cohort price is the price of that structure. Without it, students get the same content at a third the cost; with it, they're 3× more likely to finish.

## The 4-week structure

```
WEEK 1 — INSTALL THE FRAME              (calls + Modules 0–1)
WEEK 2 — INSTALL THE CAPTURE SYSTEM     (calls + Modules 2–3)
WEEK 3 — THE BIG LEVERS + CPA PANEL     (calls + Module 4 + panel)
WEEK 4 — AUDIT DEFENSE + HANDOFF        (calls + Modules 5–6 → graduation)
```

Module 7 (Year 2 & Scaling) is graduation homework — the cohort wraps before students need it, but they own the lessons for when they do.

## Week 1 — Install the Frame

### Lessons released

Modules 0 and 1 (8 lessons total, ~1h 15m of video).

### Pre-call homework (sent at enrollment)

```
Before our first call:
  1. Watch Modules 0 and 1 (1h 15m total).
  2. Run the Schedule E vs. C decision tree on YOUR portfolio.
  3. Set the four 2026 quarterly Saturdays on your calendar.
  4. Bring one question to the call. Any question.
```

### Group Call 1 — Agenda (60 min)

```
0:00–0:05   Welcome, intros (rapid — name + property count + state, that's it)
0:05–0:15   Walk through the cohort structure. Set Slack expectations.
0:15–0:35   Schedule E vs. C — round-robin verdicts.
              Each student says: my AVG rental period is X, my services are
              Y, my filing recommendation is Z. Daniel confirms or
              corrects. Total: ~30 seconds per student × 25 = 12 minutes.
0:35–0:50   The Recordkeeping Pact — three rules. Q&A on which is hardest
              for each student. Common answer: "Logging as I go." Address.
0:50–0:58   Set Week 2 expectations. Module 2 is the spine. Block 4 hours
              of focused work between now and Tuesday.
0:58–1:00   Close. Confirm next call same time next Tuesday.
```

### Slack rhythm — Week 1

- Daniel posts Mon AM: weekly thread "What you'll cover this week"
- Daniel posts Wed PM: 10-min FAQ video answering Slack questions from Mon–Wed
- Students post 1 win + 1 stuck in their `#wins-and-stucks` thread by Sunday

### Facilitator notes

- The first call sets the temperature for the cohort. Calm authority, specific dollar figures, no jokes about "boring tax stuff." If you make tax sound dry on Day 1, the cohort believes you for 4 weeks.
- Students will arrive nervous. Normalize the nerves — most of them have never taken a tax course before.
- The round-robin Schedule E/C check is the most important block of Call 1. It immediately demonstrates that the cohort gets *individualized* attention, not a webinar. Don't shortcut it.

## Week 2 — Install the Capture System

### Lessons released

Modules 2 and 3 (20 lessons, ~3h 45m).

### Pre-call homework

```
Before Call 2:
  1. Watch Module 2 in full (8 lessons).
  2. Run your first 21-minute Saturday on ONE property. Time yourself.
  3. Post your time + the property to Slack `#first-saturdays`.
  4. Watch Module 3 lessons 3.1, 3.3, 3.4. (Skip 3.5–3.12 for now.)
  5. Bring the trickiest receipt from your last quarter to the call.
```

### Group Call 2 — Agenda (60 min)

```
0:00–0:05   Quick wins from Slack. Read 3 from `#first-saturdays`.
0:05–0:25   Live receipt clinic. 5 students bring their trickiest receipt.
              Daniel runs the BAR test or §263(a) analysis live. Everyone
              else watches the reasoning. (Most students learn more from
              watching others' receipts than working their own.)
0:25–0:45   Cleaning fee gross/net. Round-robin: did anyone find that they
              were netting? Walk through fix.
0:45–0:55   Open Q&A. First-Saturday timing complaints addressed: most
              students take 2–3 hours on first run; that's normal.
0:55–1:00   Close. Set up Week 3: Module 4 + the CPA panel.
```

### Slack rhythm — Week 2

- Live BAR-test thread for Wed–Sun: students post receipts, peers and Daniel weigh in
- Daniel posts a Module 2 cheatsheet PDF as pinned message
- Friday: post the Week 2 stuck-list summary (anonymized)

### Facilitator notes

- Week 2 is where the cohort either commits or drifts. The first-Saturday assignment is the single most important assignment in the course; if students don't run it, they won't run it the rest of the year.
- The live receipt clinic is high-trust theater. Don't preview the receipts before the call — the value is in working them in real time.
- Some students will not have run the first Saturday. Acknowledge gently, normalize, set expectation that they run it before Call 3. Don't shame.

## Week 3 — The Big Levers + CPA Panel

### Lessons released

Module 4 (6 lessons, ~1h 30m).

### Pre-call homework

```
Before Call 3:
  1. Watch Module 4.
  2. Run the cost-seg pencil-test on your most expensive property.
  3. Run the Augusta self-check: does it pencil for your situation?
  4. Submit ONE question for the CPA panel via the Slack form by Friday.
```

### Group Call 3 — Agenda (60 min)

```
0:00–0:05   Wins from Week 2 + Module 2 completion check.
0:05–0:25   Cost seg round-table. Each student who ran the pencil test:
              "My basis is X, my marginal rate is Y, the math says Z."
              Daniel confirms direction. Identify the 2–3 students for
              whom cost seg is most likely to pencil; they get the
              CPA-panel priority slot.
0:25–0:45   Augusta self-check. Round-robin. Identify students for whom
              Augusta is a fit (entity exists, real meetings happen,
              FMV defensible).
0:45–0:55   STR loophole — material participation logging refresher.
              Surface students whose hour count is tight.
0:55–1:00   Reminder: CPA panel Thursday 7 PM Eastern. Ask any question
              that's still on your mind in `#panel-questions` thread.
```

### CPA Panel — Agenda (90 min, separate Thursday call)

```
0:00–0:05   Daniel opens. Introduces the 3 panelists (CPA names + STR
              client count + state of practice).
0:05–0:15   Each CPA's 3-minute warm-up: "Most common STR mistake I see."
0:15–1:00   Cohort questions. Daniel curates from `#panel-questions`,
              prioritizing:
                - Cost seg (the highest-leverage Q most years)
                - STR loophole / material participation
                - Augusta election
                - 1099-NEC late filings
                - Personal-use threshold
              Round-robin format: each Q goes to all 3 CPAs for a brief
              answer. Different CPAs sometimes disagree — let them. The
              disagreement teaches more than consensus.
1:00–1:25   Live cohort Q&A — students who didn't submit can raise a
              hand. CPAs answer the smaller ones in 1–2 min each.
1:25–1:30   Close. Daniel thanks panelists. Mentions Directory listing.
              Reminds students the panel is recorded and replays land
              in 48 hours.
```

### Facilitator notes — CPA panel

- The panel is the highlight of the cohort experience for many students. Hire it.
- Pick CPAs from the Directory who score 30+ on the scorecard *and* who interview well live (some great CPAs are bad on panels).
- Disagreement among panelists is gold. If everyone agrees on every Q, the panel was over-curated.
- Have a backup question stack of 5–8 ready in case Slack submissions are thin.
- Always end with a panelist sentence each on "what's the one thing you'd want every cohort student to remember." Memorable closer.

## Week 4 — Audit Defense + Handoff

### Lessons released

Modules 5 and 6 (10 lessons, ~1h 50m).

### Pre-call homework

```
Before Call 4:
  1. Watch Module 5 in full.
  2. Run the Tabletop Drill (Bonus B2). Score yourself. Post score (or
     anonymously via the form) to Slack.
  3. Watch Module 6.
  4. Build the shell of your CPA Handoff Folder. Don't fill it yet —
     just create the 12 subfolders.
  5. If you don't have a CPA, run the 12-question scorecard against
     ONE candidate from the Directory before the call.
```

### Group Call 4 — Agenda (60 min)

```
0:00–0:05   Recap Week 3 wins + CPA panel highlights.
0:05–0:20   Tabletop Drill scores — anonymous histogram on screen.
              Common gaps: §274(d) substantiation (mileage logs),
              cost-seg studies, Augusta documentation. Address each.
0:20–0:35   CPA selection round-robin. Students who interviewed a CPA:
              what was their score, what did you learn? Students
              still searching: what state, what specialization?
              Cohort warm-intro courtesy offered to one student per
              cohort if a Directory match exists.
0:35–0:50   Module 7 preview — what the year-2 system looks like.
              Reset expectations: the next year is maintenance, not
              installation. Aim for 25+ on the Year-End Health Check
              by December.
0:50–0:58   Graduation. Certificate issued in LMS. Slack channel stays
              open lifetime. Quarterly office hours reminder.
0:58–1:00   Close. The cohort is over; the system is ongoing.
```

### Post-call rituals

- **Certificate of Completion** issued via LMS — branded PDF, signed by Daniel, with the student's name and "47-Deduction Operator" credential
- **Mailing address request** for cohort buyers who didn't already provide one — wall poster mails in a tube within 7 days
- **NPS survey** sent 24 hours after Call 4 — single question: *"Would you recommend the cohort to another STR host? (0–10)"* + optional comment
- **30-day check-in** automated email — *"How's your Q1 going?"* — sent 30 days post-graduation
- **90-day check-in** — *"Year-end is in 6 weeks. Anything we can answer?"*

### Facilitator notes

- The graduation moment matters. Don't rush past it. The certificate, the wall poster, the lifetime Slack — these are the rituals that make the cohort feel like an alumni network, not a course transaction.
- The NPS at 24 hours is more honest than at 30 days; capture it before the post-cohort glow fades.
- Quarterly office hours are real obligations. Block them on the calendar at cohort start, not after.

## Quarterly office hours (lifetime, post-cohort)

### Cadence

One 60-min Zoom per quarter, scheduled on the last Saturday of the quarter (mirroring the seven-minute tax tab cadence). Held year-round for cohort alumni from any prior cohort.

### Format

```
0:00–0:05   Welcome. Note the calendar — current quarter ending.
0:05–0:15   "What changed this quarter" — IRS guidance updates, mileage
              rate changes (if mid-year), notable court cases.
0:15–0:50   Open Q&A. Submitted questions go first; live Qs after.
0:50–1:00   Reminder of next quarter's date. Slack reminder follow-up.
```

### Facilitator notes

- Office hours are the longest-tail cohort benefit. Some alumni will use them every quarter for years. Others will never come. Both are fine.
- Don't over-curate. Office hours are for the questions that arise from running the system, not for fresh teaching.
- Co-host with a CPA panelist 2× per year so alumni get rotating perspectives.

## Slack workspace — channel design

```
#announcements              — Daniel posts only; pins for the cohort week
#wins-and-stucks            — students post 1 win + 1 stuck weekly
#first-saturdays            — Module 2 first-run timings + lessons
#receipts-clinic            — bring tricky receipts for live BAR analysis
#cost-seg-decisions         — Week 3 cost-seg pencil-test discussions
#augusta                    — §280A(g) elections being considered
#cpa-search                 — students sharing CPA interview notes
#panel-questions            — submission thread for CPA panel
#audit-dossier              — Week 4 dossier-building threads
#alumni-graduates           — opens at graduation, lifetime access
#announcements-alumni       — Daniel-only, post quarterly office hours
```

### Slack rules

1. **No direct DMs to Daniel** — questions go in public channels so others learn from the answer
2. **No cross-promotion** of unrelated products / coaches / services — cohort is paid space
3. **No "send me your contact info" requests** — student-to-student introductions are fine via thread; bulk solicitation is not
4. **No specific tax advice without disclaimer** — peer help is welcome; framing matters
5. **Be specific** — "I had a $4,200 receipt for X — thoughts?" outperforms "advice on receipts?"

## Pricing economics

The cohort tier has more delivery cost than Self-Study. Worth tracking:

| Cost | Estimate per cohort |
|---|---|
| Daniel time — 4 calls × 1h + prep + Slack + emails | ~24 hours |
| CPA panel honoraria (3 × $0–$300; pro-bono with Directory listing exchange) | $0–$900 |
| Wall poster fulfillment (25 × ~$18) | $450 |
| LMS / Slack incremental cost | ~$50 |
| Zoom Pro | $0 (existing) |
| **Total per cohort** | **~$500–$1,400 + Daniel time** |

Revenue at 25 students × $997 = **$24,925** per cohort. Margin is healthy at any reasonable hourly value of Daniel's time.

Two cohorts per year: ~$50K revenue against ~$2,000 hard cost + ~50 Daniel hours.

## Risk management

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Disruptive student | Slack rules clear; private warning, then refund + remove if pattern continues |
| CPA panelist no-show | Backup panelist pre-arranged; pre-brief 5 days out; tech check 1 day out |
| Daniel sick on call day | Reschedule (preferred) or send 30-min recorded substitute lesson + open async Q&A |
| Cohort under-fills (<10 enrolled) | Run anyway with the smaller cohort *or* refund and combine into next cohort (refund is the brand-aligned move) |
| Cohort over-asks DWY upgrade | Cap DWY upgrade offers per cohort to preserve the tier's exclusivity |
| Module 2 first-Saturday compliance low | Send personal Slack DM to non-completers; the Module 2 assignment is the most important; protect it |

## Continuous improvement

After each cohort:

- Write a 1-page debrief (what worked, what didn't, what to try next)
- Update this playbook with any new patterns
- Review NPS comments for product improvements (course or cohort)
- Cross-cohort metrics: completion rate, NPS, time to first 21-min Saturday, time to first audit dossier shell

The cohort is the highest-bandwidth feedback channel the brand has. Use it.

---

## What this playbook is not

- Not a script — agendas are scaffolding, not lines to read
- Not exhaustive — emergent topics from the cohort take priority over the planned agenda
- Not for cold delivery — the playbook assumes Daniel (or a similarly experienced facilitator) is running the calls; rotating in a stand-in requires extra prep
- Not legal or tax advice — cohort calls follow the same disclaimer cadence as the lessons
