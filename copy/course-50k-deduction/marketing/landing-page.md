## Section 7 — What this course is not

*(Cormorant Italic 18pt headline. Five short bullets in Inter 400.)*

### *Five things this course will not do.*

- **Not a CPA replacement.** Every module ends with the same disclaimer the book uses: general information, not tax advice, consult a qualified pro before filing.
- **Not a recorded reading of the book.** 60% of run-time is screen-share and worked example, not voice-over slides.
- **Not a tax-season-only course.** The capture system runs year-round. Most of the value is what happens between filings, not during them.
- **Not a beginner's intro to STR investing.** Assumes one or more properties operating today. If you're pre-acquisition, finish the book first; come back when you're operational.
- **Not a community of cheerleaders.** The community is honest about gaps. The brand voice is calm, specific, and rigorous. If you want hype, this isn't the room.

## Section 10 — FAQ

*(Eight questions. The eight a host actually asks before buying. Each answer 2-4 sentences in the same brand register.)*

**Q1 — How is this different from the book?**
The book teaches the rules. The course teaches the judgment and installs the system. The book is reference; the course is apprenticeship. Both are useful; they don't substitute for each other.

**Q2 — How long does it take to complete?**
Core video is roughly 9 hours. Most students watch 30–45 minutes per evening across 3–4 weeks. The actual system installation — Module 2 — takes a single Saturday and recovers the time across the year.

**Q3 — Will this work if I'm on Schedule C, not Schedule E?**
Module 1 walks through the Schedule E vs. Schedule C decision; if you're filing on Schedule C and shouldn't be, the course catches it. If you genuinely belong on Schedule C (substantial services, hospitality-style operation), most of the system still applies — line numbers shift, but the capture discipline, the BAR test, the CPA handoff, and the audit dossier are the same.

**Q4 — Do I need Excel? Can I use Notion or Airtable?**
The primary workbooks are Excel. The course also ships a Notion mirror and an Airtable mirror so hosts who don't live in Excel can run the same logic on their preferred surface. Pick one; the system works on any of the three.

**Q5 — Is the cohort capped?**
Yes. 25 students per cohort. Two cohorts per year. The cap is genuine — group calls don't work above 25 — and the waitlist for the next cohort starts as soon as the current one fills.

**Q6 — What's the refund policy?**
14-day money-back on Self-Study and Cohort. No survey, no negotiation. Done-With-You is non-refundable after the first 1:1 session because the bulk of the cost is the customized work that's already underway.

**Q7 — Will the course be updated when tax law changes?**
Yes. Every January, a 30-minute "What Changed" update video ships to all enrolled students. Lessons impacted by material code changes are re-shot or annotated. Lifetime updates are part of every tier.

**Q8 — I'm not a U.S. host. Will this work for me?**
The course is built around U.S. federal tax law (Internal Revenue Code). Hosts in other countries can still benefit from the capture system and recordkeeping discipline, but the deduction list, code citations, and CPA Directory are U.S.-specific. If you file a U.S. return on U.S. property as a foreign host, the course applies.
