# Pinterest Pin Catalog — *The 47-Deduction Operator* Launch

**Status:** Draft v0.1 — 2026-04-29
**Purpose:** 30 launch-window pins driving organic Pinterest traffic to the course landing page
**Schedule:** 1 pin/day for 30 days starting Day 0 of public launch; native Pinterest scheduler with Creasquare backup
**Linked from:** `00-course-plan.md` §6.2 (Day 1–3 launch tasks); pairs with the existing book/blog pin catalog at `copy/pinterest/pin-catalog-first-30.md`

> **Pinterest is the brand's slowest-compounding traffic channel and the most durable.** A pin that ranks lands traffic for 18+ months. The course launch deserves its own 30-pin push, designed to ride the existing book pins' momentum without cannibalizing them.

---

## Why a separate course pin catalog

The book/blog pin catalog (already published) drives `/47` lead-magnet traffic and Schedule E / depreciation product pages. Those pins are working.

This catalog adds course-specific pins that:

1. Land on the course landing page rather than the lead magnet
2. Speak to a more committed reader (someone evaluating a $497–$2,997 purchase, not a free PDF)
3. Lean harder into outcomes ("21 minutes a quarter" / "Audit-ready") and less into discovery ("47 deductions you didn't know")
4. Build a second SEO surface for the same keyword family without duplicate-content risk

Pin styles, dimensions, and brand spec inherit from `copy/pinterest/pin-catalog-first-30.md` §design-spec — same Vista Create master, same color palette, same logo treatment.

Decision threshold: 95% confidence on a 30%+ lift in click-through rate over 30 days of equal impression delivery.

## How this catalog interacts with the existing book/blog catalog

The book/blog catalog (`copy/pinterest/pin-catalog-first-30.md`) keeps running on its own cadence. It drives free-PDF traffic; this catalog drives paid-course traffic. The two address different intent levels — early-funnel discovery vs. late-funnel commitment.

Cross-pinning suppressed for the first 60 days; after that, cross-pinning the highest-performing pins to a second board is allowed if Pinterest's algorithm has already ranked them.

No pin description references both the book AND the course on the same pin — that splits intent and tanks click-through. Each pin commits to one CTA.
