# CPA Handoff Folder Template

**Format:** Folder structure + cover letter + index + this design spec
**Course module:** Module 6 (Lesson 6.1)
**Use case:** What you send your CPA in February so March is preparation, not archaeology

> **Your CPA's job is to file. Your job is to hand them a complete file.** This template is the file. Sent on the same date every year, organized in the same structure every year, with the same cover letter pointing the CPA to the items that need their judgment.

---

## Why a handoff folder (not just "send the books")

Most STR hosts email their CPA a workbook on a random February evening with the subject line "for the return." The CPA opens it, finds questions, emails back, waits, the host responds late, the cycle repeats. A two-week filing turns into eight weeks.

The handoff folder is the alternative. One delivery, organized, complete, with a cover letter that tells the CPA exactly which items need judgment and which are settled. The CPA spends time on tax decisions, not on Q&A. The host's filing closes in the first pass.

Hosts who send a handoff folder:
- File by mid-March on average
- Pay 20–30% less in CPA fees (less back-and-forth = less hourly time)
- Get more proactive planning suggestions (the CPA has bandwidth left over)

---

## Delivery convention

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Date sent** | February 15 — the same date every year |
| **Delivery method** | Cloud-share link (Dropbox, OneDrive, Google Drive) — pick one and stay |
| **Format** | Read-only access for the CPA; comment/edit access on a single review file |
| **Notification** | One email with the cover letter as the body and the share link |
| **Follow-up** | Single 30-min call scheduled at handoff for "questions after first pass" — usually 7–10 days later |

The fixed February 15 date matters. CPAs prioritize clients whose materials arrive early and complete. A reliable February 15 handoff makes you a top-of-stack client.

## The question list — `01-the-question-list.pdf`

The single document that drives the CPA conversation. Each question has the same structure:

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
QUESTION 1 — [HEADLINE, e.g., "Cabin C HVAC: §179 vs. capitalize"]
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

THE FACTS
  HVAC unit on Cabin C failed July 14. Replaced with like-capacity
  unit. Total cost (unit + install): $9,400. Vendor invoice in folder
  04/placed-in-service-log.xlsx, row 12.

  Cabin C average rental period (2026): 4.2 nights. Treated as
  nonresidential rental property for MACRS purposes per §168(e).

WHAT I PROPOSE
  §179 election in full ($9,400). Reasoning: HVAC on nonresidential
  rental qualifies as §179 property under §179(d)(1)(A) (post-2017
  PATH Act extension). Cabin C generates positive income for the
  year ($14,400 net before this election), so the §179 cap is not
  triggered.

WHAT I'D LIKE YOU TO CONFIRM
  1. Agreement that Cabin C qualifies as nonresidential under §168(e)
     given its <7-day average rental period.
  2. Agreement that the HVAC qualifies as §179 property in this
     classification.
  3. Whether you'd recommend §179 vs. §168(k) bonus given the
     stacking with the cost seg election (Question 4 below).

ALTERNATIVE TREATMENT
  Capitalize over 27.5 years if Cabin C is treated as residential.
  Year-1 deduction in that case would be ~$170 instead of $9,400.

PRIORITY: HIGH

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
QUESTION 2 — ...
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

### Why this format works

- **Facts first.** The CPA gets the situation without scrolling.
- **Proposed treatment.** The CPA sees what the host already thinks. They confirm or override — they don't have to start from zero.
- **Specific asks.** "Confirm 1, 2, 3" is faster than "tell me what to do."
- **Alternative treatment.** Shows the host considered the other path. CPA doesn't have to suggest it.
- **Priority.** High / Medium / Low — lets the CPA batch low-priority items.

A handoff with a thoughtful question-list reduces CPA hours by 30–50%. The host has done the framing; the CPA does the judging.

## Common questions to include *(prompt list, populate as applicable)*

A starter library. Most years, 5–10 of these apply.

### Capital & depreciation
- New property placed in service — basis allocation, cost seg election
- Like-kind component replacement — §263(a) BAR test result
- §179 vs. §168(k) bonus on a specific asset
- Disposition of a partially-depreciated asset

### Schedule E vs. C
- Operational change that may shift Schedule E vs. C classification
- New property with different average rental period

### §469 / loophole
- Material participation hour count for a year that's tighter than usual
- New properties affecting the aggregation election
- Real-estate-professional status — qualifying year-by-year question

### Personal use
- Property approaching the §280A(d)(2) threshold
- Augusta election — first time, or rate change

### Special situations
- Property acquired through inheritance
- Property sold during the year — recapture, §1250 unrecaptured gain
- Casualty loss
- Refinance — points, prepayment penalty
- 1031 exchange contemplated for next year

### State / multi-state
- New property in a different state
- Hosts living in a state different from property locations
- State conformity question on §168(k) bonus

### Reasonable comp / S-corp
- W-2 reasonable compensation level for the year
- New employee added
- Benefits / retirement plan questions

## What the CPA does back

After receiving the handoff, a well-prepared CPA returns:

1. **Confirmation of receipt** within 24 hours
2. **First-pass questions list** within 5–7 business days — typically smaller than the host expects because the question-list answered most
3. **Draft return** within 14 days of resolving the question-list
4. **6-line review file** with the host's draft for the lines the host should verify (per Lesson 6.4)
5. **Signed return** within 5 business days of host's review approval
6. **Post-filing debrief** — a 15–30 minute call within 30 days of filing reviewing what to set up differently for next year (per Lesson 6.5)

If the CPA isn't producing this cadence, the engagement letter or the CPA needs adjustment. The CPA Interview Scorecard (Bonus) is the screening tool; the handoff folder is the working tool; the cadence above is the test of whether the engagement is functioning.

---

## Annual refinement

The handoff folder should improve every year. After each filing, capture:

- What questions did the CPA add that weren't in the question-list? *(Add to next year's prompt library.)*
- What folders did the CPA never open? *(Consider thinning next year.)*
- What folders did the CPA ask for that weren't included? *(Add for next year.)*
- What time did the engagement actually take? *(Trend year-over-year.)*

The post-filing debrief (Lesson 6.5) is the venue for this conversation.

## What this template is not

- Not a substitute for a CPA's preparation work — the CPA still files the return
- Not a guarantee of fast filing — a slow CPA is slow regardless of how clean the handoff is
- Not appropriate for first-year hosts who don't yet have a clean Year-N–1 return for context
- Not a unilateral document — the CPA's preferences override; if the CPA wants a different folder structure, conform to theirs

---

## When to push back on the CPA

If the CPA pushes back on the handoff format — e.g., wants everything dumped into QuickBooks Online, or insists on a specific intake form — accommodate up to a point. But:

- A CPA who *won't accept* organized files is signaling a workflow that may not adapt to your portfolio's complexity. Use the CPA Interview Scorecard at next renewal.
- A CPA who *prefers* a different structure but will work with yours is fine.
- A CPA who *demands* the host re-do bookkeeping in their preferred software *and* charges for the re-do is double-charging. Push back.

The handoff folder is a host's tool to keep CPA fees in line with the work that actually needs the CPA's judgment, not their typing time.
