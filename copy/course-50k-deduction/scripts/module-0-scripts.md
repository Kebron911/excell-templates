## Lesson 0.1 — What you're about to install

**Length:** 6 min
**Format:** Title card → VO over keynote slides → close

```
[OPEN — title card: "0.1 — What You're About to Install"]

[VO]

The book taught you the rules. This course installs the system. Different
skill.

The hosts who buy this course tend to be hosts who already finished
*The $50,000 Deduction*. They marked the chapters. They highlighted
Amanda's seven-minute tax tab. They wrote down the IRS code sections that
mattered.

And in eighty percent of cases, they put the book on the shelf and did
not, in fact, sit down for their first twenty-one-minute Saturday.

That gap — between knowing the rules and running the system — is what
this course exists to close.

[FRAME — 30s]

In this lesson we're going to walk through three things.

One. What this course will install on your portfolio.

Two. What it explicitly will NOT do.

Three. The order to watch the modules in, and the four-property promise.

After that, lessons 0.2 and 0.3 are operational — a tour of the workbook
bundle and a calendar exercise. Total time for Module 0 is twenty-five
minutes. Then you put it down for the day.

[TEACH — 4 min]

What this course installs.

Module 1 is the frame — Schedule E versus Schedule C, substantial
services, the recordkeeping pact. Five lessons.

Module 2 is the capture system — the spine of the entire course. Eight
lessons that walk through every step of the seven-minute tax tab. If
you finish only Module 2 and stop there, you have already installed
the asset most hosts spent five years not building.

Module 3 is the forty-seven deductions in workshop format. Twelve
lessons, each one running a worked example end-to-end.

Module 4 is the big levers — cost segregation, the STR loophole, the
Augusta Rule. Six lessons on the four asymmetric deductions where most
of the dollars live.

Module 5 is audit defense. Five lessons on building the dossier.

Module 6 is the CPA handoff. Five lessons on what to send your tax
preparer in February so March is preparation, not archaeology.

Module 7 is year two and scaling. Five lessons on what changes when
you graduate from the year-one installation.

What the course will NOT do.

It will not file your taxes for you. Every module ends with the same
disclaimer the book uses — general information, not tax advice, consult
a qualified pro before filing.

It will not catch a fraudulent return. The course is built to recover
deductions you legitimately earned. It is not a vehicle for positions
that don't survive examination.

It will not work for hosts who haven't acquired a property yet. The
course assumes one or more doors operating today.

[DEMONSTRATE — 1 min]

Open your LMS dashboard. You'll see seven modules in the left sidebar

files we promised on the landing page.

We unlock modules in order across the first fourteen days. Module 0 is
open now. Modules 1 and 2 unlock in the next thirty-six hours. Modules
3, 4, 5, 6, 7 unlock weekly thereafter.

If you're in the cohort tier, your first call lands in seven days. The
calendar invite is already in your inbox.

[ASSIGNMENT — 30s]

One thing before you close the LMS for today: download the workbook

Course — Workbook Bundle." About forty megabytes, ninety files,

Download it now. Put it somewhere you'll find it. Don't open the files;
that's the next lesson.
```

## Lesson 0.3 — Set your quarterly cadence

**Length:** 7 min
**Format:** Title card → VO → live calendar demonstration → close

```
[OPEN — title card: "0.3 — Set Your Quarterly Cadence"]

[VO]

If you don't put four Saturdays on the calendar before the next lesson,
you will not run this system.

That's not a guess. That's the pattern across every cohort we've run.
Hosts who block the four Saturdays at the start of the year run them.
Hosts who plan to block them later don't.

This lesson is the most important assignment in the course. It takes
two minutes. Don't skip it.

[FRAME — 30s]

In this lesson:

One. Why quarterly beats monthly and beats annual.

Two. Which four Saturdays you should block.

Three. The actual calendar event we're putting on your calendar right
now, together.

[TEACH — 3 min]

Why quarterly.

Annual bookkeeping is archaeology. By the time April arrives, you're
reconstructing nine-month-old transactions from credit-card statements
and memory. The math takes hours per property. The substantiation is
weak. §6001 contemporaneous principles fail.

Monthly bookkeeping is overkill. Most STR portfolios produce
twenty-five to seventy transactions per month. Sitting down twelve
times a year for ninety minutes each is a worse use of time than
sitting down four times a year for twenty-one minutes each.

Quarterly is the discipline. Ninety-day blocks are short enough that
you remember every transaction's context — what the receipt was for,
why the trip happened, who the cleaner was that week — and long enough
that the session has enough volume to justify sitting down.

The IRS doesn't require quarterly bookkeeping. The auditors prefer it.
Hosts who survive examinations cleanly almost universally run quarterly.

The four dates.

Last Saturday of each quarter, or the closest weekend if travel or life
intervenes:

  March 28
  June 27
  September 26
  December 26

These are the dates for next year. Adjust the year if you're starting
mid-year. The pattern is the same: the last Saturday of March, June,
September, and December.

[DEMONSTRATE — 2 min]

Open Google Calendar, Outlook, Apple Calendar — whichever one runs
your week.

I'm going to set up the four events right now while you watch. Pause
the video and do it with me if you'd like.

Click "create event." Title: "Quarterly Tax Tab — Q1 close." Date:
March 28. Time: 9 AM to 11 AM, default. Notes field: "Bank/card CSV
imports. Categorize. Receipt photos. Mileage review. Cleaner
reconciliation. 1099 tracking. Backup. ~21 minutes per property."

Save.

Repeat for Q2, Q3, Q4 with the corresponding dates.

You should now see four calendar events spaced roughly ninety days
apart. Confirm they made it.

If your calendar app supports recurring events, you can set this up as
a single recurring event with a custom recurrence — but I prefer the
four discrete events because each one becomes a checkbox you actively
complete. Recurring events disappear into the routine.

[ASSIGNMENT — 30s]

That's the assignment.

Two minutes ago, this didn't exist. Now it does. Four Saturdays, on the
calendar, for the next year. The course's promise — installing the
seven-minute tax tab — runs through these four dates.

If you didn't actually do it: pause the video, do it now, then come
back.

When you have the four events on your calendar, that's the end of
Module 0. Module 1 unlocks Wednesday morning.

Set down the LMS for today. We'll see you in two days.

[ON-SCREEN DISCLAIMER CARD — 10s]

[OUT]
```
