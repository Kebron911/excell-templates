# Bonus — The Year-End Portfolio Health Check

**Format:** 4-page printable PDF + matching Excel scorecard
**Course module:** Module 7 (Lesson 7.4)
**Use case:** December 28 half-day review that sets up next April

> **December 28 is the most useful day of the tax year. Most hosts waste it.**
>
> Block half a day. Run the ten-question scorecard. Identify the two or three things you can still fix before December 31 — the tax year closes that night, and decisions that were available on the 30th are not available on the 1st.

---

## How to use this

1. **Block 4 hours.** Solo work, no calls, no guests, the workbook open.
2. **Walk through all ten questions in order.** Score each 0, 1, 2, or 3 per the rubric.
3. **Total at the end.** The total is your portfolio's tax-health score for the year.
4. **Identify the two or three "before December 31" actions.** Some questions surface decisions that lapse at year-end. Take them now or lose them.
5. **Identify the "before April 15" actions.** The remainder feed the CPA handoff (Module 6).
6. **Save the scorecard.** Compare year over year. The trend matters more than the score.

## The 10 questions

### Question 1 — Quarterly cadence

*Did you run the seven-minute tax tab on the last Saturday of every quarter this year?*

| Score | Pattern |
|---|---|
| 3 | All four quarters closed on schedule, before the next quarter began |
| 2 | Three of four — one quarter slipped but caught up within 30 days |
| 1 | Two of four, or all four delayed |
| 0 | Did not run the cadence |

**Why this question is first.** Every other answer is downstream of this one. A 3 here makes the rest of the scorecard a formality. A 0 means the rest is reconstruction.

**Action if 0–1:** Block the four 2027 Saturdays on the calendar before you finish this scorecard. Lesson 0.3.

---

### Question 2 — Bookkeeping completeness

*Are all transactions for the year categorized in TAX-002 / TAX-004, with no uncategorized rows in any property tab?*

| Score | Pattern |
|---|---|
| 3 | Zero uncategorized rows. Every transaction routed to a Schedule E line. |
| 2 | <5 uncategorized rows, all flagged for resolution |
| 1 | 5–20 uncategorized rows, scattered |
| 0 | >20 uncategorized, or whole quarters un-imported |

**Why this matters.** Uncategorized rows are decisions you haven't made. By December 28, every transaction has a home or a flag.

**Action if 0–2:** Sweep the workbook today. Categorize what you can; flag the unclear ones for the CPA conversation.

---

### Question 3 — Receipt substantiation (§274(d) categories)

*Are all §274(d) receipts — meals, lodging, vehicle expenses on overnight trips — paired with their photographed receipt and contemporaneous purpose entry?*

| Score | Pattern |
|---|---|
| 3 | Every §274(d) entry has photo + log entry within 48 hours of the trip |
| 2 | All have logs; some photos missing (e.g., per-diem trips where receipts not required) |
| 1 | Some logs incomplete; reconstruction in progress |
| 0 | Reconstructing from memory or credit-card statements |

**Why this matters.** §274(d) substantiation is strict. Reconstructions don't survive.

**Action if 0–1:** What's reconstructable from contemporaneous corroborating evidence (calendar entries, contractor invoices, guest messages) gets reconstructed and saved. What can't be substantiated is dropped from the deduction. Painful but cleaner than risk.

---

### Question 4 — Mileage log

*Is your mileage log (TAX-001) complete and contemporaneous, with each entry containing date, odometer readings, miles, business purpose, and destination?*

| Score | Pattern |
|---|---|
| 3 | All trips logged within the week of the trip; year-end total locked |
| 2 | Mostly contemporaneous; 1–2 trips reconstructed with corroboration |
| 1 | >5 trips reconstructed; some trips missing entirely |
| 0 | No log or pure reconstruction from Google Timeline |

**Action if 0–1:** Reconstruct what you can with corroborating evidence. Drop what you can't substantiate. Set a phone reminder to log the next trip *the day it happens.*

---

### Question 5 — 1099-NEC readiness

*Does TAX-003 show every contractor at year-to-date totals, with W-9s collected for any contractor over $600?*

| Score | Pattern |
|---|---|
| 3 | Every $600+ contractor has a W-9 on file; 1099-NEC packets ready to file by January 31 |
| 2 | All contractors tracked; 1–2 W-9s still being collected |
| 1 | Some contractors not on the tracker; W-9 collection incomplete |
| 0 | Cash payments, no W-9s, no tracker |

**Action if 0–2:** This is a December decision. Collect the missing W-9s before December 31 if possible. A late W-9 is recoverable; a January-30 scramble for an unresponsive contractor is not.

---

### Question 6 — Depreciation schedule

*Is the depreciation schedule (TAX-004) up to date, with every property's MACRS schedule current and any new placed-in-service assets entered?*

| Score | Pattern |
|---|---|
| 3 | Schedule is current; new assets entered within 30 days of placement-in-service |
| 2 | Schedule current; some new assets pending entry |
| 1 | Schedule out of date; new acquisitions un-tracked |
| 0 | No depreciation tracking |

**Action if 0–2:** Enter all 2026 placed-in-service assets before year-end. Decide on §179 / §168(k) elections — those decisions are made *with the return,* but the data feeding the return needs to be current.

---

### Question 7 — Capital decisions still pending

*Are there any large purchases or projects you've been deferring? Would year-1 acceleration (§179, §168(k) bonus) make this year a better year to act?*

| Score | Pattern |
|---|---|
| 3 | All capital decisions made on a calendar; year-end timing was deliberate |
| 2 | Most decisions made; one or two pending CPA review |
| 1 | Several deferred decisions; no analysis of year-end timing |
| 0 | No capital plan |

**Why this matters.** Capital expenditures placed in service before December 31 generate this year's depreciation. Same purchase placed in service on January 5 generates *next* year's depreciation. The 5-day gap can be worth thousands.

**Action if 0–2:** List every pending purchase or project over $1,500. For each, decide: ship before year-end, defer to next year, or no action. Run the §179/Bonus/Capitalize decision tree (Decision-Tree Pack #3).

---

### Question 8 — Personal-use audit

*If any property has a §280A(d)(2) personal-use limitation risk, have you verified the day count is under the threshold?*

| Score | Pattern |
|---|---|
| 3 | Every property's personal-use days verified; well under threshold |
| 2 | Verified; one property is close to the threshold and being managed |
| 1 | Have not run the calculation; uncertainty |
| 0 | Suspect a property is over the threshold |

**Why this matters.** §280A(d)(2) — the *greater of 14 days or 10% of rental days* — flips a property into "residence" status if exceeded. One overnight too many at year-end can disqualify the entire year's deductions.

**Action if 0–2:** Pull the personal-use calendar (Augusta Documentation Pack, Template 3) and run the math today. If close to the threshold, do not stay an additional night at any rental property between now and December 31.

---

### Question 9 — Estimated tax

*Have you funded estimated tax payments to safe-harbor levels?*

| Score | Pattern |
|---|---|
| 3 | Q1–Q4 payments made; safe harbor satisfied (110% of prior-year liability or 90% of current-year) |
| 2 | Mostly current; Q4 payment scheduled |
| 1 | Behind on quarterly payments; underpayment penalty possible |
| 0 | No estimated payments made |

**Why this matters.** Underpayment penalties under §6654 are non-deductible and avoidable. The Q4 payment due January 15 is the last lever to pull.

**Action if 0–2:** Run TAX-005 (Quarterly Estimated Tax workbook). Compute the gap. Make the Q4 payment by January 15 to mitigate the penalty.

---

### Question 10 — Audit dossier readiness

*If a correspondence-examination IDR arrived in your mailbox tomorrow, could you produce the substantiation for every line on this year's return within 30 days?*

| Score | Pattern |
|---|---|
| 3 | Yes, with confidence. Tabletop drill score above 13. |
| 2 | Mostly. A few line items would require reconstruction. |
| 1 | Significant gaps. Would need help. |
| 0 | No. Would lose deductions on examination. |

**Why this matters.** This is the only question that asks not "did you do it" but "can you prove it." Most year-end gaps live here.

**Action if 0–2:** Re-run the Tabletop Drill (Bonus B2) before next April. Identified gaps go on a 60-day close-out list.

## Verdict

| Score | Verdict |
|---|---|
| **27–30** | **Operating cleanly.** This is a graduate of the system. Maintain cadence; don't drift. |
| **22–26** | **Mostly clean, two gaps.** Identify the two lowest-scoring questions, write 60-day close-outs. |
| **15–21** | **System is partial.** The bookkeeping is being done; the substantiation, planning, or compliance is not. Treat next year as an installation year, not a maintenance year. |
| **8–14** | **System is not running.** This year's return will be reconstructed. Future years require restarting at Module 2. |
| **Below 8** | **Stop and rebuild.** Treat this year's return as triage. Engage a CPA. Restart Module 1 in January with a fresh year and clean cadence. |

## The "before December 31" list

Some scorecard items lapse at year-end. Pull these forward to today:

- [ ] **Q5 W-9 collection** — collect outstanding W-9s before contractors disappear over the holidays
- [ ] **Q7 capital placement-in-service** — purchases placed in service by 12/31 generate this year's depreciation
- [ ] **Q8 personal-use audit** — ensure no rental property crosses the §280A(d)(2) threshold
- [ ] **Q9 estimated tax Q4** — January 15 deadline; pay this week to avoid the holiday-week scramble
- [ ] **Augusta day cap** — verify ≤14 days; do not exceed in last week of year
- [ ] **Charitable contributions** *(if claiming on personal return)* — payment must clear by 12/31

---

## The "before April 15" list

These feed the CPA handoff and the next return:

- [ ] **Q1 quarterly cadence** — confirm 2027 dates; calendar entries created
- [ ] **Q2 bookkeeping cleanup** — uncategorized rows resolved
- [ ] **Q3, Q4 substantiation** — gaps closed where possible; documented where not
- [ ] **Q6 depreciation schedule** — finalized for the return
- [ ] **Q10 audit dossier** — folder built; tabletop drill re-run
