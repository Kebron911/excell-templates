# Bonus — The 47-Deduction Self-Audit Checklist *(Reader Edition+)*

**Format:** 8-page printable PDF + matching Excel workbook tab
**Delivered as:** Module 3 capstone tool
**Course module:** Module 3 (Lesson 3.12)

> **Use this once a year, in February.** Walk through your prior-year Schedule E line by line. Check each deduction against the 47-line list. If you missed three or more, you've already paid for this course.

---

## How to score

For each deduction below:

| Mark | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| ✓ | Claimed correctly on last year's return | Continue to capture in current year |
| ✗ | Should have claimed but didn't | Amend return (Form 1040-X) if material; capture going forward |
| – | Not applicable to your portfolio | Skip |
| ? | Unsure | Flag for CPA review at handoff (Module 6) |

Total ✗ marks at the end. **Three or more = the course paid for itself this morning.**

## Part I — Operating expenses (the recurring ten)

*Schedule E lines 5, 7, 8, 9, 17, 19. Most hosts catch these. The traps are completeness and gross-vs-net reporting.*

| # | Deduction | Sched E line | Got it? | Trap to watch |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cleaning fees paid to cleaners | 7 | ☐ | Did you pay $600+ to any one cleaner without a 1099-NEC? |
| 2 | Cleaning supplies (consumables) | 17 | ☐ | Allocate mixed-use receipts (Example 4 in the Worked-Example Library) |
| 3 | Property utilities — electric, gas, water, sewer | 17 | ☐ | Make sure the meter is on the rental property, not personal |
| 4 | Internet and cable TV at the property | 17 | ☐ | Full deduction if dedicated to rental; allocate if shared |
| 5 | Trash and recycling service | 17 | ☐ | — |
| 6 | Lawn care, snow removal, pest control | 17 | ☐ | 1099-NEC if any single vendor crossed $600 |
| 7 | Pool, hot tub, septic service | 17 | ☐ | Pool maintenance is ordinary; capital pool work is depreciable |
| 8 | Security system monitoring fees | 17 | ☐ | Monitoring is current; alarm install may be capitalized |
| 9 | HOA dues (if applicable) | 17 | ☐ | Special assessments are sometimes capitalized — check the assessment letter |
| 10 | Property management fees | 11 | ☐ | If you self-manage, this is zero — note that |
| 11 | Mortgage interest on rental property | 12 | ☐ | Pull from Form 1098; allocate if the loan is mixed |
| 12 | Property tax on rental | 16 | ☐ | County assessor's annual statement; not the escrow estimate |
| 13 | Insurance premiums | 9 | ☐ | Allocate umbrella across rental + personal (Example 11) |

## Part II — Repairs and capital (the BAR-test set)

*Schedule E lines 14 (repairs) and 18 (depreciation). The line between repair and improvement is where the audit risk lives.*

| # | Deduction | Sched E line | Got it? | Trap to watch |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 14 | Routine repairs (paint, minor plumbing, fixture replacements) | 14 | ☐ | Document the BAR test for any single line over $1,000 |
| 15 | Routine maintenance (HVAC service, gutter cleaning) | 14 | ☐ | Routine-maintenance safe harbor under §1.263(a)-3(i) |
| 16 | Like-kind component replacements (failed appliances, water heater) | 14 | ☐ | De-minimis safe harbor under $2,500 per invoice |
| 17 | Building depreciation — MACRS 27.5-year (or 39-year if nonresidential) | 18 | ☐ | Land is not depreciable; pull land allocation from county tax records |
| 18 | Improvements depreciation (roof, deck rebuild, full renovation) | 18 | ☐ | Track each improvement on its own MACRS schedule |

| Score | Verdict |

| 3–5 ✗ | The course paid for itself. Consider amending if any single missed item is >$1,000. |
| 6+ ✗ | Stop. Schedule a CPA consult before filing this year's return. There's a system gap, not a one-off. |
| Any ? | Bring to the CPA handoff (Module 6.4). The "?" rows are the highest-leverage conversation. |

---

## What to do with the ✗ rows

For each ✗:

1. **Quantify it.** What's the deduction worth, and at what marginal rate?
2. **Decide on amendment.** Form 1040-X is available for 3 years from the original filing. Materiality threshold is judgment — most CPAs amend for $500+ in tax effect.
3. **Capture going forward.** Add the missed item to your TAX-002 / TAX-004 capture rules so it doesn't slip again.
4. **Talk to your CPA.** Some amendments raise audit risk; some don't. The 12-question scorecard (Bonus B-CPA-Interview) helps frame which preparers handle amendments well.

---

## What to do with the ? rows

For each ?:

1. **Don't guess.** Flag and move on.
2. **Compile evidence.** What documentation do you have or can you get?
3. **Bring to Module 6.4.** The draft-return review is where these get resolved.
