# Bonus B7 — The Annual "What Changed" Update Video

**Format:** 30-min video, recorded once per year in early January
**Course module:** Living-content commitment to all enrolled students
**Use case:** Tells students what to update, what to leave alone, and what to ask their CPA

> **Tax law moves every year. Students who bought the course in 2026 deserve to know what changed by 2027 without re-reading a 700-page IRS publication.**
>
> This video is the course's annual obligation. Recorded the first week of January after IRS rate releases land. 30 minutes. The same structure every year so students know exactly where to find what they need.

---

## Why this video exists

A static course on tax compliance has a half-life of about 12 months. Without an annual refresh, the content drifts from defensible into "based on the law as of January 2026," and students lose trust.

The "What Changed" video is the contract. Buyers know:

1. The course will be re-grounded every January
2. The structure will be the same so they can navigate quickly
3. Material changes will be flagged in the lessons themselves (annotated with year and version)

This is what justifies "lifetime updates" as a bona fide promise rather than marketing language.

---

## Production cadence

| Date | Action |
|---|---|
| **Mid-December** | Pre-production: gather IRS Rev. Procs., new pubs, Treasury notices |
| **First week of January** | IRS releases standard mileage rate, per-diem CONUS rates |
| **Second week of January** | §168(k) bonus rate confirmed (verify against statute, not just IRS publication) |
| **Third week of January** | Record the video; edit; ship to LMS |
| **Fourth week of January** | Email all enrolled students linking to the new video |

Tight cadence so the video lands well before tax season runs.

---

## The 5-section structure (same every year)

```
1. RATES                    (5 min)
2. THRESHOLDS               (5 min)
3. CODE / GUIDANCE CHANGES  (10 min)
4. WHAT'S UNCHANGED         (3 min)
5. ACTION ITEMS             (7 min)
                            ─────
                            30 min
```

The structure is rigid because **viewers come back to last year's video to remember where a fact lived**. Year-over-year consistency makes the videos searchable as a corpus.

---

## Section 1 — Rates (5 min)

Cover the IRS-published rates that change every year. Even when they don't change, *say so* — a viewer needs to know whether to update or not.

Each rate gets:
- Current-year value
- Prior-year value
- Direction of change
- Where it shows up in the workbook
- One-sentence implication

### Standard checklist

- IRS standard mileage rate
- Per-diem CONUS rates (lodging + M&IE)
- Foreign per-diem (if any STR is overseas)
- §401(k) contribution limits *(only if relevant to STR-host audience)*
- Standard deduction (informational, not an STR rate)

### Sample script — rates section (year-agnostic template)

```
[OPEN — title card: "Rates"]

[VO]

Section one. Rates that change every January.

The IRS standard mileage rate for {{YEAR}} is {{RATE}} cents per mile,
{{up/down/unchanged}} from {{PRIOR_RATE}} cents the year before.

If you logged miles last year at the {{PRIOR_RATE}} cent rate, the
{{YEAR}} rate doesn't apply retroactively — last year's miles use last
year's rate. {{YEAR}} miles use {{RATE}}.

In your workbook, the standard rate lives in TAX-001's "Rate" cell at
the top of the year tab. Update that one cell. The mileage formula
recalculates automatically.

The per-diem CONUS rate for high-cost localities is now ${{HIGH_COST}}
per day, with ${{MEALS}} of that allocated to meals. Standard CONUS is
${{STANDARD}}. {{COMMENTARY ON CHANGE}}.

If you take overnight repair trips and use the per-diem method, the
new rates apply for travel beginning {{DATE}}. Travel before that
date uses the old rates. Workbook reference: TAX-007, Rate tab.

[Continue with each rate...]
```

## Section 2 — Thresholds (5 min)

Dollar thresholds that change with inflation indexing.

### Standard checklist

- §179 expensing cap and phase-out threshold
- §163(j) interest-deduction limit *(if applicable)*
- 1099-NEC reporting threshold (rare to change but verify)
- §1.263(a)-1(f) de-minimis safe harbor *(rare to change but verify)*
- §6651 / §6654 penalty rates (varies with federal short-term rate)
- Estate and gift tax thresholds (informational for high-net-worth segment)

### Sample script — thresholds section

```
Section two. Thresholds.

The §179 expensing cap for {{YEAR}} is ${{CAP}}. The phase-out begins
at ${{PHASE_START}} of total qualifying purchases.

For an STR host, §179 hits this cap rarely — it's structured around
much larger asset purchases — but it matters if you're stacking §179
across cost-segregated reclassifications.

The de-minimis safe harbor is unchanged at $2,500 per invoice
(or $5,000 with applicable financial statement). If you elected this
on a prior return, the election persists; you do not need to re-elect
unless your facts changed materially.

The 1099-NEC threshold remains $600 for the year — note: there have
been legislative proposals to lower this to $200 across multiple
sessions. None have passed yet. If they do, the change will be
mid-year and we'll record a supplemental update.

[Continue...]
```

## Section 3 — Code & guidance changes (10 min)

The biggest section. New regulations, new IRS guidance, court decisions, statutory changes from year-end legislation.

This is the section that most needs the host's CPA to see — material code changes are not always self-applicable.

### What goes here

- **§168(k) bonus depreciation** — the phase-down rate is statutory and changes each year. Confirm the rate. Note any acceleration or extension legislation passed in December.
- **§163(j) interest limits** — relevant for hosts with multi-property mortgage stacks
- **§469 / §1.469 guidance** — any revenue rulings or court cases on STR loophole, material participation, real-estate professional status
- **Treasury regulations finalized in the prior year** — proposed regs sometimes finalize and the answer changes
- **IRS Practice Units released** — informal but signals examiner posture
- **Notable Tax Court / Federal Circuit cases** — STR-relevant decisions
- **Court of Appeals splits** — relevant for hosts in different circuits
- **State law changes** — flagged generally; states are out of scope for individualized advice but acknowledge the variation

### Sample script — code/guidance section

```
Section three. Code and guidance.

This is the section to bring to your CPA. The rates and thresholds
above apply mechanically. Code changes are interpretive and may need
your specific facts examined.

{{YEAR}} brought {{N_CHANGES}} material changes I want to flag.

CHANGE ONE: {{HEADLINE}}.

{{2-MIN explanation. What was the old rule? What's the new rule?
Who does it affect? Worked example.}}

If you're affected by this — {{CRITERIA}} — bring it to your CPA
review in February. Specific question to ask:
"{{LITERAL CPA QUESTION}}".

[Continue with each material change. 2-3 minutes per change. The
goal is not exhaustiveness — it's "the CPA-conversation list" for
the host.]
```

### What does NOT go in this section

- Esoteric code changes that don't affect typical STR portfolios
- Speculation about pending legislation (mention only if signed)
- Generalized commentary on the political environment
- Anything that requires the host to know more than the course taught

## Section 4 — What's unchanged (3 min)

This section is short but psychologically important. Hosts who watched the prior section worried the system needs a rebuild. This section lets them exhale.

### Standard checklist

- Schedule E vs. Schedule C decision tree
- The recordkeeping pact (§6001, §274(d))
- The BAR test under §1.263(a)-3
- Material-participation tests under §1.469-5T
- The Augusta Rule (§280A(g))
- The 14-day personal-use line (§280A(d))

### Sample script — unchanged section

```
Section four. What's unchanged.

The framework you built last year still holds. The Recordkeeping
Pact, the BAR test, the seven material-participation tests, the
14-day personal-use threshold, the Augusta Rule — none of these moved.

The system installs once. The rates inside it refresh each January.
That's the deal.

If you completed Modules 1 through 7 last year, you do not need to
re-watch them. You need to update the rate cells in the workbooks
and the depreciation schedule. Section five walks through that
update list.
```

## Section 5 — Action items (7 min)

The host's checklist for the year. This is what people open the video for.

### Action template

```
ACTION 1 — Update workbook rate cells.
  Where: TAX-001 → Rate tab → cell B2
  What: Change from {{OLD_RATE}} to {{NEW_RATE}}
  Time: 30 seconds
  Done by: Before logging the first {{YEAR}} mileage entry

ACTION 2 — Update per-diem rates.
  Where: TAX-007 → Rate tab → cells B2:B5
  What: Update lodging + M&IE for both standard and high-cost CONUS
  Time: 2 minutes
  Done by: Before claiming the first {{YEAR}} per-diem entry

ACTION 3 — Re-review §168(k) bonus rate.
  Where: TAX-004 → Depreciation tab → cell K3
  What: Verify the bonus % matches statute
  Time: 30 seconds
  Done by: Before placing first {{YEAR}} asset in service

ACTION 4 — {{If applicable}} Discuss {{material code change}} with CPA.
  Where: At your February CPA handoff meeting
  What: Specific question script provided in the lesson description
  Time: 5 minutes of CPA time
  Done by: Before {{YEAR}} return is filed

ACTION 5 — Re-run the Year-End Health Check on prior year.
  Where: Bonus — Year-End Portfolio Health Check
  What: Score the year now-closed; capture lessons for the new year
  Time: 4 hours (one-time annual exercise)
  Done by: End of January
```

The action list is downloadable as a 1-page PDF alongside the video — *"{{YEAR}} Update Action List"* — so hosts can print it and check off as they go.

## Voice & pacing rules

- **Calm, not alarmed.** Tax law changes annually; this is not news, it is maintenance. The video's tone reflects that.
- **Specific dollar figures, every time.** No "the rate went up a bit" — "the rate went up 1.5 cents to 65.5 cents."
- **Cite the source.** Rev. Proc. {{YEAR-1}}-X, IR-{{YEAR}}-X, the relevant statutory section. Code citations on screen, not just spoken.
- **No commentary on policy.** This is not the venue for opinions about whether the change is good or bad. State the rule, state the impact, move on.
- **Honor the 30-minute cap.** A 50-minute "What Changed" video is a 25% drop in completion rate. Edit ruthlessly.

## Archival policy

- Videos older than 3 years are archived but not deleted — students who joined later can scroll back to see the trend.
- Each video stamped clearly with year and recording date.
- A "Video Archive" page in the LMS lists all years for navigation.

---

## What this video is not

- Not a substitute for a CPA's review of a specific return
- Not a comprehensive read of IRS publications
- Not legal advice on contested provisions
- Not a vehicle for marketing other products (the course's promise is content, not upsell)
