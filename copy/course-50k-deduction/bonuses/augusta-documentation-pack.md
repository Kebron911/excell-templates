# Bonus — The Augusta Documentation Pack

**Format:** 3 templates + this design spec
**Course module:** Module 4 (Lesson 4.4)
**Use case:** The complete documentation set for a defensible §280A(g) Augusta Rule election

> **§280A(g) is one of the cleanest deductions in the Internal Revenue Code — and one of the most commonly disallowed on examination, because hosts elect it without building the file.**
>
> This pack is the file. Three templates, one workflow. Build the file before you take the deduction, not after.

---

## Pack contents

| # | Template | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Board Minutes Template | Per-meeting documentation of business purpose, attendees, agenda |
| **2** | FMV Comparable-Rate Worksheet | Substantiation that your rental rate is reasonable |
| **3** | Personal-Use Calendar | Contemporaneous record of the 14 rental dates and personal-use boundaries |

All three ship as Word + PDF + Excel. The Excel version is integrated as a tab in TAX-002.

---

## Why three templates (not one)

The §280A(g) deduction rests on a three-legged stool:

1. **Reasonable rate** — the FMV worksheet
2. **Legitimate business purpose** — the board minutes
3. **Discrete days, no overlap with personal use** — the calendar

Pull any one leg out and the deduction collapses. Examiners know this. They ask for all three.

```

### Rules for filling it out

1. **Same day, not later.** Minutes drafted contemporaneously hold up. Minutes drafted in March of the following year do not.
2. **Specific agenda.** "Discussed Q3 results, reviewed Cabin C occupancy data showing 67% vs. 78% target, voted to engage PriceLabs revenue management at $20/month" beats "discussed business operations."
3. **Real attendees.** If only one member exists in a single-member LLC, the minutes reflect that. Don't invent attendees.
4. **Real decisions.** If the meeting produced no decisions, document what was discussed and why no decisions were finalized. A meeting without business is a sham-transaction problem.
5. **Sign and date.** Unsigned minutes are draft minutes.

### Common failure mode

A host holds 14 "meetings" of a single-member LLC where she is the only attendee, the agenda is "review of operations," no decisions are made, and the minutes are drafted in February of the following year. On examination, the IRS disallows the deduction as a sham transaction.

The fix: substantive meetings, real attendees (advisors, accountants, contractors, key personnel), real agendas, real decisions, signed and dated the same day.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

reasonable based on the data above.

Prepared by:               _________________________________
Date prepared:             ________________
                          (must be before first rental day)
```

### Rules for filling it out

1. **Three comparables, not one.** A single comparable is anecdote. Three is a defensible range.
2. **Real venues with public rates.** Peerspace, Splacer, and local hotel meeting-room rate cards are good sources. "I asked my neighbor" is not.
3. **Date the research.** Screenshot the comparable rates as of the research date. Save to the Augusta folder.
4. **Land at or below the median.** Above-median rates are not disqualifying but require justification.
5. **Refresh annually.** Market rates move. Update the worksheet each January before the first rental day of the new year.

### Common failure mode

The host charges the entity $1,500/day for a home office, surveys nothing, and reasons "it's my house, my rate." On examination, the IRS challenges the rate, finds comparable Peerspace listings at $200–$400/day, and disallows the rent above the defensible amount.

The fix: do the research first. Land in the defensible band. Document the work.

## Template 3 — Personal-Use Calendar

**File:** `augusta-personal-use-calendar.xlsx`
**Use:** One calendar per tax year, integrated as a tab in TAX-002

### The calendar (logical structure)

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
              PERSONAL-USE & §280A(g) RENTAL CALENDAR
                          Tax Year ________
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PROPERTY:                 [Address]
HOMEOWNER:                [Name]

Day-by-day record (one row per day in the year):

Date         Day type                        Notes / Business purpose
──────────   ─────────────────────────────   ───────────────────────
2026-01-01   [ ] Personal                    ___________________
             [ ] Augusta §280A(g) rental
             [ ] Home office only
             [ ] Mixed
             [ ] Vacant

2026-01-02   [ ] Personal                    ___________________
             [ ] Augusta §280A(g) rental
             [ ] Home office only
             [ ] Mixed
             [ ] Vacant

[continue for all 365 days...]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ANNUAL TOTALS (auto-summed in Excel version)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Personal use days:                          ____
§280A(g) Augusta rental days:               ____  (cap: 14)
Home-office-only days:                      ____
Mixed-use days:                             ____
Vacant days:                                ____
                                           ─────
TOTAL                                        365

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
COMPLIANCE CHECKS (auto-flagged in Excel)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[ ] Augusta day count ≤ 14 ........................... ____ / 14
[ ] No same-day overlap (personal + Augusta) ......... [ OK / FLAG ]
[ ] Each Augusta day has matching board minutes ...... [ OK / FLAG ]
[ ] Each Augusta day has FMV-supported rate .......... [ OK / FLAG ]
[ ] No clustering at year-end (audit-risk pattern) ... [ OK / FLAG ]
```

### Rules for filling it out

1. **Contemporaneous, not reconstructed.** Update the calendar at least weekly. The Excel version syncs to TAX-002 automatically once linked.
2. **One use per day.** A day cannot be both personal and Augusta. Pick one. Mixed-use days are *home office only* — neither personal nor Augusta-rentable.
3. **Cluster avoidance.** 14 consecutive Augusta days at year-end looks engineered. Spread across the year alongside your real meeting cadence.
4. **No 15th day.** The Excel version flags the moment you cross 14. Do not override.
5. **Cross-reference the meetings.** Each Augusta day must have a corresponding board-minutes file (Template 1) and reference an FMV worksheet entry (Template 2).

### Common failure mode

The host marks 14 Augusta days in late December — all clustered, all "year-end strategy retreat" — with no other business activity at the residence during the year. On examination, the IRS treats the cluster as evidence of tax-motivated transactions rather than legitimate business purpose.

The fix: Augusta days follow the entity's actual meeting cadence. If the entity holds quarterly board meetings, those four days are natural Augusta candidates. Strategy retreats and training sessions fill the rest. The calendar reflects the business, not the deduction.

## What the entity files and what the homeowner files

| Side | What gets reported |
|---|---|
| **Homeowner (you)** | Rental income from the entity is *excluded* from gross income under §280A(g). No Schedule E entry for these days. The exclusion is automatic if the day count is ≤14; no election form is filed. Keep the documentation for your records. |
| **Entity (LLC/S-corp/C-corp)** | Rent paid is a §162 ordinary-and-necessary business expense. Deducted on the entity's return (Form 1120-S, Form 1065, Schedule C, etc.). Issue a 1099-MISC to the homeowner reporting the rent paid in box 1, even though the homeowner excludes the income. The 1099 is a paper-trail signal, not a tax assessment. |

The 1099-MISC seems redundant — and arguably is — but issuing it removes ambiguity if the IRS computer-matches the entity's deductions to information returns. The homeowner reports the 1099 and then claims the §280A(g) exclusion on the return.

## What this pack is not

- Not a substitute for a CPA's review of the §280A(g) election. Some practitioners are conservative on the rule; some are aggressive. Build the file, then have the file reviewed.
- Not appropriate for sole-proprietors. §280A(g) requires an entity-to-homeowner transaction.
- Not a workaround for personal-use limitations on the STR property itself. §280A(g) covers the personal residence; §280A(d) governs personal use of the rental property — different rules, different documentation.
