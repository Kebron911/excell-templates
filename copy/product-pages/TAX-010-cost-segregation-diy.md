## FAQ

**Is DIY cost seg actually IRS-acceptable?**
Yes — for properties under $1M with reasonable allocations and proper documentation. The IRS Cost Segregation Audit Techniques Guide explicitly contemplates self-performed studies. The workbook's photo + measurement checklist meets the documentation standard. For properties over $1M, the IRS prefers engineering-based studies because the dollar amounts make the additional rigor worthwhile.

**What's "bonus depreciation" and is it still around?**
Bonus depreciation (§168(k)) lets you deduct a percentage of qualifying property in year 1 instead of straight-line. Was 100% through 2022, phasing down: 80% (2023), 60% (2024), 40% (2025), 20% (2026), 0% (2027) — though Congress sometimes extends. **Verify the current-year percentage before filing.** The workbook has a single cell to update as rates change.

**STR Loophole — what is it really?**
IRC §469 generally treats rental losses as passive (can't offset W-2 income). The exception: if your average guest stay is ≤ 7 days AND you materially participate (100+ hours, more than anyone else), the activity is treated as a non-rental active business — losses can offset W-2 income. Cost seg supercharges the loss. This is the highest-leverage tax move in the STR investor playbook.

**What if I bought the property last year?**
Form 3115 (Application for Change in Accounting Method) lets you catch up missed depreciation in one year via §481(a) adjustment. The workbook produces the asset breakdown. Your CPA files Form 3115. We're not your CPA — confirm before filing.
