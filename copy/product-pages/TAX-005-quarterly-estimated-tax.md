## The 30-second pitch

You got hit with a $400 underpayment penalty last April. The IRS doesn't care that your STR income was lumpy — they care that you didn't pay enough as you went. The fix is the quarterly schedule: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15. Pay roughly 25% of your projected annual tax each quarter, and the penalty vanishes.

The hard part is "roughly." Your STR income is seasonal. Your spouse's W-2 covers some of the bill. You took a §179 vehicle deduction that flips your tax picture. SE tax may or may not apply depending on whether you're Schedule E or C.

This workbook does the math. Plug in your forecast, your prior-year tax, your already-paid amounts. The Quarterly Schedule tab tells you what to send each quarter. Pay via IRS Direct Pay (free), mark paid, re-run after each quarter to recalibrate.

## FAQ

**Do I have to pay quarterly estimated taxes?**
The IRS requires it whenever you'll owe more than $1,000 at year-end and your withholding doesn't cover at least 90% of current-year tax (or 100%/110% of prior-year tax). Most STR hosts hit this threshold by their second year of profitable operation.

**What happens if I miss a quarter?**
You can catch up the next quarter, but the underpayment penalty accrues per-quarter (Form 2210). Better to pay something each quarter than skip a quarter and double up.

**Where do I actually send the money?**
IRS Direct Pay at irs.gov is free and instant. Quick-reference tab links straight there. Your state has its own portal.

**Schedule E or Schedule C — does it matter for estimateds?**
Yes. Schedule E filers owe income tax only. Schedule C filers owe income tax + 15.3% SE tax. The workbook auto-routes based on your Settings tab.
