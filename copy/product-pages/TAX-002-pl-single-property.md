## FAQ

**Is the depreciation calculation IRS-correct?**
For straight-line residential rental real property over 27.5 years using the mid-month convention, yes — that's the IRS-prescribed method for rental buildings placed in service after 1986 (Pub 527). The math is straightforward: depreciable basis ÷ 27.5 = annual; first-year prorated by months in service. The workbook's Depreciation tab walks the calculation transparently so your CPA can verify in one glance.

**Why isn't asset-by-asset depreciation included?**
Furniture (5-yr), appliances (5-yr), improvements (15-yr), bonus depreciation, cost segregation — those live in the Portfolio Master ($97 own-site) because the inputs and forms get materially more complex. Most single-property hosts get 80%+ of their depreciation from the building alone, which is what this tab handles.

**Does this support Schedule C (active trade or business)?**
Settings tab has a Schedule E vs Schedule C dropdown. The Schedule E Summary tab's net-income footnote routes to Line 26 (E) or Line 31 (C) accordingly. The form structure is the same — what changes is whether SE tax (15.3%) applies.

**What if I bought the property mid-year?**
The Depreciation tab handles mid-month convention automatically. Enter your in-service date on Property Info; the calculator computes months-in-service for the first calendar year. Subsequent full years auto-fill at the full annual amount.

**What if I'm in year three and never tracked depreciation before?**
Two options. (1) The Depreciation tab has an "Implied prior accumulated" helper that estimates what you should have taken. (2) Have your CPA file Form 3115 (Application for Change in Accounting Method) to catch up missed depreciation in one year. The latter is the right answer for amounts over a few thousand. We're not your CPA — confirm before filing.
