## FAQ

**Lite or Full?**
If you're underwriting one or two properties a year, Lite ($27) is enough. If you're running 5+ deals through the workbook before pulling the trigger, the $20 upgrade to Full pays for itself the first time the stress test catches a deal that looked good on paper. The Lite has no stress test, no Y1/Stabilized toggle, and no printable summary.

**Does it pull live AirDNA data?**
No. AirDNA is a paid API and not legally re-distributable inside an Excel template. You paste ADR and occupancy from AirDNA (or your own comp work) into the Market tab. Everything else calculates.

**What's the difference between Y1 and stabilized cash flow?**
Y1 occupancy for a new STR is typically 65% of stabilized — BiggerPockets community consensus, cited in the workbook. Most failed acquisitions assume Y1 = stabilized. The Full toggles between them on the Verdict tab.

**What appreciation rate does IRR assume?**
3% annual appreciation, 6% sell costs, 5-year hold. Editable assumption cells on the Verdict tab.

**Can I compare three properties side-by-side?**
One property per workbook (this is wizard mode). Multi-property comparison is a separate SKU. Most users duplicate the file once per candidate, then transfer the Verdict outputs into a 3-property summary.

**How is this different from the BiggerPockets calculator?**
BP's free calculator is built for long-term rentals — no avg LOS, no platform commission, no STR-specific occupancy modeling, no AirCover or cleaning fee mechanics. This workbook is built for short-term rentals from the inputs up.

**Why is the verdict tied to BiggerPockets benchmarks?**
Because they're the most-quoted STR cash-on-cash benchmarks in the BP forums and BP podcast — the same benchmarks your future buyer or lender will reference. Cited on the Verdict tab so you can argue them with your partner.

**Can my CPA review the output?**
That's what the CPA / Partner Summary tab (Full) is for — a 1-page printable with property details, 5-year pro-forma, verdict, and stress-test result, with a notes box for buyer commentary. Print to PDF, email, done.
