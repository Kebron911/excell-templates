## FAQ

**What is a 1099-NEC?**
Form 1099-NEC ("Nonemployee Compensation") reports payments of $600 or more in a calendar year to a person or unincorporated business who is not your employee, for services rendered. Required since the 2020 tax year (replaced 1099-MISC for this purpose). Filed by January 31 of the year following payment.

**Who needs a 1099 from me?**
Any individual or unincorporated business (sole prop, single-member LLC) you paid $600+ in a calendar year for services. Does NOT apply to: corporations (with limited exceptions like attorneys), payments via credit card or third-party platforms (those are reported on 1099-K by the platform — but Venmo/Zelle person-to-person are NOT exempt from 1099-NEC), and product-only purchases.

**What's the W-9 traffic light for?**
You can't issue a 1099 without the contractor's tax information. The W-9 form (filled out by the contractor and given to you) provides their legal name, address, and EIN/SSN. The dashboard's status column shows green ✓ Ready (W-9 on file) or yellow ⚠ Need W-9. Chase yellow rows in November/December — not January.

**What if I pay via Venmo or Zelle — does that exempt me?**
No. Person-to-person Venmo/Zelle payments to contractors are NOT covered by the platform's 1099-K reporting. You are still responsible for the 1099-NEC. Business-mode Venmo payments may be reported on 1099-K depending on the platform's current policy and the recipient's business setup. When in doubt, treat Venmo/Zelle as "your responsibility to 1099."

**What about my property manager?**
If your PM is a corporation, no 1099 typically required. If sole prop / single-member LLC, yes — and PM payments are often well over $600. The dashboard shows them either way.

**Can I file the 1099s from this workbook?**
No — the workbook prepares the data. To file, use a service like Track1099, Tax1099.com, or QuickBooks. Most cost $3–5 per form. Or your CPA files them. The dashboard exports clean.
