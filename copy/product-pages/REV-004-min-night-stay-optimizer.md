## The 30-second pitch

Most hosts assume "1-night MNS = max revenue." It isn't. A 2-night minimum books fewer total nights but spends 50% less on cleaning per night. A 3-night minimum maximizes per-stay revenue. A 7-night minimum loses short trips but wins for cabins where 5-7 nights is the natural booking length anyway. This workbook computes net revenue per night under each MNS using YOUR ADR + cleaning cost + demand pattern.

## The pattern

For most STRs:
- **2-night MNS** wins gross, highest turnover cost
- **3-night MNS** wins NET (sweet spot — most cleaning recovery, modest booking loss)
- **7-night MNS** wins for cabins / beach / ski / week-stay markets

The workbook tells you which applies to YOUR property. Often surprises operators by $2K-$5K/yr in net revenue.
