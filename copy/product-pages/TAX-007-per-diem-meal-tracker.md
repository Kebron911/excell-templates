## FAQ

**What counts as a "business meal"?**
A meal where the primary purpose is to discuss business with someone in your STR business: a cleaner, contractor, co-host, owner you're talking to about a deal, or an agent. Solo meals while traveling for the property are also deductible (you have to eat). Personal-only meals are not.

**What about meals for GUESTS?**
Different rule. If you're providing meals as part of substantial-services hosting (Schedule C operators only — see TAX-012), those are 100% deductible and live in cost-of-goods or hospitality services, not Line 7.

**Per-diem or actual?**
Whichever is higher per trip. Per-diem is simpler (no receipts needed), but capped by GSA rates. Actual is uncapped but requires receipts and substantiation.

**Do I really need photos of every receipt?**
For meals over $75, yes. Below $75, IRS allows a written log without receipts, but receipts are strongly recommended. Photo + cloud link works fine — the workbook has a column for the URL.
