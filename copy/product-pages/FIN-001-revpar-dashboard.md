## FAQ

**Q: Why is the blocked-night method so important?**
Because it is the only one that produces an occupancy figure comparable to the rest of the industry. Templates that ignore blocks overstate your number. Templates that count blocks as available understate it. PriceLabs and AirDNA both exclude blocks from the denominator. This workbook does too. That means your number plays in the same conversation as theirs.

**Q: I do not have AirDNA. Does the dashboard still work?**
Yes. The comp-benchmark row auto-hides when the comp Settings cell is empty. The dashboard then shows YoY comparison only. AirDNA is context, not required input.

**Q: What is RevPAR in plain English?**
Revenue Per Available Night. RevPAR = Gross Revenue ÷ (Total Nights − Blocked Nights). It mathematically equals ADR × Occupancy. The advantage: it captures rate and occupancy together. You cannot game it.

**Q: How do I get prior-year reference data if I never tracked this?**
Pull total gross revenue and total booked nights per property per month from your Airbnb / VRBO host dashboards. Enter on Settings rows 40-100. Roughly thirty minutes per property for a full prior year. Once entered, it does not need to be re-entered.

**Q: Multi-year tracking?**
One workbook per tax year, by design. Re-run annually with prior year as reference. The daily-calendar pivot degrades above one year of data.

**Q: Does this work in Google Sheets?**
Yes. The Daily Calendar pivot may take 10-15 seconds to render on first load — Sheets handles array formulas slightly slower than Excel. After load, scrolling is smooth.

**Q: Pace pickup or demand modeling?**
No. This is a backwards-looking KPI dashboard plus a forward-looking what-if simulator. Pace pickup is a separate SKU on the roadmap.
