## The 30-second pitch

The honest answer to "when can I quit?" is rarely the answer the gurus give. It is usually further out than they claim, and closer than the partner fears. The number itself is not the point. The spread between the optimistic and conservative date — that is the conversation you have been avoiding.

This workbook produces both. Optimistic uses your portfolio's actual numbers extrapolated cleanly. Conservative haircuts the occupancy, slows the acquisition pace, raises the buffer, and applies the partner risk-share slider. The spread between them is what you bring to the kitchen table.

The thing most exit plans forget — and the reason a lot of W2 quitters end up back at a job 18 months later — is healthcare. COBRA at 102 percent of premium for 18 months is roughly $14,400/yr for a family of four. ACA after that, depending on income and subsidy phase-outs, runs another $9,600 to $14,400/yr. Total bridge cost: typically $25,000 to $35,000 over the first 24 months. The Risk Plan tab models this. Your napkin probably did not.

The output is calm. The Conversation Doc is a one-page printable designed to be forwarded to a CPA or a fee-only financial planner without embarrassment. You are running a real business. Your exit plan should look like one.
