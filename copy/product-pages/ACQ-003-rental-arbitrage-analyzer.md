## The 30-second pitch

Rental arbitrage fails in Year 2, not Year 1. The landlord raises rent 7 to 10 percent at renewal and the operator is stuck with $14,800 of furniture in a unit that no longer cash flows.

Most arbitrage spreadsheets stop at Year 1 math. This one runs three scenarios — base, stabilized with a 5 percent escalator, and a worst case with a 7 percent escalator plus occupancy stress plus supplies inflation. If the unit dies at any of those, you find out before you sign.

There is also a fallback. If short-term demand collapses in your market, can mid-term rents (corporate housing, traveling nurses) cover the lease? The workbook benchmarks the MTR rate from Furnished Finder against your operating costs. Real operators have a Plan B. The workbook makes you build one.

And then there is the page that earns the price by itself: a printable, white-label, one-page master-lease proposal. Walk into the landlord meeting with a proposal, not a request.

A note before you buy: rental arbitrage is legal only when the lease permits subletting and the jurisdiction permits short-term rentals. The workbook checks both before any other math runs. If either is missing, the verdict is PASS. We are not your attorney — go get one before you sign.

## FAQ

**Is rental arbitrage legal?**
Only if the lease permits subletting in writing AND the city permits non-owner-occupied STRs. Both. The workbook flags either gap. Consult an attorney before signing.

**What if my landlord says yes verbally?**
Insufficient. Get the sublet permission in the lease itself or as a written addendum. Verbal permission evaporates at renewal.

**Why is the Landlord Pitch Page white-label?**
You are pitching the landlord on you, not on us. Strip our brand and add yours. Looks more professional. Closes more deals.

**One workbook per unit, or one for the portfolio?**
One per unit. Each lease has its own terms.

**Does this cover co-living or room-by-room?**
No. Different operating model, different math. Out of scope.

**Why is the MTR fallback included?**
Because real operators have a Plan B. If you cannot articulate one before signing, do not sign.
