## FAQ

**Q: I already own TAX-002. Do I throw it out?**
No. Keep prior years as archives. This workbook replaces TAX-002 going forward and the categories match exactly, so you can paste prior totals into the YoY reference table on Settings.

**Q: What about depreciation?**
Out of scope here, on purpose. Schedule E line 18 (depreciation) is a single dollar amount per property in this workbook. Calculate the underlying schedule with TAX-006 (Home Office Allocator), TAX-010 (Cost Segregation DIY), or your CPA's depreciation tool, then enter the total. The Portfolio Bundle pairs this workbook with both for $397.

**Q: How does shared-expense allocation actually work?**
Set Shared? = Yes on the Expense Log row. List the property names in the Allocation column, comma-separated. The workbook splits the amount equally at calculation time. For square-footage-weighted splits, do the math by hand and enter separate per-property rows — a footnote on Settings explains.

**Q: My properties span two LLCs and one personal-name. Will Entity Rollup handle that?**
Yes. The Entity column on Properties Register is the rollup key. Use "Personal" as one of your entities. The Entity Rollup tab will produce one column per entity you list.

**Q: Multi-year tracking?**
One workbook per tax year, by design. Tracking multiple years in one file degrades performance above 5,000 expense rows. Re-run annually with prior year as your YoY reference.

**Q: Capacity limits?**
20 properties, 3,000 revenue rows, 5,000 expense rows. Above that, recommend portfolio split into two workbooks. A typical 10-property STR portfolio uses about 1,200 revenue rows and 1,800 expense rows in a year — comfortably under cap.

**Q: Does this work in Google Sheets?**
Yes. Formulas remap on import. SUMIFS, OFFSET, and INDEX/MATCH all behave correctly. We test before each release.
