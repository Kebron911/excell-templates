### Rules for the response
1. **Respond within 90 minutes** of the query landing. Journalists pick the first credible source. Speed matters more than polish.
2. **Answer every question they asked**, in their order. Don't reorder.
3. **Use one concrete number per answer**. "$11K" is a quote. "A lot of money" is not.
4. **No links to product pages in the response**. The bio at the bottom links the homepage — that's enough.
5. **Offer one followup hook**: a related fact they didn't ask about. "Side note: most CPAs miss this — happy to walk through if useful."
6. **Bio must be ≤50 words, copy-paste ready.** Make their job easy.

## Topic-specific prepared responses (paste-ready)

### "What deductions do Airbnb hosts miss?"
Top 5 (memorize / paste):
1. Mileage — most hosts miss the 21¢/mile (2026 IRS standard for rental property) for trips to deliver supplies, meet cleaners, handle maintenance
2. Depreciation acceleration via cost segregation — easy +$5K–$15K first year
3. STR-specific insurance (Proper, Obie) often categorized as regular homeowners and not maxed out
4. Welcome gifts categorized as "meals" (50%) when they should be "supplies" (100%)
5. Internet, streaming, security subscriptions

### "How are STR regulations changing?"
Top 3:
1. **Honolulu** — 90-day minimum for any STR outside resort zones (effective late 2023)
2. **NYC Local Law 18** — registration requirement effectively killed STRs under 30 days (effective September 2023)
3. **Several Texas counties** + Tennessee mountain towns — moving to outright bans or 6-month-equivalent permitting; 12 states have legislation pending

### "Is Airbnb still profitable in 2026?"
Frame:
- Median revenue is down vs 2021 peak but UP vs pre-COVID baseline
- Best-operated 25% are MORE profitable than ever (better tools, less competition)
- Worst-operated 25% are losing money — fee creep + cleaning cost inflation
- Margin discipline is the divider; the "buy and ignore" hosts are getting squeezed out

### "Schedule E vs Schedule C for Airbnb?"
- 90% of hosts file Schedule E (average stay >7 days, no substantial services)
- Schedule C only if substantial services (meals, daily housekeeping, concierge) AND avg stay <7 days
- Wrong filing = 15.3% self-employment tax surprise
- Easy fail: hosts who provide breakfast accidentally trigger Schedule C
- Reference: IRS Pub 527
