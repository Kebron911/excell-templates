## Outreach email — Template A (cold, no warm intro)

> **Subject:** Idea for an STR-tax episode

> Hi {{ first_name }},
>
> Big fan of {{ specific recent episode }}. The way you handled {{ specific point }} made me re-think my own bookkeeping for last quarter.
>
> Quick pitch — I'm the founder of The STR Ledger, a financial-ops toolkit for STR operators. Over the past 14 months I've worked with hosts to find on average $11,000 in missed tax deductions per portfolio. I have a 3-part story angle that I think your audience would actually use, not just nod at:
>
> 1. **The 7 deductions most hosts (and their CPAs) miss** — specific Schedule E lines, dollar examples, the exact paperwork that triggers them
> 2. **Why "passive activity" is wrong for most STRs** — and how material participation flips the rules
> 3. **The compliance trap that's quietly making STRs in 12 US states illegal** without the host noticing
>
> I'm a guest, not a hard-pitcher — I won't read off product names on air. If your audience wants the worksheet that goes with the talk, we put it free at thestrledger.com/47 with no email gate.
>
> If this is interesting, I can pre-write 5 listener questions that map to your usual segment structure. Happy to do a 15-min pre-call.
>
> Daniel
> The STR Ledger
> {{ link to founder bio }}

### Personalization rules
1. **Reference one specific episode** (last 3 published). No generic "love your show."
2. **One concrete number** in the pitch (the $11K figure).
3. **Promise concrete listener value** (3 angles, not "I'd love to chat about STR taxes").
4. **Remove product pitch.** Hosts hate that. Mention the magnet only — and only if no email gate.
5. **Make their job easy** (offer pre-written questions, offer pre-call).

## After-booking process

1. **Confirm:** within 24h of booking, send recording-prep email (questions, agenda, mic/connection check).
2. **Pre-record:** ship the host a 1-page bio + 3 high-res headshots + 3 social-share quote cards.
3. **Day-of:** record clean audio on Riverside.fm/Squadcast (avoid Zoom — host audio quality matters).
4. **Post-release:** within 48h of episode going live —
   - Tweet/share the episode (don't just RT host's tweet — add commentary).
   - Email own list with "here's what we talked about + 2 worksheets we mentioned."
   - Drop a custom landing page at `thestrledger.com/listen/{{ show-slug }}` with the worksheets the host can link to in show notes (= clean attribution).
5. **30 days post-release:** track signups via the custom landing-page URL.

## Don't-bother list

Shows we don't want on the resume even if they say yes:
- Anything pay-to-play (charges for guest slots)
- Anything with a known crypto/scam co-host (reputation by association)
- Shows that haven't published in 90+ days (probably dead, can be discovered later)
- Shows that average <2K downloads/episode (small audience + same time-cost as a big one)
