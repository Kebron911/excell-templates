## Sponsorship slot types (what we'll buy)

### Best fit: "primary text ad" slot
- 50–80 words of copy
- 1 image (1200×628 or 1080×1080)
- One CTA link
- Placed in top-3 of newsletter (above-fold)

**Avoid:** banner-only slots, sidebar ads, podcast-style audio inserts in text newsletters. Low CTR.

### Best fit for premium send: "single-sponsor takeover"
- The entire newsletter is "brought to you by The STR Ledger"
- Costs 3-5x normal slot
- Only worth it for top 3 newsletters where audience match is perfect

## Negotiation rules

1. **Always ask for a discount on first send.** "I'd like to test the audience fit first — what's your introductory rate?"
2. **Negotiate by bundle, not single send.** "What's your 3-send rate?" usually saves 15–30%.
3. **Ask for performance data after the send.** Open rate, click rate to your CTA, total clicks. If they refuse, that's a red flag.
4. **Use a unique tracking URL per send.** `thestrledger.com/47?utm_source=<newsletter-slug>&utm_medium=newsletter&utm_campaign=<date>` — measures real attribution, not just clicks-on-day.

## Performance benchmarks (sets your "is this newsletter worth it?" bar)

| CPM range | Expected outcome (per $1,000 spent) |
|-----------|--------------------------------------|
| $20-30 | 250-500 sessions, 50-150 email signups, $300-1,200 revenue (12mo attribution) |
| $40-60 | 400-800 sessions, 80-250 signups, $600-2,400 revenue |
| $60-80 | 500-1,000 sessions, 120-400 signups, $1,000-4,000 revenue |

If a sponsorship does worse than the low end of its CPM tier, don't re-buy. If it does better than the high end, double the budget on the next send.
