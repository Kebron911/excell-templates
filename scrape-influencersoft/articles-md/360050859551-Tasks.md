## What are the tasks for?

This section assigns the tasks to the employees (additional administrators, call center operators) or even to a Department.

Tasks enable you to select the hot leads or doubters to suggest the best offers or provide the best services. It becomes easier to arrange and record the end-to-end interaction process with the clients. This is the main application of tasks (together with the existing Processes). Read two examples of this work below.

Tasks have some extra useful functions. You (or your employees) may, from time to time, need to call a client or to provide him or her with some extra materials or whatever it may be. More often than not, you may not have the proper instrument to record these tasks, and you just text them to your employees in different messengers (or external CRM systems as a better alternative). In the best case, these tasks are executed for a long time, sometimes they are partly done or even lost. Now, you can assign the tasks in InfluencerSoft and monitor their execution.

### A call to a website visitor (a simple case)

**Goal:** You would like to increase sales. You have decided to call your website visitors who, for example, visited a page with a course description and make them a special limited offer. If this offer is not accepted, then a discount on a product could be given.

**What should be done?**

-
Go to Process editor and add “Visited page” in a start trigger. An example below gives a typical page with a list of all courses. You are likely to have a landing page for a particular course or a group of courses.
The trigger is set to 1) “Visited URL”, 2) “Without parameters” to count the visits with the ad tags and without, and 3) enter the page address.[![](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks1-2.png)](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks1-2.png)

-

Add “Task” action. It will automatically generate a task to a call center manager. See the manager’s view of the page at the end of the article. Now, we will focus on business logics design. Let’s consider a simple case: a website visitor would like to either buy a course or not after the call. ### Calls to hot clients by Sales Department (a hot case)

-
**Goal:** You want to bring the purchase to the clients that 1.) have not asked for order yet 2.) have been sent the order, but have not paid them. Do this ASAP, literally within 5 to 10 minutes after a webpage visit.

**What should be done?**

First, find those clients that have opened the checkout page, then see whether the order has been generated. This determines the next steps. We can generate automatic tasks to the Sales Department, then add call-dependent stages of our sales funnel. So, let’s consider these steps:

Create an auto-list that will collect the unpaid orders by one of the products. This is a two-click action.

- Now, go to [Process](https://help.influencersoft.com/hc/en-us/articles/360051178071-Creating-and-Editing-Processes) editor and add “Visited a webpage” to a start trigger. This example gives a typical checkout page.

[![](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks6.png)](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks6.png)3.Now, the order page visit triggers the process. How can we understand whether an order has been generated or not? Whether it is paid or not? Here, we use a filter and an auto-list we created earlier. The filter parameters are in the auto-list with the unpaid orders by this product![](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks7.png)

-
Assign the task to the Sales Department. The first alternative: a customer 1) visits an order checkout page and 2) the order is not paid. In this case, the Sales Department is assigned a task, and call the customer, and help with the buying process. The tasks are in the Actions field. This is how it looks:![](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks8.png)

-
Tasks give certain advantages by two factors: they are automatically generated once pre-defined (by you) settings are reached, and one can configure different scenarios at further stages.  There are two alternatives by default — a task can be either “Done” or “Failed, but the actual sales give various scenarios. A customer can pay the invoice during the call — a perfect scenario, can ask for a discount, or the call can reveal a need in another product, etc. You can add any scenarios as a task result. A call center manager just registers the task result that automatically triggers the process.[![](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks9.png)](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks9.png)

-
Assign the actions for each case. For example, we can mail letters containing a link to the sales page (and a discount offer), describe other products, or mail some free useful information. These can be accomplished by familiar actions of the [Processes](https://help.influencersoft.com/hc/en-us/articles/360051178071#h_01ERDBVT8C6AQ0G7DK57BXTQXJ). Let’s go back to point 4. We have classified a customer’s action 1.) visited a page 2.) generated an order, but did not pay it. What if the order is not generated? In this case, we can automatically set the task to the Sales Department, for them to find out why the order is not generated and to assist in its processing. ## Can we assign a Task manually?

Yes, we can. We mentioned that at the beginning of the article.

### From Automation section

Go to “Automation” – “Task” subsection and click “Create a task”.[![](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks12-2.png)](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks12-2.png)

### From Lead or Order cards

Lead and Order cards have a new tab — Tasks. Here you can see all tasks for this order or lead and add a new task.[![](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks13.png)](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks13.png)

## Subsection features

### General view

The main page of the subsection displays “Create a task”, “Types”, and “Filter” buttons. The left upper corner shows the overall number of the tasks, as well as the number of tasks by status: opened, closed, overdue.

Chief admin of the profile can see these tasks, the employees see the tasks assigned to them.

The main part of the window displays a list of generated tasks with detailed information about each task. Pink highlights expired tasks, green – the tasks to be completed by today, white – the tasks to be completed in the future.

Tasks generated within a Process will also be displayed here.
[![](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks14.png)](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks14.png)

### Types of tasks

“Types” button controls the types of tasks: rename them, tailor them, select an icon and color them, reorder them in a list. These can be any types: just a call by default or reading specialized books before having a call to a very important customer. The key point here is that you spend less time on communicating with your employees.
Your newly created tasks will definitely be available in the tasks inside the Process.
[![](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks15-1.png)](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks15-1.png)

### Task filter

“Filter” button can find all tasks by a pre-defined parameter or a set of parameters. Filter finds a task by its number or name, by type, selects all tasks assigned on particular dates, generated or executed on particular dates, finds the tasks by a manager in charge, by status, result or the tasks generated by a particular employee.

## How to edit and delete the tasks

A task can be edited by the Chief admin only. To do this, open a task by clicking the number, task description or an object. Click task status in the opened lead card and then “Edit” button. Then you can change the task type, its description, dates or assign this task to another employee.

[![](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks16.png)](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks16.png)

You can delete a task by clicking a cross to the right of the task description in the overall list of tasks or by going to task editing in a lead card.

[![](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks17.png)](https://help.justclick.io/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/tasks17.png)

## How does an employee work with the tasks?

An employee sees the tasks in the overall list and opens them as we did it earlier. An employee can comment and select the result in a lead (or order) card, inside a task. The screenshot below gives both options by default. If you have already changed them or added your own, they will also appear in the task.
