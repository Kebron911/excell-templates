## What kind of 55-inch TV actually belongs in a short-term rental

The mistake hosts make: they buy the cheapest 55" they can find ($300 off-brand), it works for 18 months, then a software update fails, smart-TV apps stop loading, and guests review-bomb you for a "broken TV." Or they buy a $1,500 OLED, it's stunning, and the next guest spills something on it inside year 2.

The right move for STR is mid-tier, current-generation, brand-supported. Roughly $500–$800 buys a TV that:
- Has 5+ years of firmware support remaining
- Has a remote that you can replace for $20 if a guest's toddler launches it across the room
- Has streaming apps that don't break when the platform updates
- Is light enough for one person to wall-mount

## Specific picks ($500–$800)

- **TCL Q6 / Q7 Class** ($450–$650) — best value-tier QLED. Roku built-in, Roku app shipping is fast and reliable. Replace the remote on Amazon for $25.
- **Hisense U6 / U7 Series** ($500–$750) — slightly more colorful than TCL at the same price. Google TV (which guests sometimes prefer to Roku because it has YouTube TV / cable-cutter app integration).
- **LG UR8000 / UQ7570** ($600–$900) — premium-feel mid-tier, webOS, 5+ years of updates. Pricier but the OS holds up best for STR use because it's optimized for guest mode.

## Skip these

- **Off-brand under $300.** No firmware updates after year 2; apps stop working; guest can't watch their show; review.
- **OLED at the $1,500 tier.** Stunning, but burn-in is real with kid cartoons running 8 hours a day. Save it for your home, not the rental.
- **Sony Bravia at $1,000+.** Excellent TV, but you're paying for picture quality your guests largely won't notice. The ROI is wrong for STR.

## Wall mount, always

A 55" TV on a stand is a guest's child knocking it over. A 55" TV on the wall is a $35 mount and 30 minutes of your time, and it solves:

- Earthquake/toddler safety
- Listing-photo aesthetics (looks more "designed")
- Cleaning ease (no horizontal surface to dust)

Specific mount: **Mounting Dream MD2380 ($25–$45)**. Tilting (not full-motion) is best for STR — full-motion mounts get yanked on, then loosen, then sag.

## What goes wrong (and how to fix it)

- **Firmware update bricks the TV.** ~1% chance per year. Factory reset before paying for a service call; works 90% of the time.
- **Remote eaten/lost.** Replace on Amazon. Order three at once and stash two with the cleaner.
- **Wi-Fi drops, apps won't load.** Reboot the router; if persistent, the issue is router not TV.
- **Guest brings own streaming sticks.** Fine; just make sure your TV has a free HDMI port labeled clearly.

## Lifespan reality

The published 6-year lifespan tracks reality: by year 6, a mid-tier TV's apps are slowing visibly, the panel may have a stuck pixel or two, and current-gen models have meaningfully better picture quality at the same price tier. Replace on cycle, not on failure.
