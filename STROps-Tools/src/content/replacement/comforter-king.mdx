## What you actually pay to replace a king comforter in an Airbnb

The honest range for a king comforter in a short-term rental is $80-350. The cheap end gets you a polyester-filled microfiber unit from Target or Costco. The high end gets you a down-alternative or true down comforter with a 300+ thread count cotton shell.

Most STR operators land between $120 and $220 — a quality down-alternative that washes well and survives 4+ years of weekly laundering.

## Why STR comforters fail faster than residential

Guests sleep on top of the comforter in shoes. Spill drinks on it. Wipe their hands on the corner. Smoke into it (still happens despite no-smoking rules). And — the killer — operators wash comforters far more often than residential users do.

Weekly washing is the single biggest failure driver. Most comforters are designed for 4-6 washes per year, not 50+. The shell fabric breaks down at the seams and the fill clumps.

## Buy two comforters per bed, not one

The single biggest operator hack: always have a backup comforter in storage. When the in-use comforter goes in the laundry, the backup goes on the bed immediately. This means:

- Same-day turnovers don't depend on dryer time
- A wine-spilled comforter doesn't delay check-in
- Each comforter is washed half as often, doubling its life

Budget $200-500 for a "two comforters per king bed" setup. It pays back within 18 months.

## The three quality tiers worth knowing

**Tier 1 — Budget ($80-150):** Target Threshold microfiber, Costco Kirkland down-alternative, Mainstays. Microfiber shell, polyester fill. 1.5-3 year STR life with weekly washing. Right for high-volume turnover units where stains are inevitable.

**Tier 2 — STR sweet spot ($150-250):** Brooklinen Down-Alternative, Buffy Cloud, Threshold Linen. Cotton shell, down-alternative or recycled-PET fill. 3-5 year STR life. The default for stabilized properties.

**Tier 3 — Premium ($250-350+):** Brooklinen Down, Parachute Down Duvet Insert + cover, Boll & Branch. Real white down, sateen cotton shell. 5-7 year STR life.

## What to actually buy (operator picks)

Default pick: **Brooklinen Down-Alternative King** (~$219). Cotton shell, hypoallergenic fill, machine-washable, 3+ year STR life. The sweet spot.

Budget pick: **Costco Kirkland Signature Down Alternative** (~$70). Two-pack for high-turnover STRs. Replace as a set every 18 months.

Premium pick: **Buffy Cloud Comforter** (~$229). Eucalyptus shell, recycled-PET fill, machine-washable. The "feels like a hotel" comforter at a mid-tier price.

Avoid: any down comforter that requires dry cleaning. The cleaning cost across 50 STR launderings exceeds the comforter price within a year.

## Installation realities

Use a duvet cover. Always. The single biggest comforter-life extender — the cover takes the abuse, washes in 30 minutes, and the comforter itself washes 4x/year instead of weekly. Budget $60-150 for a quality cotton or linen duvet cover.

## Lifespan math under STR conditions

| Comforter tier | Residential life | STR (no cover) | STR (with cover) |
|----------------|------------------|----------------|------------------|
| Budget microfiber | 4-6 years | 1.5-3 years | 3-5 years |
| Mid-range cotton | 7-10 years | 3-5 years | 5-7 years |
| Premium down | 10-15 years | 5-7 years | 7-10 years |

## Maintenance that extends life

- Always use a washable duvet cover
- Wash on cold, gentle cycle, with wool dryer balls to prevent clumping
- Dry fully — damp comforters mildew within 24 hours
- Spot-treat stains within the turnover, not after
- Air out once a quarter (hang on a line, no sun)

## Signs it's time

- Visible flat zones where fill has clumped
- Permanent yellowing from sweat
- Stains that survive professional cleaning
- Seam separation or torn shell
- Any odor that survives a wash
