## The right TV for an Airbnb is not the right TV for your living room

A 55-inch smart TV in an STR runs $350-900. The bottom of that range — TCL or Hisense roku-built models — is unironically the right pick for most properties. Stop buying $1500 OLEDs for guest rooms. The TV doesn't need to look great; it needs to be easy to use, hard to break, and easy to replace.

Here's the math: a $400 TV that lasts 5 years in an STR costs $80/year. A $1200 TV that lasts 6 years (because guests are slightly less rough on it but still rough) costs $200/year. The premium tier costs you 2.5x more per year of ownership for marginal picture quality nobody is staying for.

## Why STRs kill TVs faster

TVs in residences last 7-10 years. TVs in STRs last 5-7 years. The failure modes are different:

1. **Cracked screens** — Guests don't crack screens at 4am. Their kids do. Their pets knock the wall mount. Cleaners bump them with a vacuum. Plan on screen damage at some point.
2. **Power surge / electrical events** — STRs are often in vacation areas with worse grids. Surge protectors help; not all TVs are on them.
3. **Remote loss** — Universal remotes go missing every 2-3 turnovers. The TV-mounted manual buttons get hammered to compensate.
4. **HDMI port failure** — Guests plug, unplug, replug fire sticks/Switches/laptops. Ports die.
5. **Smart-platform abandonment** — TCL Roku from 2019 is still updated. Some 2018 budget brand smart platforms have been dropped, leaving the TV "smart" in name only.

## Brand picks for STRs

**Best default — TCL 4-Series (Roku TV)** ($350-450). Roku is the right interface for guests who don't want to learn yet another system. Picture is fine. Build quality is acceptable. If a guest cracks it, you replace at full retail without losing sleep.

**Best budget — Hisense A6 Series** ($300-400). Slightly worse picture than TCL, slightly better deals seasonally. Same Roku-style interface (Hisense Google TV).

**Best mid-tier — TCL 5-Series QLED** ($500-700). If your property is an upper-tier STR (>$300/night ADR) and the TV is genuinely a feature, this is the pick. Real QLED contrast, still cheap enough to swallow if it breaks.

**Avoid:** Sceptre, Onn, RCA, no-name Amazon brands. The picture quality is fine — the failure rate is what kills you. Returns and warranty service on these brands is brutal.

## The mounting decision

Wall-mounted vs. dresser-top is bigger than people realize.

**Wall mount:**
- Looks better in listing photos
- Out of guest reach for accidental tipping
- Harder to steal
- Requires a stud-finder + level + 30 minutes of work
- Limits future TV size flexibility

**Dresser top:**
- 5-minute install
- Easy to swap brands/sizes
- Guests can adjust angle
- Risk of tipping (especially for kids)
- Looks less polished

For most STRs: wall-mount the bedroom TVs and dresser-top the living room TV (so guests can swivel for game nights). Use a tilting wall mount ($30-50) so you can angle for glare.

## What to actually buy and how

A reliable buying playbook:

1. **Watch Amazon and Best Buy for the TCL 4-Series 55"** — drops to $300-350 every 2-3 months, especially Black Friday and Memorial Day weekends.
2. **Check Costco for the same model** — often bundled with extended warranty for $50-80 more, which is genuinely worth it for STR.
3. **Buy 2 if you have multiple properties** — keeps a spare in the supply closet for failure replacement during a guest stay.
4. **Register the warranty immediately** — many TCL/Hisense warranties require activation within 30 days for full coverage.

## Setup script for STR

Don't just plug it in. Configure for guests:

- Set the smart-platform location to the property city (gives accurate weather/news widgets)
- Disable any password-required settings
- Pre-install: Netflix, Hulu, Prime Video, YouTube, Disney+
- DO NOT log in to your personal accounts. Guests should sign in to their own.
- Enable parental controls so kids can't accidentally rack up purchases
- Tape your WiFi password and a Roku troubleshooting card to the back of the dresser

## When to replace

- Visible cracks or dead pixels
- HDMI ports physically damaged or no longer recognizing devices
- The smart platform stops getting updates
- Streaming apps consistently crash or won't open
- Picture has noticeable burn-in or banding
- Older than 6 years and you're already doing a property refresh
