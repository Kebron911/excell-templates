## Cookware in an STR is a 4-year capital item, not an 8-year one

The published 8-year lifespan number is for residential cookware washed by an adult who reads care labels. STR cookware is washed by 100+ different adults over the same period, half of whom will use a metal spatula on the non-stick pan and a third of whom will scrape with a steel scrubber. Real-world STR lifespan is closer to 4 years before the non-stick coating fails or stainless develops permanent etching.

The signal that you're past replacement: any visible non-stick coating peel, any pan that's stained gray-black on the cooking surface even after deep cleaning, or any handle that wobbles. Guests notice. They photograph. The photo ends up in a one-star review.

## What to buy mid-tier ($300–$500)

Skip pure non-stick sets. The coating fails first; you'll be back here in 2 years. Best STR-grade pattern is a hybrid set: stainless steel pieces for searing/boiling, plus 1-2 ceramic non-stick pans for eggs/pancakes that the guest can replace cheaply if it fails.

Specific picks:
- **Cuisinart MultiClad Pro 12-piece** ($400) — tri-ply stainless, oven-safe, dishwasher-friendly. Survives metal utensils.
- **All-Clad D3 Compact 7-piece** ($600) — top of mid-tier. American-made, lifetime warranty actually honored. Pricey but holds up to 5+ years of STR abuse with grace.
- **T-fal Ultimate Hard Anodized 12-piece** ($120) — budget pick. Will need replacement at 3 years, but $120 / 3 = $40/year is acceptable for low-rate properties.

## Always include 1 cast-iron skillet

Add a 10" Lodge cast-iron ($25) to whatever set you buy. Three reasons:

1. **Damage-proof.** Cast iron survives anything; even a guest who heats it dry doesn't ruin it.
2. **Guest-pleaser.** Listing photos with cast iron in the kitchen read as "this place was set up by someone who cooks."
3. **Replacement hedge.** If a guest melts the non-stick pan, cast iron is the backup until you can swap.

## What guests will break/ruin

Documented patterns from STR cookware claims:

- **Metal utensil scratches on non-stick** (coating fails in 6 months after first scratch).
- **Empty pan on hot burner** (ceramic non-stick warps; stainless develops permanent rainbow staining; cast iron is unbothered).
- **Dishwasher abuse** of dishwasher-safe pieces (handles loosen on tri-ply over time even when label says "safe").

Mitigation: include a printed "kitchen care" 3x5 card next to the stove. Most guests honor it; some ignore it; the card is your evidence in resolution-center claims.

## When to file a damage claim

For a claim under $50 (one ruined non-stick pan), don't bother — the resolution-center friction costs you more than the pan. For $200+ (a ruined full set), file with a current Amazon/Cuisinart receipt as evidence. Airbnb adjusts based on lifespan, so you'll see roughly 60–70% of the new replacement cost. Keep all original receipts in a folder; they're the only thing the platform considers as cost basis.
