## What you actually pay to replace a mini-fridge in an Airbnb

The honest range for a mini-fridge in a short-term rental is $120-400. The cheap end gets you a 1.6-2.4 cu ft dorm-style unit with an icebox shelf. The high end gets you a 4.4-4.6 cu ft true two-door with a real freezer compartment.

Most STR operators land between $160 and $260 — enough capacity for a couple's bar setup, snack drawer, or studio kitchenette without taking up full-fridge floor space.

## Why STR mini-fridges fail faster than residential

Mini-fridges in a bedroom or studio run nearly continuously and get unplugged hard between bookings (cleaners pulling the cord rather than turning off at the thermostat). They're moved during deep cleans, which is the single fastest way to kill a compressor — tilting a fridge and plugging it back in within an hour destroys the cooling line.

The other STR-specific killer: guests stash warm beer six-packs in batches, which forces the compressor to run continuously for 12+ hours.

## The three quality tiers worth knowing

**Tier 1 — Budget ($120-180):** Magic Chef 1.7 cu ft, Frigidaire EFR176, Galanz 2.7 cu ft. Single door, icebox shelf only. 4-6 year STR life. Right for a wet bar or studio with a separate freezer.

**Tier 2 — STR sweet spot ($180-280):** Insignia 2.6 cu ft, Frigidaire 3.1 cu ft two-door, GE 4.4 cu ft. Real freezer, adjustable shelves, reversible door. 7-9 year STR life. The right default for bedroom-suite STRs or backup kitchens.

**Tier 3 — Premium ($280-400+):** NewAir AB-1200, Whynter BR-128WS beverage fridge, Frigidaire EFMIS149. Glass-front beverage models for premium bar setups. 8-10 year life with stable thermal cycling.

## What to actually buy (operator picks)

Default pick: **Frigidaire EFR331-BLUE 3.2 cu ft two-door** (~$200). Real freezer for ice trays, two cans wide on the door shelf, reversible hinge for any room layout.

Beverage-bar pick: **NewAir AB-1200 126-can** (~$300). If your listing leans into "bar setup," guests notice. The glass door doubles as marketing.

Avoid: any "off-brand" $89 dorm fridge from Amazon. They die in 18 months and the warranty is unenforceable.

## Installation realities

DIY in 5 minutes — but two things matter:

- Let it sit upright for 4 hours after delivery before plugging in. Refrigerant settles. Skipping this kills compressors.
- Don't enclose it. Mini-fridges need 2 inches of clearance on the sides and 3 inches behind for heat dissipation. Inside a built-in cabinet without ventilation, lifespan drops to 3 years.

| Budget single-door | 8-12 years | 4-6 years |
| Mid-range two-door | 12-14 years | 7-9 years |
| Premium beverage | 12-15 years | 8-10 years |

## Maintenance that extends life

- Vacuum coils (back or bottom) every 6 months — biggest single life-extender
- Wipe door gasket monthly; replace if it doesn't seal a dollar bill
- Defrost manually if frost exceeds 1/4 inch
- Set thermostat to 37-40F, not coldest setting

## Signs it's time

- Running constantly without cycling off
- Warm zones inside the cabinet
- Compressor noise getting louder over weeks
- Visible rust around the freezer compartment
- Door no longer seals (after gasket replacement)
