## What you actually pay for a work desk in an Airbnb

Honest range: $150-600. IKEA Linnmon + Adils legs at $150, IKEA Bekant at $300, Branch standing desk at $400-550, Article-tier solid wood at $500-600.

Most STR operators land at **$250-400** — large enough surface for a 15" laptop + external monitor, sturdy enough to survive guest abuse, and clean enough to photograph as a "work-from-here" space.

A real desk (not a console table relabeled "workspace") is now a baseline guest expectation, especially in business-travel and long-stay markets.

## Why a real desk is now required in STR

The 2020-2024 work-from-anywhere shift made desk-quality a search filter on Airbnb. "Dedicated workspace" is a checkbox guests filter on, and the photo of the workspace converts business travelers at materially higher rates.

A wobbly fold-up table or a 24" surface that won't hold a monitor reads as "not a real workspace" and disqualifies your listing from the business-travel segment — which is the highest-ADR, lowest-damage guest segment.

## STR failure modes

- Cheap pressboard tops chip at the edges where guests slide laptops
- Adjustable-height desks fail at the motor or screw mechanism after 18 months of moves
- Drawer slides fail when guests overload with their own items
- Cable management gets ripped out when guests plug/unplug devices
- Desk wobble shows up in Zoom calls and gets reviewed

## What to actually buy

**Default pick:** IKEA Bekant 47"x23" with stable T-leg frame (~$259). 47" surface fits a laptop + 27" external. Steel frame, no wobble. Available in white, oak, black.

**Standing desk pick:** Branch Duo Standing Desk 48"x30" (~$449). Electric height-adjust, 3-button preset, cable tray included. Long-stay and digital-nomad-market match.

**Budget pick:** IKEA Linnmon 47"x24" + Adils legs (~$70). Adequate for back-bedroom workspace. Replace every 4-5 yr.

**Premium pick:** Article Madera solid oak 55"x28" (~$549). Reads as designer-grade in photos. Worth it on photo-driven listings.

**Avoid:** Wayfair "computer desk" units under $120. Pressboard tops + plastic frame. Wobbles by month 6.

## Accessories that complete the workspace

- Desk chair (sold separately, $80-300 — Herman Miller used or IKEA Markus)
- 27" monitor on adjustable arm (~$200 monitor + $50 arm) — converts the workspace into "actually usable"
- Cable management tray ($25-40)
- Desk lamp ($30-80 — see [floor-lamp](/replace/floor-lamp/) for related lighting)
- Power strip with USB-C ($35-50)

The desk alone is $250. The fully-outfitted "remote work" workspace is $500-700 total. Worth every dollar in business-travel markets.

## Lifespan math

| Tier | Residential life | STR life |
|---|---|---|
| IKEA Linnmon / Adils | 6-8 yr | 3-5 yr |
| IKEA Bekant / mid-tier | 10-12 yr | 5-7 yr |
| Solid-wood Article / Crate | 15-20 yr | 8-10 yr |
| Electric standing (Branch / UPLIFT) | 8-12 yr | 5-7 yr (motor is the limit) |

## Maintenance + signs to replace

- Re-tighten bolts every 6 months (desks loosen from laptop banging)
- Re-glue chipped veneer edges with wood glue + clamp
- Replace electric standing desks when motor stalls at any height (repair > 60% of replacement)
- Replace any desk with visible wobble, chipped corners, or stuck drawers
