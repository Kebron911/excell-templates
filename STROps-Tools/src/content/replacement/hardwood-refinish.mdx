## What you actually pay for a hardwood refinish in an Airbnb

Honest range: $3-8 per sq ft. DIY rental sander + materials: $1-2/sq ft (plus a weekend). Standard pro sand + stain + 3-coat poly: $3.50-5/sq ft. Dustless system with premium finish: $5-8/sq ft.

A 400 sq ft living room refinish = $1,400-2,000 pro. A 1,000 sq ft whole-floor refinish = $3,500-5,500. Compared to full hardwood replacement at $12-18/sq ft, refinishing is 1/3 to 1/4 the cost.

## Refinish vs replace — the breakpoint

**Refinish** when:
- Floor is real hardwood (not engineered with thin veneer)
- Wear is in the finish, not the wood (scratches, dullness, light cupping)
- Boards are still flat (no severe gapping or cupping)
- Floor has 4-6 refinishes remaining (most 3/4" hardwood floors support 6-10 lifetime refinishes; engineered floors support 1-2)

**Replace** when:
- Boards are warped, gapped, or rotting
- Water damage extends to subfloor
- Floor is engineered hardwood with < 3mm wear layer
- Refinish has been done 6+ times already

For STR, refinishing every 5-8 years extends a floor's serviceable life by decades.

## Dustless vs traditional sanding

**Traditional sanding ($3.50-5/sq ft):** drum sander + edger. Creates massive dust. Owner / guests can't be in unit for 3-5 days. Cheaper.

**Dustless system ($5-8/sq ft):** sander with HEPA vacuum attachment. Captures 95%+ of dust. Unit can be turned in 24-48 hours. The **STR-default** because guest turnover is the constraint, not labor cost.

If you're refinishing between bookings, dustless is mandatory. The dust from traditional sanding lingers for 2-3 weeks in HVAC and on every horizontal surface.

## Stain choice that hides STR wear

The wrong stain shows every scratch, dust mote, and footprint. The right stain hides 12 months of guest abuse.

**Best STR stains (hide wear):**
- Mid-tone browns (Minwax Special Walnut, Provincial)
- Gray-brown blends (Bona Driftwood)
- Natural / clear (lets the wood character hide minor scratches)

**Worst STR stains (show wear):**
- Ebony / Jacobean (every speck of dust visible)
- Pure white / blonde (yellow stains from spills are obvious)
- High-gloss finishes (any scratch is dramatically visible)

Use matte or satin finish, not semi-gloss. Matte hides scratches; gloss highlights them.

## What to actually buy

**Stain:** Minwax Special Walnut (DIY) or Bona DriFast Special Walnut (pro). $20-40/gallon.

**Sealer + finish:** Bona Mega ONE (water-based, low-VOC, dries in 2 hours per coat). $90-110/gallon. 3 coats over 8 hours = floor walkable in 24 hours.

**For traditional pro:** Minwax oil-based polyurethane (cheaper, slower cure, longer-lasting finish). Adds 4-7 day cure time.

**Avoid:** any contractor who uses solvent-based shellac under a poly finish — non-standard, hard to recoat in future refinishes.

## Installation realities — the timeline

Day 1: clear furniture, remove baseboards (optional), sand to bare wood (3 grits: 36, 60, 100).
Day 2: vacuum, stain, allow 4-12 hr dry.
Day 3: 1st coat poly, light sand, 2nd coat poly.
Day 4: 3rd coat poly, allow 24 hr cure before walking, 7 days before furniture.

Full process: 4-5 days from "guests out" to "guests in." Budget the calendar gap.

## Lifespan math

| Floor type | Residential refinish cycle | STR refinish cycle |
|---|---|---|
| Solid 3/4" oak | 10-15 yr | 5-8 yr |
| Solid 3/4" maple | 12-18 yr | 6-9 yr |
| Engineered (3mm+ veneer) | 15-20 yr lifetime, 1-2 refinishes | 8-12 yr lifetime |
| Engineered (under 3mm veneer) | Cannot refinish — replace |

## Signs it's time to refinish

- Finish is dull regardless of cleaning
- Surface scratches visible in raking light
- Light cupping at board edges
- Water spots / stain rings that won't lift
- Original finish 8+ years old
