## What you actually pay for a small drywall patch

Honest range: $40-150. DIY materials: $15-30 (patch kit, spackle, sandpaper, primer, paint touchup). Pro single-patch service call: $100-200 minimum (most contractors won't come for under $100). Handyman batch (5-10 patches in one visit): $200-400 total = $30-50/patch.

A nail-hole or small drywall punch (under 2"): DIY with a $5 spackle tube + 5 minutes.

A doorknob-size hole (2-4"): DIY with a $12 mesh patch kit + 30 min spread over 2 days (drying time).

A fist-size hole (4-8"): borderline DIY. Patch kit with backing strips + 1 hour active work + 2-3 day drying cycle.

## DIY breakpoint vs sheet replacement

A **patch** works when:
- Hole is under 8" in any dimension
- Damage doesn't go through to studs / electrical / plumbing
- Wall surface around it is intact
- You can match existing paint

A **sheet replacement** is needed when:
- Hole is larger than 8" or compound (multiple holes in one wall section)
- Water damage extends beyond the visible hole
- The patch area is over a stud cavity (no backing)
- More than 4-5 patches in the same wall (just replace the section)

See [drywall sheet replace](/replace/drywall-sheet-replace/) for the larger job.

## Why STRs see more patches than residential

Guest movement = nail-hole damage. Common sources:

- Suitcases knocking corners (especially basement / staircase walls)
- Door handles slamming into walls (no door stop installed)
- Pictures hung by guests (sometimes), then taken down (always)
- Vacuum / mop handles
- Kids' toys
- Furniture moved during stays

A 3-month-old STR rarely has wall damage. An 18-month-old STR averages 4-8 patches needed at the next deep-clean. Build patch+touch into the maintenance schedule, not the emergency callout.

## The 1-hour spackle patch workflow

For nail-hole or small punch (under 2"):

1. Clear debris from hole
2. Lightly sand around hole (60-grit, 30 sec)
3. Spread spackle with putty knife, slightly overfilled
4. Wait 30 min (lightweight) or 2 hr (regular spackle)
5. Sand smooth with 220-grit
6. Prime with stain-blocking primer
7. Touch-up paint

Total active time: 8-10 min. Total elapsed: 1-2 hours.

For a 2-4" hole (doorknob):
- 3M Patch Plus Primer mesh kit ($8-12)
- Apply mesh patch, spackle over with putty knife
- 2-3 thin layers, sanding between each
- Total active: 30 min. Total elapsed: 4-8 hours.

## What to actually buy

**Default patch kit:** 3M Patch Plus Primer 4"x4" (~$12 for 5-pack). Self-adhesive mesh + spackle in one tube. Self-primer included. The single best DIY tool for STR wall repairs.

**Larger patches:** Hyde Drywall Patch Repair Kit 6"x6" (~$15). Aluminum mesh + spackle.

**Spackle:** DAP DryDex (color-changing — pink when wet, white when dry). $7/tub. Removes the guesswork on drying.

**Primer for touch-ups:** Zinsser Bullseye 1-2-3 stain-blocking ($12 quart). Prevents the patched area from "flashing" through the topcoat.

**Touch-up paint:** keep a labeled mason jar with original paint color in storage. Sherwin-Williams customer accounts store color codes — but the actual paint, mixed, is what you need on hand.

## Avoid these patch mistakes

- Skipping primer — the patch always flashes through the paint
- Single thick spackle coat — cracks while drying
- Sanding when wet — gouges the spackle
- Painting only the patch — visible "halo" of fresh paint against aged wall

The right approach: paint the patch + 2-3 ft of surrounding wall, feathering the edges. For visible walls, repaint the full wall.

## Lifespan math

| Repair type | Life of repair |
|---|---|
| DIY spackle nail hole | Indefinite (until painted over) |
| Mesh patch kit | 10-15 yr if done correctly |
| Pro patch with full prep | 25+ yr |
| Sheet replacement | 30+ yr (life of drywall) |

## Signs to escalate from patch to sheet replacement

- More than 5 patches in one wall
- Patches cracking after 6-12 months (substrate problem)
- Water damage discovered behind the patch
- Patches no longer disappearing under paint (texture mismatch)
