## What you actually pay for vinyl plank flooring in an Airbnb

Honest installed range: $4-8 per sq ft. Materials alone: $2-5/sq ft. Installation: $1.50-3.50/sq ft. Underlayment + transitions: $0.50-1/sq ft.

A 200 sq ft bedroom = $800-1,600 installed. A 1,200 sq ft full-house re-floor = $4,800-9,600. Compared to hardwood at $12-18/sq ft installed, LVP is the budget-conscious STR default.

## LVP vs SPC vs WPC — what they actually mean

The flooring industry uses these acronyms inconsistently. The real distinctions:

**LVP (luxury vinyl plank):** flexible vinyl with foam underlayment. 4-6mm thick. Softer underfoot. Cheaper ($2-3/sq ft material). More dent-prone.

**SPC (stone-plastic composite):** rigid limestone-core plank. 4-7mm thick. Hardest, most water-resistant, most dent-resistant. The **STR default** ($3-5/sq ft material).

**WPC (wood-plastic composite):** wood-core, softer than SPC, slightly warmer underfoot. 5-8mm thick. Premium ($4-7/sq ft material).

For STR: pick **SPC** unless you have a specific reason for WPC (warmth in unheated cabins).

## Click-lock DIY vs glue-down

**Click-lock floating ($2-5/sq ft DIY):** snaps together, sits on underlayment, no glue. DIY-able in a weekend for a 200 sq ft room. The right choice for room-by-room replacement.

**Glue-down ($3-6/sq ft pro):** adheres to subfloor. More stable for high-traffic + temperature swings. Required over radiant heat. Pro-only.

For STR, **click-lock is almost always the right move** — DIY-capable, replaceable plank-by-plank when a guest damages a section.

## What to actually buy

**Default pick:** LifeProof Sterling Oak SPC rigid core, 7mm (Home Depot, ~$3.49/sq ft). 50-yr residential warranty, lifetime against water, click-lock. Photographs as real oak.

**Budget pick:** Pergo Outlast+ LVP, 6mm (~$2.89/sq ft). Decent for back-bedrooms in low-ADR units.

**Premium pick:** COREtec Pro Plus 7mm SPC with attached cork underlayment (~$4.79/sq ft). Quietest underfoot, lifetime warranty. Worth it in luxury listings.

**Avoid:** Amazon / Wayfair "LVP" under $2/sq ft. Often 3mm with thin wear layer; dents from chair legs and luggage wheels.

## Installation realities

DIY (200 sq ft room): 8-12 hours over a weekend. Tools: tapping block, pull bar, jigsaw, T-square. $100-150 in tools if you don't own them; reusable for the next room.

Pro install: $1.50-3.50/sq ft. Includes subfloor prep, transitions, baseboard removal/reinstall.

Critical detail: **underlayment**. Most SPC has attached underlayment. If not, add a 2mm IIC-rated cork or foam underlayment ($0.30-0.50/sq ft) — required for sound dampening in upstairs units, mandatory in many condo associations.

## Wear-layer matters

The wear layer (top clear coating) determines how long the floor survives STR foot traffic:

- **6 mil:** residential light-use only. Fails in STR within 2-3 yr.
- **12 mil:** light commercial. STR-acceptable for low-traffic rooms.
- **20 mil+:** commercial. The **STR sweet spot**. 8-12 yr STR life.
- **28 mil:** heavy commercial. Overkill for STR.

LifeProof Sterling Oak = 22 mil. Pergo Outlast+ = 12 mil. Check the wear layer spec, not just the price.

## Lifespan math

| Wear layer | Residential life | STR life |
|---|---|---|
| 6 mil | 8-12 yr | 2-4 yr |
| 12 mil | 15-20 yr | 5-8 yr |
| 20 mil+ | 25+ yr | 10-15 yr |

## Maintenance + signs to replace

- Damp mop with vinyl-safe cleaner monthly (no wet mop, no steam)
- Felt pads under all furniture legs
- Replace damaged planks individually — keep 1-2 boxes in storage
- Replace full floor when: 10%+ of planks have visible wear, lock joints failing, water damage at seams
