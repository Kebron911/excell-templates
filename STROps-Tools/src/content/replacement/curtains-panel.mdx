## What you actually pay for curtain panels in an Airbnb

Honest range per window (2 panels): $40-200. IKEA RITVA pair at $40-50, Target Threshold blackout pair at $80-120, Pottery Barn linen + blackout liner at $150-200.

Most STR operators land at **$80-130 per window for blackout panels**. A 3BR property with 10-15 windows needs $800-1,800 in curtains.

## Why curtains matter alongside blinds

Most STR setups need both. Blinds = privacy + light control during the day. Curtains = blackout + thermal insulation + style. Bedrooms with only blinds get "the bedroom wasn't dark enough" reviews even on the best cellular shades.

Curtains also visually soften a room in listing photos. Bare windows + blinds reads as a hospital. Add curtains and the room reads as a home.

## Blackout vs light-filtering

**Blackout panels ($80-150/pair):** triple-weave or polyester with blackout liner. Block 95-100% of light. Mandatory in bedrooms.

**Light-filtering ($40-80/pair):** linen or cotton-look polyester. Diffuse light, provide minimal privacy. Right for living rooms and dining areas.

**Sheer ($30-60/pair):** for layering only. Doesn't function as standalone in any room.

The biggest STR mistake: buying "blackout" panels that are actually thermal-lined. Real blackout has a coated polyester back you can feel — opaque to direct sunlight.

## STR-specific failure modes

- Polyester panels fade in sun-exposed rooms within 3 yr
- Cheap rod-pocket headers stretch and sag from repeated guest pulls
- Grommets pull out from the fabric (rare on quality panels, common on cheap ones)
- Linen panels wrinkle and need re-pressing between long stays
- Blackout liner separates from the main panel at the seams

## What to actually buy

**Default pick:** Target Threshold Aruba blackout grommet pair (~$70/pair). Real blackout, grommet header (smooth pulling), washable. 4-5 yr STR life.

**Budget pick:** IKEA RITVA pair 57"x98" (~$50/pair). Cotton, not blackout — pair with a blackout liner ($25) for $75 total blackout setup.

**Premium pick:** Pottery Barn Belgian Flax Linen with blackout liner (~$179/pair). Premium texture, real linen. Reads as boutique-hotel in photos.

**Avoid:** Amazon "blackout curtains" under $30/pair. They are thin polyester with a foam coating that smells like chemicals out of the bag and peels in 6 months.

## Curtain rods + hanging hardware

The rod is half the install cost. Budget:

- IKEA RÄCKA rod + bracket ($15-25 per window)
- Target metal rod with finials ($25-45)
- Wide-pocket / double-rod (curtain + sheer): $30-60

Hang the rod **6-8 inches above the window frame** and **6-12 inches wider on each side**. This single trick makes windows look 30% larger in photos. Most rentals hang rods directly above the window — the photo loses height.

## Installation realities

15-20 min/window DIY. Drywall anchors required if rod brackets land outside the studs (almost always). For wide rods (single 96"+), use a center support bracket — wind from open windows + guest pulls bows long rods.

## Lifespan math

| Tier | Residential life | STR life |
|---|---|---|
| IKEA / budget cotton | 6-8 yr | 3-4 yr |
| Target Threshold blackout | 8-10 yr | 4-5 yr |
| Pottery Barn / premium linen | 12-15 yr | 6-8 yr |

## Maintenance + signs to replace

- Wash polyester panels every 6 months; spot-clean linen
- Inspect grommets and header for tears quarterly
- Replace when: fade visible from across the room, blackout liner peeling, header torn, grommets pulled through
- Refresh the listing visually by swapping curtains every 4-5 yr even if functional
