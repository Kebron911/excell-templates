## What you actually pay for a full drywall sheet replacement

Honest range: $150-450 per 4x8 sheet replaced. Materials: $15-40 (USG Sheetrock 1/2" sheet, joint compound, tape, screws). Labor: $100-350 depending on access, finishing level, and trade coordination.

A 4x8 patch (~32 sq ft) is the standard unit. A full wall replacement (8x10 = ~80 sq ft = 3 sheets coverage): $400-900. A water-damaged ceiling replacement in a bathroom: $300-600 for the drywall + $200-500 for trade coordination (electrical / plumbing).

## When sheet replacement is the right call

A **patch fails** and you need full replacement when:

- Hole is larger than ~8" or extensive (multiple holes within 2 ft of each other)
- Water damage extends into the drywall (sagging, soft spots, brown staining that bleeds through paint)
- Mold detected behind the wall
- Texture damage that can't be matched (orange peel, knockdown, smooth)
- Demo for plumbing / electrical access already required

For STR, the most common trigger is **water damage** — a leak from an upstairs unit, a roof failure, or a slow plumbing leak that wasn't caught for weeks.

## The hidden costs — trade coordination

Sheet replacement is **rarely just drywall work**. The realistic scope:

1. **Demo** — old drywall removed, debris hauled. $50-100/sheet.
2. **Investigation** — what caused the damage? Plumbing? Roof? HVAC condensation? $100-300 for the inspection.
3. **Repair the underlying cause** — separate trade. Plumber ($150-500), roofer ($200-800), HVAC ($150-400).
4. **Insulation replacement** — if wet ($30-50/sheet area).
5. **Vapor barrier replacement** — if exterior wall ($20-40/sheet area).
6. **Drywall install + tape + 3 coats mud + sand** — $200-400/sheet pro.
7. **Texture match** — $50-150 if texture (orange peel, knockdown).
8. **Primer + paint** — full wall, not just patch ($150-400/wall).

Total for a single water-damaged ceiling section in a bathroom: easily **$1,200-2,500** even though the drywall portion is $300.

## DIY vs pro reality

Sheet replacement is **pro-only territory** for STR operators. The reasons:

- Joint finishing requires 3-4 days of mud/sand cycles
- Texture matching is a learned skill — DIY usually looks "patched"
- Trade coordination (plumbing/electrical) creates liability if mis-sequenced
- Mold remediation has legal disclosure implications

A handyman with drywall experience can do single-sheet replacements at $150-250/sheet labor. A full drywall contractor handles multi-sheet jobs at $300-400/sheet but includes finish-grade results.

## Finishing levels — what to spec

Drywall finish levels are a real industry spec. Match to the room:

- **Level 1:** tape only. Garages, mechanical rooms.
- **Level 2:** tape + 1 thin coat. Behind cabinets.
- **Level 3:** tape + 2 coats mud, sanded. Walls receiving heavy texture.
- **Level 4 (STR default):** tape + 3 coats mud, fully sanded smooth. **Standard residential / STR walls.**
- **Level 5:** Level 4 + skim coat across entire surface. Premium properties with glossy or critical-light finishes.

Spec **Level 4** in writing on any STR drywall work. Some contractors will default to Level 3 to save labor and you'll see joint flashing through paint.

## What to actually buy / spec

**Drywall:** USG Sheetrock 1/2" 4x8 (~$15-18/sheet). 5/8" for ceilings. Moisture-resistant green board for bathrooms.

**Joint compound:** USG All-Purpose ($15/bucket).

**Tape:** USG paper tape (not mesh — paper is stronger on butt joints).

**Screws:** drywall screws 1-1/4" coarse-thread.

**Texture match (orange peel):** Homax Pro Grade aerosol spray ($12-18).

## Mold remediation — STR liability

If water damage is older than 48-72 hours, assume mold. STR mold is a **disclosure issue** in many states and a 1-star review magnet.

The right sequence:
1. Test affected area before demo ($50-150 mold testing kit, or $200-400 pro inspection).
2. If positive: contain the area before demo (plastic sheeting + negative air).
3. Demo + dispose as contaminated waste.
4. Apply mold-resistant primer (KILZ Mold & Mildew).
5. Replace with mold-resistant drywall (USG Mold Tough green board).

Skipping these creates a 2-year-out callback when mold returns.

## Lifespan math

| Repair quality | Life of repair |
|---|---|
| DIY patch over water damage | 6-18 months (returns) |
| Pro single-sheet replacement | 25-30 yr |
| Full wall replacement w/ mold remediation | 30+ yr |

## Signs you need sheet replacement (not patch)

- Sag visible in ceiling
- Soft spots when pushing on wall
- Brown water staining returning after primer
- Visible mold or musty smell
- Multiple patches in close proximity
- Demo already happened for trade work
