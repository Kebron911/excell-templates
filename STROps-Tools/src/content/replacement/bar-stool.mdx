## What you actually pay for a bar stool in an Airbnb

Honest range per stool: $60-250. IKEA backless metal at $60-80, World Market upholstered counter-height at $130-180, Article mid-century at $200-250.

Most STR operators land at $90-140/stool. A 2-stool kitchen island needs $180-280; a 4-stool full counter setup runs $360-560 in stools alone.

## Counter-height vs bar-height — measure first

This is the single most common bar-stool mistake. The wrong height makes the stool unusable.

- **Counter-height stools: 24-26"** seat height. Standard for kitchen islands and most STR counters (36" counter height).
- **Bar-height stools: 28-30"** seat height. For dedicated home bars or pub-style tables (42" surface).
- **Spectator-height: 32-34"** seat height. Game rooms with raised back bars. Rare in STR.

Measure your counter from floor to underside of countertop. Stool seat should be 10-12" below the counter. Anything closer = guests can't fit their legs.

## Backed vs backless

**Backed stools ($120-250):** comfortable for sustained sitting (work-from-island, long breakfasts). Photo better. Eat more visual space.

**Backless stools ($60-130):** tuck completely under the counter — disappear when not in use. Photo cleaner (open kitchen). Less comfortable beyond 30 min sitting.

In small studios + 1BRs, **backless wins** for the floor-space-feels-bigger effect. In larger properties, **backed wins** for guest comfort.

## Weight rating in group rentals

Bar stools fail more frequently than dining chairs because guests sit, swivel, lean, and put feet on the leg rail. For 4+ guest properties, prioritize:

- **300 lb weight rating** minimum
- **Steel frame** over pressboard
- **Welded joints** over screw-only construction

A failed bar stool with a guest mid-cocktail is both an injury risk and a 1-star review.

## What to actually buy

**Default pick:** World Market Jozy counter-height upholstered (~$159). Backed, 24" seat, 300-lb rated, real wood frame. Photographs well.

**Budget pick:** IKEA FRANKLIN 25" backless folding bar stool (~$65). Stacks/folds for storage. Right for compact urban units.

**Photo-driven pick:** Article Sede leather counter stool (~$219). Mid-century walnut + tan leather. The "designed kitchen" upgrade.

**Premium pick:** Industry West Atlas counter stool (~$249). Steel-frame welded, swivel, 350-lb rating. Closest to commercial-grade.

**Avoid:** Amazon "set of 2 bar stools" under $120. Pressboard seats, screw-only construction. Fails within 18 months of STR use.

## Installation + assembly

Most bar stools ship flat-pack. Assembly: 20-30 min per stool. Use thread-locker (Loctite blue) on the leg-to-seat bolts — STR vibration loosens factory bolts by month 6.

Felt floor protectors on the leg tips: $5/stool. Prevents kitchen-floor gouging when guests drag stools.

## Lifespan math

| Tier | Residential life | STR life |
|---|---|---|
| IKEA / budget metal | 7-9 yr | 3-4 yr |
| Mid-tier upholstered (World Market) | 10-12 yr | 4-6 yr |
| Premium welded steel (Industry West) | 15-20 yr | 8-12 yr |

## Maintenance + signs to replace

- Re-tighten leg bolts every 4-6 months
- Replace felt floor protectors annually
- Scotchgard upholstered seats every 18 months
- Replace when: wobble that re-tightening doesn't fix, cracked seat, weld failure, swivel mechanism stuck
