## What it costs to replace a 65" smart TV in an Airbnb

Honest installed range: $500-1400. Budget Hisense / TCL Roku $480-650; mid-tier TCL QLED or Sony X-series $700-1000; premium Samsung Q-series or LG OLED $1100-1400+.

For STR, the right default is a **mid-tier 65" 4K QLED with smart platform** ($650-850). Big enough that "65" TV" makes the listing description; cheap enough that replacement after 4-6 years doesn't sting; smart-platform integration means guests can sign into Netflix/HBO/Hulu without setup help.

## Why STR TVs fail differently

Residential TVs last 8-12 years. STR TVs average 4-7. The killers:
- **Guests sign into accounts.** Streaming services accumulate; smart-TV memory fills up; performance degrades 2-3 years in.
- **Power surges during turnovers.** Cleaners unplug for deep cleans; the inrush current when reconnecting stresses the power board.
- **Sun damage.** TVs near windows fade or burn. Plasma was worst; OLED is also vulnerable. QLED is most sun-resistant.
- **Physical impact.** Bumped corners during cleaning, kids hitting the screen with toys. Replacement screens cost 60-80% of replacement.

## What to actually buy

**Default pick:** Hisense U7K 65" (~$679). 4K QLED, Roku TV smart platform (clean UI, signs guests out automatically with Roku Guest Mode). 120 Hz panel — good for sports markets. 4-7 year STR life.

**Budget pick:** TCL 65S455 65" (~$479). 4K LED, Google TV. Acceptable picture, 3-5 year STR life. Right when stocking 3+ TVs across a property.

**Premium pick:** Samsung QN65Q60D (~$999). 4K QLED, Tizen smart platform. Anti-reflective coating handles sunny rooms well. 5-8 year STR life.

**Avoid:** OLED in any STR with windows or pool exposure — pixel burn is real on static news/sports channels. Save OLED for primaries.

## Installation realities

- **Wall mount or stand:** TVs ship with feet for tabletop. A wall mount is $40-100 + 30 min DIY or $80-150 install.
- **HDMI / power cable runs in wall:** If guests can see cables, the room looks unfinished. In-wall power and HDMI kit $40-60.
- **Streaming-stick fallback:** Even with a smart TV, keep a $30 Roku stick on hand for guest issues. Smart platforms drift; sticks are replaceable.

## Guest-mode setup (the move that prevents 80% of TV calls)

Roku TVs have Guest Mode: signs out all streaming accounts after a set inactivity period or at a scheduled time. Enable this. It eliminates:
- Guests signing in and forgetting to sign out
- Next guest seeing the previous guest's recommendations
- Slow performance from 12+ stored sign-ins

Samsung and LG don't have a true guest mode — use a streaming stick instead (which can be reset with a remote button hold).

## Lifespan math under STR conditions

| Tier | Residential life | STR life |
|---|---|---|
| Budget LED | 7–9 yr | 3–5 yr |
| Mid-tier QLED | 9–12 yr | 5–7 yr |
| Premium QLED / mini-LED | 10–14 yr | 6–9 yr |
| OLED (full-time STR) | 10–14 yr | 4–7 yr (burn) |

## Signs it's time

- Dead pixels visible from 6 feet
- Backlight bleeding (clouds of brighter areas at corners)
- Vertical lines (panel failure — non-repairable, replace)
- Smart platform unresponsive even after factory reset
- Sound delay (audio sync drift)
