## What you actually pay for a 50" TV in an Airbnb

Honest installed range: $280-700. Builder-grade TCL 4-Series at $280-330, mid-tier Hisense U6 at $380-500, premium LG QNED or Sony X80 at $550-700.

Most STR operators land on the **Hisense U6 ($430)** — Roku-free smart OS that doesn't trap guests in a Samsung account prompt, 60Hz refresh rate that's fine for travel + streaming, and bright enough for a sun-lit living room.

A 50" is the right size for studios, primary bedrooms in 2BR units, and any room where viewing distance is under 8 ft. Bigger isn't always better — at 6 ft viewing distance, a 50" reads as cinematic.

## Why STR TVs fail faster than residential

Residential TVs run 4-6 hours a day. STR TVs run 12-16 hours — guests leave them on for "ambient sound" all day, then sleep with them on. That's 3x the panel hours per calendar year.

Failure modes: HDMI ports loosen from repeated cable swaps, the smart-OS gets bricked when a guest factory-resets it trying to log into their Netflix, and the panel develops uniformity issues (dim corners, banding) at year 3-4.

## Quality tiers worth knowing

**Budget ($280-380):** TCL 4-Series, Vizio V-Series. 4K, basic HDR. Roku built-in (TCL) or SmartCast (Vizio). 4-5 yr STR life. Right for back-bedroom or kitchen TVs.

**Mid-tier ($380-550):** Hisense U6 / U7, TCL 5-Series, LG UQ75. Quantum-dot or local-dimming. Better off-angle viewing. 5-6 yr STR life. The default.

**Premium ($550-700):** LG QNED, Sony X80, Samsung Q60. Full-array local dimming. 6-7 yr STR life. Worth it in primary living rooms on photo-driven listings.

## What to actually buy

**Default pick:** Hisense 50U6K (~$430). Quantum-dot panel, Google TV OS (guests sign into their own accounts, no carryover), 4 HDMI ports.

**Budget pick:** TCL 50S4 (~$298). Roku TV. Guests already know the interface. Cheapest TV that doesn't look cheap.

**Photo-driven listing pick:** LG 50QNED80 (~$649). Bezel reads premium in listing photos. Real upgrade if your listing leads with the living-room shot.

**Avoid:** any TV under $250 — typically 1080p panels marketed as "4K-ready," and the smart OS is unsupported in 12 months.

## Installation + mounting

- VESA mount: 200x200 or 300x300 — confirm before buying bracket
- Tilt mount: $25-40, hides the wall connection
- Full-motion mount: $45-80, useful in corner rooms
- Cable management box ($15) — guests photograph clean walls
- Pro mount install: $100-150 with stud-finder + level

## Lifespan math

| Tier | Residential life | STR life |
|---|---|---|
| Budget (TCL 4 / Vizio V) | 7-9 yr | 4-5 yr |
| Mid (Hisense U6 / TCL 5) | 8-10 yr | 5-6 yr |
| Premium (LG QNED / Sony) | 10-12 yr | 6-7 yr |

## Maintenance + signs to replace

- Dust the vents every 6 months (overheating shortens panel life)
- Power-cycle weekly to clear smart-OS memory leaks
- Factory-reset between long-term stays
- Replace when: panel uniformity issues, HDMI port no longer holding cable, smart OS no longer receiving updates
