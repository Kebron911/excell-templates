## Sofa is the most-photographed and most-broken item in your STR

Every listing photo includes the sofa. Every guest sits, stains, and occasionally jumps on the sofa. Replacement-cost ranges go from "embarrassing" ($600 budget) to "I cannot believe a guest broke this" ($3,500 premium), and the right pick depends on whether your nightly rate justifies the hit.

The published 7-year lifespan number is for a residential sofa. STR reality: 5–6 years on a $800 sofa, 8–10 years on a $2,000+ performance-fabric sofa. The math usually favors going slightly above mid-tier and staying there longer.

## What to buy in the STR sweet spot ($1,200–$2,000)

Three features matter:

1. **Performance fabric** (Sunbrella, Crypton, or equivalent). Non-negotiable. Spills bead and wipe; pet hair doesn't embed; UV doesn't fade. Pay the upcharge.
2. **Removable washable covers** OR **pet-friendly stain-resistant fabric.** Both work. Removable covers let you wash; resistant fabric lets you spot-clean. Pick one based on whether your laundry capacity is at a property level or a service level.
3. **Solid hardwood frame, jointed (not stapled).** Frame failure is the #1 reason a sofa is "ruined" before its time.

Specific picks:
- **Article Sven series** ($1,400–$2,100) — leather or performance velvet, performance-fabric upgrade available. Lookbook-friendly photos. Free white-glove delivery for STR-friendly setup.
- **West Elm Harmony** ($1,800–$3,200) — slip-cover option for STR mode. Mid-tier price, premium-tier finishes.
- **Burrow Nomad** ($1,300–$2,500) — modular, can be reconfigured by future you when the next room layout makes sense. Performance fabric standard.
- **IKEA EKTORP** ($800–$1,200) — budget pick, but specifically chosen because the cover is removable and washable. EKTORP is the only IKEA sofa we'd put in an STR; the others have stapled frames that fail at year 4.

## Avoid

- **Velvet (real, not performance).** Photographs beautifully, stains catastrophically.
- **White or cream linen.** Looks great Day 1; stains by Day 30.
- **Genuine leather under $1,500.** Cracks. Splits. Looks worse at year 3 than the day you bought it.
- **Modular pieces with metal-clip joinery.** Joints loosen; sectional starts wobbling within 18 months.

## Sofa-cover insurance for high-traffic listings

If your nightly rate justifies a $2,000+ sofa, also buy a slipcover that fits over it ($150). Layered defense: performance fabric repels most spills; the slipcover catches the catastrophic ones. Wash the slipcover quarterly between guests. The slipcover ages out (replace at year 3); the underlying sofa stays pristine.

## What guests break

- **Cushion zippers.** Repair with a $5 zipper-pull replacement; if catastrophic, replace the cover.
- **Frame on jumping kids.** Resolution-center claim with original receipt. Document with photos before reporting; Airbnb adjusts based on age.
- **Stains.** With performance fabric, almost always spot-cleanable. With non-performance fabric, often permanent — then it's a claim or a slipcover-and-pretend.

## Disposal

Mid-tier and budget sofas are bulky and hard to dispose of. Two paths:
1. **Schedule pickup at delivery** if buying new (most retailers haul old for free).
2. **List free on neighborhood marketplace** the day before delivery; first taker hauls it away.

Don't pay for a junk hauler unless the new sofa delivery doesn't include haul-away.
