## What it costs to replace a toilet in an Airbnb

Honest installed range: $200-700 per toilet. Builder-grade Glacier Bay or American Standard at $130-200; mid-tier Kohler / Toto at $250-450; premium dual-flush comfort-height at $500-700. Plus install: $150-300 if you hire a plumber.

For STR, the right default is a **mid-tier elongated comfort-height dual-flush** ($350-500 installed). Comfort height (17–19" rim) is preferred by 80%+ of adult guests over standard 14–15" rim. Dual-flush saves water and gives operators a story for sustainability-conscious guests.

## Why STR toilets fail differently

Residential toilets last 20-30 years. STR toilets see 4-8x the daily usage cycles, and worse:
- Foreign objects flushed (toys, wipes, towels) — clogs the trap
- Hard tightening at every cleaning — cracks the porcelain
- Long-stay guests using non-toilet-grade chemicals (drain cleaner, bleach tablets) — destroys the wax ring and seals

STR toilets typically need replacement at 8-12 years (or sooner on porcelain crack).

## What to actually buy

**Default pick:** Kohler Cimarron (Model K-3589, ~$329). Comfort-height, elongated, single-flush 1.28 GPF. Glazed trapway resists clogs. Repair parts available at Home Depot/Lowes.

**Budget pick:** Glacier Bay Power Flush (~$199). 1.28 GPF, elongated. Plastic flapper assembly is the weak point — replace at install ($8 Korky upgrade).

**Premium pick:** Toto Drake II (~$549). Elongated, comfort-height, 1.28 GPF, Cefiontect glaze that resists buildup. Best-rated reliability in plumber surveys.

**Avoid:** Wall-hung toilets unless your building requires them. Repairs require opening the wall — a $20 part becomes a $400 job.

## Installation realities

- **Old toilet removal:** Most plumbers haul away; if DIY, your municipality has bulky-item pickup days.
- **New wax ring + supply line + bolts:** $20-40 in parts. Always replace the wax ring at install; never reuse.
- **Closet flange inspection:** If the flange is cracked or below subfloor level, add $40-180 for repair (DIY flange-extender kit) or $200-400 (plumber).
- **Total DIY cost:** $250-550 (toilet + parts + your time, ~2 hours).
- **Total with plumber:** $400-800.

## Maintenance that extends life

- **Quarterly drain inspection** — slow-drain is a clog warning; address before backup.
- **Replace flapper every 2-3 years** ($8) — silently leaks waste 100s of gallons before guests notice.
- **Tighten bolts hand-tight only** — never wrench-tight; train cleaners.
- **Provide guest signage** — "do not flush wipes, paper towels, feminine products." Reduces clog calls 60%.

## Signs it's time

- Porcelain hairline cracks (replace immediately — sudden failure floods the bathroom)
- Constant running you can't fix with a flapper replacement
- Recurring clogs (trapway buildup; fixable only by replacement)
- Wobble at the base (flange failure)
- Tank-to-bowl gasket leak
