## What it costs to replace a clothes dryer in an Airbnb

Honest installed range: $450-1500. Basic 7.4 cu ft electric or gas Whirlpool $500-650; mid-tier LG / Samsung 7.4 cu ft with steam $800-1100; premium 9.0+ cu ft with sensor-dry $1200-1500.

For STR, the right pick is a **7.4 cu ft basic electric or gas dryer without smart features** ($550-750). Guests don't care about app control. Big enough to handle a single linen set; small enough that the price doesn't sting on a portfolio outfit.

## Electric vs gas — the actual question

If your house has a 240V dryer outlet (the big 4-prong one), it was wired for electric. If it has a gas line at the dryer location, it was wired for gas. Don't try to switch — converting between the two is $400-900 and requires a licensed electrician or plumber.

**Gas dryers** run about 50% cheaper per cycle and dry 25% faster, but cost $50-150 more up front. In a high-volume STR (12+ turnovers/month), gas pays back in year 2.

**Electric dryers** are simpler to install (no gas line, no venting an LP cabinet) and have fewer failure modes.

## What to actually buy

**Default pick:** Whirlpool WED5050LW (electric, $649) or WGD5050LW (gas, $749). 7.4 cu ft, 4 cycles, AutoDry sensor. Repair-able anywhere.

**Budget pick:** GE GTD33EASKWW (electric, $549). 7.2 cu ft, basic timed-dry + AutoDry. Reliable for 3-5 years STR.

**Premium pick:** LG DLEX7900BE (electric, $1199). 9.0 cu ft, steam refresh, sensor dry that actually works on big linen loads.

**Avoid:** Compact 24" dryers unless your laundry closet physically requires one — they cost twice as much per cu ft and break twice as fast.

## Installation realities

- **Delivery + haul-away + installation:** $100-200 at big-box.
- **Vent kit:** $20-40 for the flexible aluminum duct + clamps. Use rigid metal duct where possible — flexible plastic accordion duct is a fire hazard and is banned by code in most jurisdictions.
- **Vent run cleaning:** $80-180 for a dryer-vent specialist. Schedule at install. Lint buildup is the #1 cause of dryer fires.

## Maintenance that prevents fires

This is the one appliance where neglect causes fires, not just failures:

- **Clean the lint trap every cycle.** Train cleaners on this.
- **Annual full vent-run clean.** Lint accumulates beyond the lint trap.
- **Inspect the vent hood outside.** Birds nest in dryer vents constantly; the louver flap must move freely.

A clogged dryer vent triples drying time and quadruples fire risk.

## Signs it's time

- Drying time over 90 minutes for a normal load (vent clog or heating element)
- Drum not turning (belt or motor)
- Unusual noises (drum support rollers failing)
- Burning smell (urgent — stop using immediately)
- Error codes recurring after reset
