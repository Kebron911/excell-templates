## What it costs to replace a 6-person dining table in an Airbnb

Honest range: $400-1500. Particle-board IKEA / Wayfair $300-450; mid-tier solid-wood Article / Floyd / West Elm $700-1100; premium solid-wood Crate & Barrel / Room & Board $1200-1500+.

For STR, the right default is a **mid-tier solid-wood top with metal or wood base** ($700-1100). Particle-board fails fast — water rings, heat marks from hot dishes, chipped edges. Solid wood survives 10-15 years if sealed.

## Why dining tables matter for ADR

The dining table is the #1 photo in most STR listings. Guests evaluate "can my group eat together?" within 2 seconds of the dining photo. A solid 6-person table photographed well closes more bookings than the same square footage put into a designer headboard.

## What to actually buy

**Default pick:** Article Madera (~$899). 71" × 38" walnut veneer with solid-wood edges, metal base. Seats 6 comfortably. 10-12 year STR life with care.

**Budget pick:** IKEA Mörbylånga (~$799). Solid oak, real-wood top, weighs 175 lbs (won't move under group loads). 12-15 year STR life. Wide base of finishes.

**Premium pick:** Room & Board Andover (~$1499). American hardwood, walnut or maple, dovetail apron joinery. 20+ year STR life if maintained.

**Avoid:** Any dining table sold under $300 with a "wood-look" finish — it's MDF with melamine. Water rings the first time a glass condenses on it.

## Sizing for STR — what 6-person actually means

A real 6-person dining table is **at minimum 72" × 38"**. Tables sold as "6-person" at 60" × 36" really seat 4 comfortably. Guests don't trust the listing copy after they discover this.

Round 60"+ tables seat 6 well; round 48" tables seat 4 max. Rectangular tables under 72" should be marketed as "4-6 person" or "intimate dining."

## Installation realities

- **Assembly:** Most ship with the top assembled and the base flat-pack. ~30 min to attach the base.
- **Felt pads under feet:** Prevent floor scratches on hardwood. $4 pack at any hardware store.
- **Table-pad protector:** $40-80 quilted pad. Optional; protects veneer tops from hot dishes during group meals.

## Lifespan math under STR conditions

| Type | Residential life | STR life |
|---|---|---|
| Particle / MDF wood-look | 5–8 yr | 3–5 yr |
| Veneer on engineered wood | 10–15 yr | 7–10 yr |
| Solid wood with metal base | 20–30 yr | 12–18 yr |
| Solid wood antique / heirloom | 50+ yr | 15–25 yr |

## Maintenance that extends life

- **Quarterly oil or wax re-application** on solid-wood tops — restores the protective seal that group meals strip.
- **Annual screw-tighten** on the base — group weight at one end racks the base over time.
- **Replace felt pads annually** — wear through, then the base feet scratch floors.
- **Provide trivets in the kitchen drawer** — guests will set hot pans down without one if you don't.

## Signs it's time

- Wobble that screws can't fix (base racking)
- Visible water rings that won't sand out
- Cracked or split veneer that exceeds 1/4" length
- Chipped edge corner from being moved during cleaning
- Loose joinery (table flexes when you push the corner)
