## Bath towels are the highest-frequency replacement item in your STR

If you replace nothing else on the cycle, replace towels. Stained, threadbare, or scratchy towels surface in cleanliness reviews more often than dirty floors do, and they cost $40–$80 to replace per set. Two sets per bedroom, replaced every 2–3 years, plus a quarterly stain-screening cycle.

The mistake hosts make: they buy white hotel-style towels, pretend bleach works forever, and don't notice that "hotel-style" is a different fabric weight than what they bought. Then one guest washes their face with mascara on, the towel goes pink-gray, and it stays in the rotation for another year because nobody on the cleaning team has authority to retire linens.

## Buy specifically for STR use

Not all towels are STR-grade. Three things matter:

1. **Weight: 600 GSM minimum.** Lighter towels feel papery, dry guests poorly, and pill on first wash. The published "luxury" 700+ GSM tier is overkill for STR — the towel is heavy in the dryer, takes longer to wash, and isn't perceived as nicer by guests.
2. **Tight twist** (look for "low-twist" or "long-staple cotton" claims; both signal durability). Cheap loosely-woven towels lint everywhere on first wash.
3. **Color: white or off-white only.** Bleach-friendly. Anything else stains visibly and gets retired in 18 months. White lasts the full 3 years if you let your cleaner bleach the load every 4-6 washes.

Specific picks:
- **Brooklinen Classic Bath Towels** ($25 each, often 6-pack discount) — 600 GSM, long-staple, washes well, accepts bleach.
- **Snowe Bath Towel** ($35 each) — slightly heavier, also long-staple. Costs more but the white stays whiter.
- **Target Threshold Performance Towel** ($14 each) — surprisingly good budget option. Replace on a 2-year cycle instead of 3 and it's a wash.

## STR towel inventory math

Per bathroom, keep:
- **3 bath towels** (so one is always clean while two are in the wash)
- **3 hand towels**
- **3 washcloths**
- **1 set of "spare" towels** in a closet for guests who request more

For a 2-bath property, that's 12 bath towels in rotation. Buy 18 — keep 6 in storage as the "next year's set" so you can rotate stained ones out as you go without scrambling to reorder.

## The stain-screening cycle (quarterly task)

Every 90 days, lay out the entire towel inventory on a flat surface in good light. Anything with:
- Yellow underarm-style stains
- Pink/gray makeup stains that survived bleach
- Threadbare patches you can see light through
- Permanent fold lines (towels stored too long under heavier items)

…gets retired immediately. Cut into rags for the cleaner. Replace from the spare set in the closet, then reorder the next set so storage refills.

## Why this is on a 2-year cycle, not 4

Cotton towels lose absorbency from minerals in laundry water more than from physical wear. By year 3 of heavy STR use, a towel feels rough but technically functional — and rough-feeling towels are a guest complaint waiting to happen. Replace before they feel rough, not after a guest writes the review.
