## When to actually replace a queen mattress in an Airbnb

The published 8-year lifespan number is for a residential mattress with two adult sleepers. An STR queen mattress with 100+ guests cycling through it has a real-world lifespan closer to 5–7 years. The signal that yours is past replacement isn't a sag you can see — it's reviews quietly saying "the bed was uncomfortable" three times in three months.

By the time three guests have written that, three more have thought it and not bothered. Your nightly review average is decaying because of a $700 part you can replace in a Tuesday afternoon.

## What to buy in the STR mid-tier ($700–$1,200)

Skip the foam-only options. They sleep hot, soak up sweat, and develop body impressions on the side a heavier guest sleeps on. STR-grade picks share three features:

- **Hybrid construction** (pocketed coils + foam top). Coils breathe, foam adds comfort without trapping heat.
- **Mid-firm to firm** (6.5–7.5 / 10). Soft mattresses fit fewer body types and accumulate impressions faster. Firm pleases more guests; you can soften with a topper if you want.
- **Replaceable cover.** Look for one with a zip-off top cover that can be machine-washed (or replaced every 2 years for $80).

Specific picks:
- **Saatva Classic** ($1,400 queen, often $1,000 on sale) — best STR-grade hybrid, 365-night trial that doesn't matter to you, free white-glove delivery + old mattress haul-away that does. Lifetime warranty against sag, which is the only thing you actually care about for STR use.
- **DreamCloud** ($800 queen, often $600 on sale) — strong middle option, hybrid, generous 365-night trial.
- **Tuft & Needle Original Queen** ($600) — the floor for "this isn't embarrassing." Foam-only, 100% better than budget store-brand foam.

## Always buy the protector

Always. Every time. A $30 SafeRest mattress protector turns "ruined mattress" into "ruined protector." Do not rent the property without one.

## Resolution-center math

When a guest spills wine on the protector, you submit a claim for a $30 protector replacement. When a guest spills wine and the protector wasn't there, you submit a claim for $1,000+ and Airbnb adjusts down based on lifespan. The protector is the cheapest insurance product on this entire site.

## Disposing the old one

Most U.S. metros have mattress recycling programs (sometimes free, sometimes $25). If yours doesn't, the white-glove delivery from Saatva or similar will haul it away for free — schedule the delivery the same day as your weekly cleaner so you're not making two visits to the property.
