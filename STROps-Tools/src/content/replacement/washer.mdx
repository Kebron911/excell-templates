## What it costs to replace a clothes washer in an Airbnb

Honest installed range: $500-1600. Builder-grade top-load Whirlpool $500-700; mid-tier LG / Samsung front-load $800-1200; high-efficiency top-load with steam $1100-1600.

For STR, the right pick is almost always **top-load high-efficiency without an agitator** ($700-1000). Front-load looks premium but mold issues with infrequent use destroy it in 3-5 years of STR cycles. Top-load HE handles guest laundry, towel-and-linen loads from cleaners, and survives extended weeks-between-uses without growing mildew.

## Why front-loads fail in STR

Front-load washers seal a wet drum. Residential use runs daily — the drum dries between loads. STR use is feast/famine: heavy use during cleaner turnover days, then nothing for 3-7 days. The seal stays damp. Mold blooms. Within 18-36 months, the door gasket smells permanently musty and reviews start mentioning "weird laundry-room smell."

Top-load HE has an open-top design that air-dries between uses. No mold-bloom risk.

## What to actually buy

**Default pick:** GE GTW720BSNWS (~$849, top-load HE no agitator). 4.8 cu ft, large enough for sheet sets + comforters, deep-fill option for guests who don't trust HE cycles.

**Budget pick:** Whirlpool WTW5057LW (~$649, top-load HE no agitator). 4.7 cu ft, fewer cycle options but reliable, repair-able by any tech.

**Premium pick:** LG WT7900HBA (~$1199, top-load HE with steam). 5.5 cu ft, TurboWash, steam cycle that helps with sweat-soaked turnover linens.

**Avoid:** Front-loaders for any STR use. Avoid Samsung washers in any tier — the top-load Samsung lawsuit-recall history is well-documented.

## Installation realities

- **Delivery + haul-away + installation:** $100-200 at big-box.
- **Stainless braided hoses:** $30-50. Replace rubber hoses at install — a burst hose at 2am during a guest stay can do $5,000+ in damage. Worth the $40.
- **Drain pan + leak alarm:** $50-80. Required by code in many states for second-floor laundry; smart for any floor in an STR (insurance discount on some policies).

## Lifespan math under STR conditions

| Type | Residential life | STR life |
|---|---|---|
| Top-load with agitator | 11–13 yr | 6–8 yr |
| Top-load HE no agitator | 11–14 yr | 8–10 yr |
| Front-load | 11–14 yr | 5–7 yr (mold) |
| High-end with steam | 10–13 yr | 7–9 yr |

## Maintenance that extends life

- Monthly self-clean cycle with washing-machine cleaner (Affresh).
- Leave the lid open between uses (HE only — no lid for kids/pets to slam).
- Drain-pump filter clean every 6 months on front-loads.
- Replace fill hoses every 5 years (industry standard).

## Signs it's time

- Persistent smell that returns after cleaning
- Visible mold on the gasket (front-load) or drum (any)
- Drum bearings whining or clunking
- Leaking from underneath during fill or spin
- Cycles aborting with error codes
