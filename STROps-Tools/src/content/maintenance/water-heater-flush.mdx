## Why water heater flush matters more in an STR than in a home

A residential water heater serving two adults sees roughly 80 gallons of throughput a day. An STR water heater hosting a family of six sees 250+ gallons across the same window — and STR sediment buildup tracks gallons, not years. The published 12-month cadence is fine for a primary residence; for an active STR it's the absolute minimum, and a 6-month flush is closer to right if your water source is hard.

Sediment buildup does three things, all bad for guest experience:

1. **Reduces effective tank capacity.** A 50-gallon tank with 4 inches of sediment in the bottom holds only 38 gallons of usable hot water. The fourth shower goes cold. The fourth shower is the difference between a 5-star and a 3-star review.
2. **Forces the burner to work harder.** Sediment is a thermal blanket between the burner and the water. Energy bills creep up about 15% over a year of heavy use, and the dip-tube and burner shroud age fast.
3. **Drops the recovery rate.** A clean tank refills 30 gallons of hot water in about 30 minutes; a sediment-loaded tank takes 45–60. Back-to-back guests in a quick turnover hit cold water far sooner than expected.

## How to flush (DIY, 30 minutes)

1. **Turn it off.** For gas: set thermostat to "pilot." For electric: trip the breaker. Do not skip this — flushing a hot heater with the burner active will trigger high-temp limit and may damage the dip tube.
2. **Connect a garden hose** to the drain valve at the bottom. Run it to a floor drain or outside.
3. **Turn off cold-water supply** at the inlet valve.
4. **Open a hot-water tap** on a sink upstairs (relieves vacuum so the tank drains).
5. **Open the drain valve.** Let it run until the water comes out clear (5–10 minutes for a normal year; 20 minutes if you're catching up).
6. **Close drain, restore cold supply, let tank refill,** then turn the heater back on.

## When to call a pro instead

- The drain valve is plastic and won't close fully after opening (very common on tanks 8+ years old). A pro replaces with a ¾" brass ball valve at the same time.
- The tank is showing rust at any seam. Flushing a rusty tank can punch a hole through a thinned wall.
- You smell gas, ever, around a gas water heater. Stop, ventilate, call a plumber.

## Tankless caveat

If the property has a tankless heater, this task changes entirely — it becomes "descale the heat exchanger with vinegar," same cadence, cost $50–$100 in supplies. Tankless units fail catastrophically when scale builds; do not skip it just because there's no tank to look at.

## Anniversary trick

Pair this with the smoke detector battery swap (also annual). Fall is best — your shoulder season has the slowest bookings, and a flush takes 30 minutes between guests if you must.
