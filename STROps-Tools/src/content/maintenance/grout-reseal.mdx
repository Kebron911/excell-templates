## How often to reseal grout in an Airbnb

**Annually for STR-grade usage**, regardless of what the original installer told the homeowner. Residential grout sealers are rated for 1-3 years; STR usage compresses that to 12 months because of cleaning chemical exposure and traffic frequency.

Cost is **$40–$200** in materials and time. A quart of penetrating sealer (Aqua Mix, Miracle Sealants 511, or StoneTech BulletProof) runs $25-$40 and covers a typical bath. Add a foam-tip applicator bottle, painter's tape, and shop towels — total under $60 per property if you DIY.

## Why grout resealing matters more for STR

Unsealed grout is porous concrete. It absorbs water, soap scum, body oils, cleaning chemicals, and bacteria. Within months it turns grey, then brown, then it hosts mildew you can't bleach out.

Guests don't say "the grout is unsealed." They say:
- "Bathroom looked dingy"
- "Floor felt grimy even though it was mopped"
- "Tile looked dated"

That last one is the killer. Sealed, freshly-cleaned grout reads as new even on 15-year-old tile. Unsealed grout reads as old even on a 2-year remodel.

Pair this task with the annual [caulk and grout inspection](/maintenance/caulk-grout-inspect/) — inspect first, fix any failures, then reseal everything healthy.

## What a real grout reseal includes

A proper reseal pass covers seven steps:

1. **Deep-clean the grout first.** Sealer locks in dirt; resealing dirty grout permanently traps the grime. Oxygen bleach + stiff brush, or steam, or a grout-renewer product. Let it dry 24+ hours.
2. **Tape adjacent surfaces** if you're using a film-forming sealer (mostly skip — penetrating sealers don't need tape, just wipe-off discipline).
3. **Apply with a foam-tip bottle** along every grout line. Saturate the joint but don't flood the tile.
4. **Let it dwell 5-10 minutes** per the product spec.
5. **Wipe excess off the tile** with a clean microfiber. Aggressively — sealer haze cures into a permanent film if you don't.
6. **Second coat after 30-60 minutes** for high-water areas (shower walls, shower pan).
7. **Cure 24 hours before water exposure.** Block the bath; reroute guests.

A single bath takes 90 minutes plus dry time. A whole property (2 bath + kitchen) takes a half-day plus 24-hour cure window.

## DIY vs pro

DIY is correct. Penetrating sealers are forgiving — over-application is the only common mistake, and it's recoverable with a second wipe pass. Skip film-forming sealers (the high-gloss kind) — they look great for 6 months then peel and trap dirt under the film.

Pros earn the call when grout is too far gone — they'll regrout or color-seal (paint the grout) instead of just resealing. Color-sealing runs $1.50-$4/linear foot.

## When to upgrade the cadence

- **High-occupancy showers** — bump to every 180 days
- **Hard-water markets** (mineral deposits abrade the sealer) — bump to every 180 days
- **Light-colored grout** (white, beige, off-white) — bump to every 180 days; it shows everything

## The "water bead" test

Drop water on the grout line. If it beads — sealer is intact. If it darkens within 30 seconds — sealer is gone, reseal now.

Run this test on every property at the 6-month mark. It tells you whether you can push to 12 months or need to reseal early.

## Signs you missed it

- Grout color noticeably darker than when installed
- Soap scum sticks to grout that used to wipe clean
- Mildew comes back within days of cleaning
- Water absorbs into grout lines instead of beading
- Guest reviews mention "grimy" or "dated" bathrooms despite recent cleaning
