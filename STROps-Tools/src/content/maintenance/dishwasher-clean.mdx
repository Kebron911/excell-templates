## How often to clean the dishwasher in an Airbnb

**Every 90 days — quarterly.** Filter rinse should happen monthly as a cleaner-SOP task; the full deep clean (filter + spray arms + Affresh cycle) goes on the quarterly calendar.

Cost is **$0–$15** per cycle. Affresh dishwasher tablets are $5 for a 6-pack. White vinegar is a couple of dollars. Most of the work is mechanical (pulling the filter, rinsing the arms), not chemical.

## Why dishwasher cleaning matters more for STR

Guests do not pre-rinse. They scrape (sometimes), they load, they walk away. Food particles, grease, and detergent residue accumulate in the filter and the spray arms 3-5x faster than in a residential machine.

The symptom is what gets photographed: **cloudy glasses and spotted plates in the morning-coffee shot**. Your beautiful staging photo of mugs by the espresso machine? Doesn't work when the mugs have hard-water film on them.

Worse: the smell. A neglected dishwasher develops a sour-food odor that hits the kitchen every time the door opens. Guests open it to load and immediately think "dirty kitchen." That perception colors the rest of their stay.

Pair this task with the [washing machine self-clean](/maintenance/washing-machine-clean/) — both happen quarterly, both fight biofilm, both save you from "musty / spotty" reviews.

## What a real dishwasher clean includes

A proper quarterly clean covers seven steps:

1. **Pull the filter** at the bottom of the tub. Twist counter-clockwise. Most modern dishwashers (KitchenAid, Bosch, Whirlpool from the last 10 years) have removable filters.
2. **Rinse the filter under hot tap water.** Use a soft brush for stubborn debris. If it smells funky, soak in a bowl of hot water + dish soap for 10 minutes.
3. **Check and clean the spray arms.** Pull the lower arm (usually pops off). Use a toothpick to clear clogged jet holes — limescale and food bits block them and you get poor spray pattern = spotty dishes.
4. **Wipe the door gasket and the area under the door lip.** Food and standing water collect here. Vinegar + microfiber.
5. **Pull out the racks and wipe the interior walls.** Especially around the heating element at the bottom.
6. **Run an empty hot cycle with Affresh or vinegar.** One Affresh tablet on the bottom of the empty machine, longest cycle, hottest temp. (Or 2 cups of vinegar in a measuring cup on the top rack — empty machine, hot cycle.)
7. **Reinstall the filter (twist clockwise until it locks).** Forgetting to lock the filter means food bypasses it and gets pumped onto the next load of dishes.

Total time: 20-30 minutes, plus a 60-90 minute cycle that runs unattended.

## DIY vs pro

100% DIY. No pro involvement until you're replacing a pump or the heating element, both of which are end-of-life decisions, not maintenance.

## When to upgrade the cadence

- **Hard-water markets** — bump filter rinse to every 30 days, deep clean to every 60 days
- **High-occupancy (>80%)** — bump to every 60 days
- **Properties without a garbage disposal** (guests rinse less, more food enters the dishwasher) — bump to every 60 days
- **Listings with "kitchen" or "chef's kitchen" as a selling point** — quarterly minimum + monthly filter rinse, no exceptions

## Bake it into the cleaner SOP

Highest-leverage moves on every turnover:
- "Pull dishwasher filter, rinse under tap, reinstall"
- "Wipe gasket and door lip with vinegar spray"
- "Run an empty hot cycle if any food residue visible"

Adds 5 minutes per turnover. Eliminates the dominant cause of the "smell" complaint.

## Signs you missed it

- Glasses come out spotty or cloudy
- Plates have food bits stuck after a cycle
- Sour or rotten-food smell when you open the door
- Standing water in the bottom of the tub after a cycle
- Spray-arm holes visibly clogged with white scale or food

Any of these = full deep clean today, then bake the monthly filter rinse into your cleaner SOP.
