## How often to run the washing machine self-clean cycle in an Airbnb

**Every 90 days — quarterly minimum.** Heavy-use STR machines doing 15-25 loads per week should run it every 60 days. The cycle takes 2-4 hours but is hands-off, so cleaners can start it when they arrive and pick it up before they leave.

Cost is **$0–$15** per cycle. A 6-pack of Affresh tablets runs $10-$14. Vinegar costs almost nothing. Some machines have a built-in "Tub Clean" or "Self Clean" mode that runs without any additive at all.

## Why washing machine cleaning matters more for STR

Residential washers handle 4-6 loads per week. STR washers handle 4-6 loads per **day** during peak season — every single guest gets fresh sheets, every set of towels gets stripped. That's 10x the duty cycle, which means 10x the detergent residue, 10x the lint buildup behind the drum, and 10x the biofilm in the gasket.

The downstream problem is what guests notice: **linens that come out of a "clean" wash smelling musty**. Not visibly dirty — just slightly off. They unfold a towel in the bathroom and there it is. That's a 5-star review walking out the door, and you can't fix it with a louder fragrance — fragrance on biofilm just smells like floral mildew.

Front-loaders are worse than top-loaders. The horizontal axis lets water sit in the gasket fold. Skipping self-clean cycles on a front-loader for a year produces visible black mildew along the rubber gasket within 18 months.

## What a real washing machine clean includes

A full quarterly clean covers six things:

1. **Empty the drum.** No clothes, no towels.
2. **Wipe the gasket** (front-loaders especially). Pull back the rubber fold, wipe out lint, hair, and any black residue with a 50/50 vinegar-water mix.
3. **Clean the detergent dispenser.** Pull it out if removable; soak in hot water; scrub with an old toothbrush. Slime in here is biofilm.
4. **Run the self-clean cycle.** Add an Affresh tablet (or 1 cup of bleach OR 2 cups of white vinegar — not both) and run the longest, hottest cycle the machine has. Use Tub Clean / Self Clean mode if available.
5. **Wipe the drum interior** after the cycle finishes — any residue means you needed two cycles.
6. **Leave the door open** between cycles for the next 24-48 hours so the gasket dries. Add this to your cleaner SOP.

For top-loaders without a self-clean mode: fill the drum with the hottest water it allows, add 4 cups of white vinegar, agitate for a minute, then let it sit for an hour before running a full cycle. Repeat with hot water + 1 cup baking soda for the rinse.

## DIY vs pro

100% DIY. This is a cleaner-SOP task, not a repair task. The only time you call a pro is when the gasket is so far gone the mildew comes back within days of cleaning — at that point, replace the gasket ($150-$300 part + 1 hour labor).

## When to upgrade the cadence

- **Front-loaders** — bump to every 60 days
- **High-occupancy (>80%)** — bump to every 60 days
- **Properties using fragrance-free or eco detergents** — bump to every 60 days (less surfactant = more residue)
- **Hard-water markets** — bump to every 60 days

## Bake it into the cleaner SOP

The single highest-leverage move: add three lines to your cleaner's checklist:
- "Wipe gasket with vinegar spray every turnover"
- "Leave washer door open after final load"
- "Run Affresh cycle every 12th turnover"

Cost: $0.50 per turnover in supplies. Saves you the mid-season musty-towel disaster.

## Signs you missed it

- Linens smell musty out of the wash
- Visible black mildew on the gasket fold (front-loader)
- Standing water in the detergent dispenser
- Cycle leaves a film on the drum interior
- Guest review mentions "damp" or "musty" sheets or towels

Any of these = run two back-to-back self-clean cycles immediately, then add the SOP lines above.
