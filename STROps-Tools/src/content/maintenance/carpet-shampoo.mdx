## How often to shampoo carpets in an Airbnb

**Annually for high-traffic carpeted rooms** (master bedroom, living room, stairs). Every 18-24 months for low-traffic carpeted rooms (guest bedrooms, formal dining).

Cost: $100-250 per room by a professional carpet cleaner; $40-80 to rent a Rug Doctor + DIY ($20 in shampoo). Quarterly between deep cleans, do **rapid spot-treatment** (a $15 spray bottle of OxiClean Carpet Solution + a microfiber cloth).

## Why STR carpets need it more than residential

Residential carpets get vacuumed daily by one household. STR carpets get vacuumed at every turnover by cleaners — but daily-foot-traffic equivalent is 3-5x because of group sizes and shorter intervals between bookings. Add the unique STR variables:

- **Pet allowance** (where applicable): dander + pet stains
- **Spilled drinks** during group gatherings (wine, coffee, juice)
- **Shoes-on policy** uncertainty (most STRs default to shoes-on; high-end go shoes-off)
- **Vacuum-only between deep cleans:** dust and skin oils accumulate

The result: STR carpets that should last 8-10 years often need replacement at 4-6 years if shampooing is skipped.

## What a real carpet shampoo includes

A pro service (Stanley Steemer, Chem-Dry, local equivalent) covers:

1. **Pre-treatment of stains.** Spotters applied to high-stain areas before deep extraction.
2. **Hot-water extraction OR encapsulation cleaning** depending on carpet type.
3. **Deodorizer application** (often deodorizer is the operator-add-on; specify cleaning solution unscented if pet-sensitive guests are coming).
4. **Optional Scotchgard / stain-protector re-application** — $80-150 add-on per room. Worth it on light-colored carpets.

Time: 2-4 hours for a full house; drying takes 4-12 hours.

## DIY vs pro

**DIY with Rug Doctor:** Acceptable for the 12-month cycle. $40-80 rental + $20 shampoo per visit. Plan a half-day per property; carpets dry 8-12 hours.

**Pro service:** Recommended for annual + at any visible-stain emergency. $100-250 per room; better extraction, deeper cleaning, often comes with a satisfaction guarantee.

**Hybrid model (most operators):** DIY at 6 months between guest stays; pro service annually in a quiet booking week.

## Rapid spot-treatment workflow (between deep cleans)

Teach cleaners the 60-second spot-treatment for any new stain spotted at turnover:

1. **Blot, don't rub.** Always blot with a white microfiber cloth — colored cloths can transfer dye.
2. **Apply a small amount of OxiClean Carpet Solution** (or equivalent). Wait 60 seconds.
3. **Blot up again** with a clean section of cloth.
4. **For deep stains, repeat** 2-3 times.
5. **Final pass:** dampened white cloth + clean water; blot up.

This handles 80% of fresh stains. Stains that don't respond go on a list for the next deep clean.

## Replacement breakpoint

Carpet replacement is $2-5/sqft installed for builder-grade; $5-8 for mid-tier; $8-12 for premium. A 3-bedroom with carpeted bedrooms (450 sqft total) costs $900-3,600 to re-carpet.

Replace carpet when:
- Wear pattern (matting, traffic lanes) is visible in photos
- Stains survive professional cleaning
- Pet odor remains after enzymatic treatment
- Carpet is over 8 years old AND showing wear
- Pet damage is widespread (replacement is cheaper than perpetual spot treatment)

## When to upgrade to hard surfaces

In high-rotation STR markets, many operators are moving to LVP (luxury vinyl plank) over carpet. The math:

- LVP install: $4-8/sqft (similar to mid-tier carpet)
- LVP life: 15-20 years (vs 6-8 STR carpet)
- LVP cleaning: mop, no shampoo cycle
- LVP photographs better (resists "old apartment" perception)

The replacement break-even is around year 6 of LVP ownership = same as 2 carpet replacements with shampoo cycles.

## Signs your cycle is too long

- Visible darker traffic lanes in photos
- Carpet feels "crunchy" or rough underfoot
- Smell lingers despite shampoo (deep extraction needed)
- Stains accumulating faster than cleaner can spot-treat
- Allergic guests complaining about indoor air quality
