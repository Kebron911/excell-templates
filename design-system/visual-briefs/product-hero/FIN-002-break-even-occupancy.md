## Composition

**Background:** Parchment `#F6EFE2` full bleed, faint grain. Chosen because the page H1 ("How empty can it get…") is anxious — the parchment is the calm answer.
**Hero element:** MacBook mockup, head-on neutral angle, screen showing the Start tab.
- **Visible workbook content:** Start tab — eyebrow `BREAK-EVEN OCCUPANCY` JetBrains Mono 12pt navy tracked 0.22em; below it the giant readout `24%` Cormorant 500 Medium 88pt Muted Gold `#C9A24B` with the percent sign 60pt (so the digits dominate); 36px Muted Gold rule beneath; below the rule the line `Below this: you're underwater.` Cormorant 400 Italic 18pt graphite. Behind the giant number, a faint blurred 6-row sensitivity grid (Parchment Alt banding, ~30% opacity) — visible enough to suggest depth, not legible.
- **MacBook position:** centered horizontally, screen centered at x: 800; vertical anchor 40% from top (y: 140–700, mockup ~1100×560px). Lid open ~110°.
**Layered elements:**
- Pull-quote top-left, x: 80 y: 130 — see Copy.
- 48px × 1px Muted Gold rule under pull-quote at y: 200.
- Sub-line at y: 240 — see Copy.
**Lighting:** soft top-left, even, 12px soft drop at 8%.
**Color blocking:** Parchment `#F6EFE2` ground (~78%); Muted Gold `#C9A24B` for the `24%` and rule (~10% — the headline color this time, since the number IS the answer); Harbor Navy `#12304E` for headline/workbook headers (~9%); Graphite `#2B2B2B` body (~3%).

## Designer note

This is the only hero in the suite where Muted Gold owns the giant number instead of Harbor Navy — because the entire workbook is about returning ONE number, and gold is the brand's "answer" color (per brand-decisions §5.4). Do NOT recolor the `24%` to navy "for legibility" — the gold reads against parchment fine at body weight 500. Keep the percent sign smaller than the digits.
