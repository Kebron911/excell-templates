## Should you buy an STR in Tybee Island in 2026?

Selectively. Tybee Island is Savannah's beach — drive-to leisure from across the Southeast, walkable village, and strong year-round demand thanks to Georgia's mild winter. $215 ADR at 65% occupancy ($140 RevPAR) is among the strongest economics on the South Atlantic. The 66/100 market score reflects regulatory drag: Tybee operates one of the most active STR density-cap programs in the Southeast, and new licenses in some zones are not currently available.

## Regulation: where the city stands

- **City of Tybee Island** — operates an STR license program with zone-specific density caps. New licenses in capped zones are issued only when an existing license is retired.
- **Chatham County** — local option sales tax + hotel/motel tax apply.
- **License transferability** — existing STR licenses generally transfer with property; verify in writing.
- **Beachfront / R-T (Residential-Tourist) zones** — generally permissive within cap.

The density cap is the deal-defining variable. Confirm license eligibility and transferability on the specific parcel before EMD goes hard. See the City of Tybee Island STR licensing page.

- **South End / 16th Street** — village core; walkable; premium pricing.

## The 3 mistakes buyers make here

1. **Assuming you can apply for a new STR license.** In capped zones, you cannot. License transferability is what you're really buying.
2. **Underestimating storm-surge insurance.** Tybee is a barrier island; flood and wind coverage materially affect the pro forma.
3. **Modeling Savannah ADR.** Tybee runs higher than Savannah city per night but with more seasonality. Comp within Tybee, not within Savannah.
