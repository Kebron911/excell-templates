## Should you buy an STR in Cannon Beach in 2026?

Only if you can acquire an existing transferable STR license. Cannon Beach has the strongest economics in the PNW coastal STR market: $287 ADR, 56% occupancy, $161 RevPAR, 84/100 market score, tier-B saturation. Demand is dominated by Portland metro drive-to plus shoulder-and-summer tourism. The deal-defining catch: the city operates one of the strictest STR-density-cap programs in the country, and new licenses are not currently being issued outside the limited commercial-zone allowance. The buy = the license.

## Regulation: where the city stands

- **City of Cannon Beach** — operates an STR license program with a hard cap. New licenses outside specific commercial zones are not available; existing licenses transfer with property.
- **Clatsop County (unincorporated)** — separate jurisdiction with its own STR rules.
- **Oregon state lodging tax + transient lodging tax** — both apply.
- **License transferability** — verify in writing on the specific property before EMD goes hard.

See the City of Cannon Beach short-term rental program page for current rules.

## The 3 mistakes buyers make here

1. **Buying expecting to apply for a new STR license.** In most zones, you cannot. The transferable license is what you're buying.
2. **Underestimating PNW seasonality.** Cannon Beach has a longer shoulder season than most beach markets, but November-February is meaningfully softer.
3. **Skipping the license-transferability paperwork.** Get the license-transfer process confirmed by the city, in writing, before EMD goes hard.
