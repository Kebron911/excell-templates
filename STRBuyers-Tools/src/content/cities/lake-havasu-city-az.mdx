## Should you buy an STR in Lake Havasu City in 2026?

Yes, with the right submarket. Lake Havasu City is a water-recreation destination — boating, jet-ski, spring-break for the Southwest college market, plus a strong snowbird base from the Midwest and Canada. $194 ADR at 56% occupancy is solid, and the 74/100 market score is honest. The Arizona structural advantage: state law (SB 1350) preempts municipal STR bans, so registration-only is the rule. The deal is decided by lake-access value and HOA rules in some master-planned communities.

## Regulation: where the city stands

- **Arizona state preemption (SB 1350, 2016 + subsequent amendments)** — cities cannot ban STRs but can require registration, post a contact, and enforce nuisance rules.
- **City of Lake Havasu City** — STR registration required; transaction privilege tax (TPT) remittance required.
- **Mohave County (unincorporated)** — permissive.
- **HOA / master-planned communities** — some communities (Refuge, Havasu Foothills Estates) carry rental restrictions.

See the City of Lake Havasu City transaction privilege tax and STR registration pages for current rules.

## Submarkets that matter

- **Lakefront / channel access** — premium ADR; boat-slip premium.
- **The Island (London Bridge area)** — tourist core; walkable.
- **Foothills / North Havasu** — quieter SFR; lower ADR.
- **South Havasu (Sara Park, Lake Havasu Estates)** — family suburban.

## The 3 mistakes buyers make here

1. **Buying without boat-slip or boat-access confirmation.** Lake access is the value driver. A property without a slip or close-walk access prices very differently from one with.
2. **Skipping the HOA rules in master-planned communities.** Some Lake Havasu HOAs restrict rentals to 30+ days.
3. **Modeling year-round 60%+ occupancy.** The market is heavily spring + summer weighted; winter is snowbird, not vacation.
