## Should you buy an STR in Biloxi in 2026?

Yes, with realistic insurance. Biloxi has the most underrated demand engine on the Gulf Coast — casino-driven mid-week occupancy that smooths out the leisure peaks. $175 ADR at 60% occupancy is solid, and the 73/100 market score reflects a genuinely accessible market where new entrants can still find footing. The catches: Hurricane Katrina rewrote the flood map, the windstorm insurance market is dominated by the Mississippi Wind Pool, and tourism upside is gated by casino industry health.

## Regulation: where the city stands

- **City of Biloxi** — STR allowed; registration and lodging tax remittance required. No density caps.
- **Harrison County (unincorporated)** — permissive.
- **Mississippi Wind Pool (state-backed)** — provides wind-only coverage in coastal counties when private market won't bind.
- **Flood zones** — post-Katrina FEMA updates put much of the south Biloxi waterfront in high-risk zones requiring flood insurance.

See the City of Biloxi STR ordinance and Harrison County tax collector page for current rules.

## Submarkets that matter

- **Beach Boulevard / Casino Row** — premium ADR; flood-zone heavy.
- **Pointe Cadet / East Biloxi** — historic, lower price point.
- **Downtown Biloxi / D'Iberville edge** — quieter SFR.
- **West Biloxi / Edgewater** — family leisure, lower insurance.

## The 3 mistakes buyers make here

1. **Underwriting like a normal coastal market.** Biloxi is a casino-leisure hybrid. Mid-week occupancy is driven by gambling weekends; build the pro forma around that, not pure summer.
2. **Skipping the FEMA flood map review.** South Biloxi flood zones changed post-Katrina. Pull the elevation certificate.
3. **Ignoring wind pool dependency.** Many Biloxi properties cannot bind windstorm coverage in the private market and must use the MS Wind Pool. Get the quote real.
