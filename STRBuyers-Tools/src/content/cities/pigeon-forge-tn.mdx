## Should you buy an STR in Pigeon Forge in 2026?

Yes, with disciplined product selection. Pigeon Forge sits in the heart of Sevier County's Smoky Mountain cabin tourism economy — anchored by Dollywood, gateway access to GSMNP, year-round drive-to demand from the Southeast and Midwest, and famously permissive STR rules. $168 ADR at 67% occupancy is solid, and the 82/100 market score is honest. The catch: Sevier County (Gatlinburg, Pigeon Forge, Sevierville) has tens of thousands of professionally-marketed cabins. Saturation in the mid-tier is real.

## Regulation: where the city stands

- **City of Pigeon Forge** — STR allowed; standard business license + lodging tax remittance.
- **Sevier County (unincorporated, Wears Valley, Cobbly Nob)** — generally permissive.
- **Tennessee state sales tax + occupancy tax** — both apply; remittance audited.
- **No state income tax** — Tennessee structural margin advantage for owners.

See the City of Pigeon Forge business license and Sevier County hotel-motel tax pages for current rules.

## Submarkets that matter

- **Parkway corridor** — attractions-adjacent; family demand.
- **Wears Valley (county)** — premium cabin; quieter; mountain views.
- **Cobbly Nob / Black Bear Ridge** — premium resort cabins; HOA-managed.
- **Sevierville edge** — lower price entry; commuter cabin tier.

## The 3 mistakes buyers make here

1. **Buying a generic 2BR cabin in a crowded development.** Mid-tier inventory is the most saturated. Differentiate (view, hot tub, theater room, pet-friendly, multi-master) or buy at the entry tier.
2. **Modeling 75%+ occupancy.** The market averages 67%. A new entrant with no reviews will be below that in year one.
3. **Underestimating wildfire and steep-slope insurance.** Post-2016 Gatlinburg fires, insurers have repriced. Mountain-slope properties carry the highest premium.
