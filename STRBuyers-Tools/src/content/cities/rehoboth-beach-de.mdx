## Should you buy an STR in Rehoboth Beach in 2026?

Yes, with realistic seasonality. Rehoboth is one of the strongest Mid-Atlantic leisure markets: $256 ADR, 57% occupancy, $146 RevPAR, 81/100 market score. The Delaware structural advantage matters — no state sales tax, which materially lowers guest friction and improves margin. Demand is drive-to from DC, Philly, and the Mid-Atlantic. The catch: heavily summer-weighted (15-17 peak weeks plus shoulder), and the small geographic footprint means saturation is real even with tier-B classification.

## Regulation: where the city stands

- **City of Rehoboth Beach** — STR allowed; rental license required; gross-rental-receipts tax remittance required.
- **Sussex County** — permissive; some unincorporated areas (Dewey edge, North Shores) under county rule.
- **No state sales tax** — Delaware structural advantage; lodging tax handled at city/county level.
- **HOA / condo** — common in newer beachfront and bayside developments.

See the City of Rehoboth Beach rental license page for current rules.

## Submarkets that matter

- **Boardwalk / downtown core** — walkable; premium ADR; oldest inventory.
- **North Shores / Henlopen Acres** — premium SFR; HOA presence.
- **South Rehoboth** — quieter; family-oriented.
- **Dewey Beach (separate, adjacent)** — younger demographic; party-noise enforcement; separate rules.

## The 3 mistakes buyers make here

1. **Modeling 65%+ occupancy.** Rehoboth runs ~57%. Build the pro forma on real numbers.
2. **Confusing Rehoboth and Dewey rules.** Dewey enforces noise ordinances aggressively and has its own license regime.
3. **Underestimating off-season carry.** November-March generates a fraction of peak revenue. Reserves matter.
