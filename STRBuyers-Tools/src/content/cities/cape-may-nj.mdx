## Should you buy an STR in Cape May in 2026?

Selectively. Cape May is one of the East Coast's most distinctive leisure markets — Victorian historic core, walkable village, strong drive-to demand from Philly, NYC, DC, and the Mid-Atlantic. $244 ADR at 56% occupancy is solid, and the 79/100 market score reflects genuine demand strength. The hard truth: occupancy is heavily summer-weighted. The pro forma has to work on roughly 14-16 peak weeks plus shoulder.

## Regulation: where the city stands

- **City of Cape May** — STRs allowed; registration and mercantile license required. Historic-district overlay (HPC review) on exterior modifications.
- **Cape May County** — county-level tourism tax applies.
- **Wildwood / Avalon / Stone Harbor (separate municipalities)** — own rules; some are more restrictive.
- **Insurance** — Atlantic Coast hurricane and nor'easter exposure; flood-zone heavy.

See the City of Cape May mercantile license and Historic Preservation Commission pages for current rules.

## Submarkets that matter

- **Historic District (Beach Avenue, Ocean Street)** — Victorian B&B and SFR; HPC overlay.
- **West Cape May (separate borough)** — quieter; lower ADR.
- **The Cove / Beach Drive west** — beachfront premium.
- **North Beach / Higbee Beach edge** — quieter, fewer rentals.

## The 3 mistakes buyers make here

1. **Modeling 60%+ occupancy.** Cape May is summer-peak. Build the pro forma on 14-16 weeks of strong peak plus shoulder, not year-round.
2. **Underestimating HPC renovation friction.** Historic-district exterior changes require Historic Preservation Commission sign-off.
3. **Ignoring flood and nor'easter exposure.** Cape May sits at the southern tip of NJ — repeated storm impact in the past decade has driven insurance up.
