## Should you buy an STR in Wisconsin Dells in 2026?

Yes, if you can buy a large-occupancy property or a condo in a resort-pool program. Wisconsin Dells is a heavily concentrated tourism market built around indoor and outdoor water parks (Kalahari, Wilderness Resort, Mt. Olympus, Noah's Ark). ADR is $215, occupancy 59%, RevPAR $127, market score 77/100. Demand is family-group-driven: properties that sleep 8-12+ outperform standard 2-bedroom units materially.

## Regulation: where the city stands

City of Wisconsin Dells and Lake Delton STR rules:

- **Tourist Rooming House license** required statewide.
- **Wisconsin state law** preempts most municipal STR bans but allows licensing.
- **Room Tax** stack (city/village + state) around 8-9%.
- **Resort/condo-hotel programs** at Kalahari, Wilderness, and similar handle most rental-pool compliance.

The Dells straddles two municipalities — the City of Wisconsin Dells and the Village of Lake Delton — plus surrounding Adams, Columbia, Juneau, and Sauk Counties. Verify which jurisdiction the parcel falls in. Most water-park-adjacent product is concentrated in Lake Delton.

Demand drivers: summer outdoor parks (June-August), indoor water parks year-round (December holidays, March spring break, weekend escapes from Chicago / Milwaukee / Madison / Twin Cities). The indoor water-park product is what makes Dells different from most Midwest summer markets — winter occupancy in family-group SFR can run 50%+.

## Submarkets that matter

- **Lake Delton** — Kalahari and Mt. Olympus adjacency, condo-resort dominant.
- **Wisconsin Dells City** — downtown, river boats, walkable to attractions.
- **Lake Delton residential (off-resort)** — large-occupancy SFR, family-group target.
- **River Road / Wisconsin River corridor** — riverfront cottage, summer-focused.

## The 3 mistakes buyers make here

1. **Buying a small condo expecting Kalahari ADR.** The premium goes to large-sleeper units; small condos compete on price.
2. **Confusing Wisconsin Dells with Lake Delton.** Two separate municipalities; permit and tax processes differ.
3. **Underwriting at hotel ADR.** Hotels and condo-resorts capture much of the family demand; STR competes on cabin/SFR product where 8-12 sleepers separate the winners from the losers.
