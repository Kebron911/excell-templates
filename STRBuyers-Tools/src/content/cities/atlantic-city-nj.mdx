## Should you buy an STR in Atlantic City in 2026?

Selectively. AC is the unusual urban-casino STR market: $156 ADR, 60% occupancy, $94 RevPAR. The 57/100 market score is honest — demand exists, but it's heavily mid-week-mid-tier casino-driven, with regulatory friction at the city level. The buy case is narrow: beachfront condos with strong building amenities, or properties in the Ducktown / Chelsea Heights submarkets where SFR demand holds.

## Regulation: where the city stands

- **City of Atlantic City** — STRs allowed but registration and mercantile license required; lodging tax remittance required.
- **State of New Jersey** — state-level STR registration; Casino Reinvestment Development Authority (CRDA) overlays in some zones.
- **Atlantic County** — county tourism tax applies.
- **HOA / condo rules** — common in beachfront towers; some carry 7-day or longer minimums.

See the City of Atlantic City licensing department page for current rules.

## Submarkets that matter

- **Boardwalk / Marina District** — casino-adjacent; condo-heavy.
- **Ducktown / Chelsea** — historic SFR; lower ADR; quieter demand.
- **Ventnor / Margate (separate cities)** — different ordinances, often more residential; covered in their own guides.
- **Brigantine (separate city)** — family beach; separate rules.

## The 3 mistakes buyers make here

1. **Modeling weekend-leisure economics.** AC demand is heavily mid-week casino-driven. Build the pro forma around that pattern.
2. **Confusing AC with Ventnor / Margate / Brigantine.** Different cities, different rules.
3. **Ignoring NJ state STR registration.** State-level compliance is mandatory and audited.
