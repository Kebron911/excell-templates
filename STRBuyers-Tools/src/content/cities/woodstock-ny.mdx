## Should you buy an STR in Woodstock in 2026?

Conditionally. Woodstock is a low-priced point of entry into the Catskills STR market with a durable cultural-tourism brand. ADR $157, occupancy 56%, RevPAR $88, market score 56/100. The town has been tightening STR rules in response to neighborhood pushback — verify current posture before offer.

## Regulation: where the city stands

Town of Woodstock STR rules:

- **STR permit / annual registration** required, with primary-residence and second-home distinctions.
- **Density / parking rules** vary by zoning district.
- **Ulster County** room-occupancy tax stacks on New York State sales tax — total around 12-14%.
- **New York State STR registry (in development; pilot in NYC, broader rollout pending)** adds a state-level disclosure layer.

Note: Woodstock the town is in Ulster County, NY — not Vermont. The Woodstock '69 festival was actually held in Bethel, NY, but the cultural-tourism brand attaches to the town. Bearsville, West Hurley, Saugerties (nearby separately governed) have their own STR rules.

Demand: weekend escapes from NYC (2.5-3 hours), summer arts festivals, fall foliage, winter skiing at Hunter and Belleayre (nearby). Weekday occupancy is materially lower than weekend.

## Submarkets that matter

- **Woodstock Village** — walkable Tinker Street, premium ADR, parking-restricted.
- **Bearsville / Wittenberg** — wooded SFR, cabin product, family demand.
- **Bythe Hollow / Glasco Turnpike** — rural, longer drive to town, cheaper basis.
- **Saugerties (adjacent)** — different town, different STR rules, Hudson River access.

## The 3 mistakes buyers make here

1. **Underwriting weekday occupancy at weekend rates.** Friday-Saturday-Sunday is the demand. Reserve cash for weekday softness.
2. **Confusing Woodstock with Saugerties or Bearsville.** Each town governs separately.
3. **Buying without checking the current Town of Woodstock STR ordinance.** Rules have tightened in recent years and continue to evolve.
