## Should you buy an STR in Gulfport in 2026?

Yes, selectively. Gulfport is the larger, less casino-centric, more residential half of the Mississippi Gulf Coast metro. $189 ADR at 58% occupancy is competitive, and the 74/100 market score holds up. Demand here leans toward family leisure, military (Keesler AFB-adjacent), and Gulfport-Biloxi International Airport business travel. The same flood and wind issues that shape Biloxi apply, but Gulfport's higher elevation in some neighborhoods materially lowers insurance.

## Regulation: where the city stands

- **City of Gulfport** — STR allowed; registration and lodging tax remittance required.
- **Harrison County (unincorporated)** — permissive.
- **Mississippi Wind Pool** — state-backed windstorm market available where private won't bind.
- **Tourist development tax** — collected county-wide.

See the City of Gulfport STR ordinance for current rules.

## Submarkets that matter

- **East Beach / US-90 corridor** — beachfront condo and SFR; highest ADR.
- **Downtown Gulfport / Centennial Plaza** — emerging redevelopment; lower ADR.
- **Lyman / Orange Grove (north Gulfport)** — quieter SFR; lower insurance.
- **Long Beach edge** — adjacent municipality; similar economics.

## The 3 mistakes buyers make here

1. **Lumping Gulfport with Biloxi.** Different demand drivers (family + business vs. casino). Comp within the right city.
2. **Skipping the FEMA flood map.** Post-Katrina flood zone redraws still affect coastal Gulfport. Pull the elevation certificate.
3. **Ignoring wind pool dependency.** As in Biloxi, many properties require MS Wind Pool windstorm coverage.
