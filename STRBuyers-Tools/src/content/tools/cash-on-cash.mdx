## How cash-on-cash works

Cash-on-cash return measures what your invested dollars earn in year 1, expressed as
a percentage:

```
CoC = annual cash flow / total cash invested
```

Where:

- **annual cash flow** = annual rent − operating expenses − annual debt service
- **total cash invested** = down payment + closing + furnishing + reserves

This is in-pocket cash only — appreciation, principal pay-down, and tax benefits all
get tracked separately.

## What's a good number

For STRs in the 2025–26 market:

- **10%+** — strong. Pencils cleanly even with conservative occupancy.
- **6–10%** — solid, in line with most STR investors' buy boxes.
- **3–6%** — marginal. Re-check expenses; line items drift higher than projections.
- **Under 3%** — below most operators' hurdle.

If you're underwriting at 6% but the deal hits 8% in year 1, you have margin for the
inevitable surprises.

## FAQ

### What about appreciation?

Not in cash-on-cash. Track appreciation separately in your total return calculation.
Cash-on-cash is the in-pocket year-1 number; appreciation is realized at sale.

### What's the difference between cash-on-cash and ROI?

Cash-on-cash is year 1 only. ROI typically refers to multi-year total return
including appreciation and principal pay-down. Both are useful, both measure
different things.

### Should I include depreciation tax savings?

Most operators model after-tax cash flow separately. Pre-tax cash-on-cash is
the underwriting metric most lenders and investors use. Depreciation is a real
benefit but it complicates the comparison; most calculators publish pre-tax.
