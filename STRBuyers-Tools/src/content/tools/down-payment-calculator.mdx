## Loan types compared

The down-payment requirement for an STR depends entirely on which loan product you
qualify for. Four common paths:

- **Conventional investment** — 25% min down, lowest rate among investment options,
  but lender weighs your personal DTI and reserves.
- **DSCR loan** — 20% min down (some lenders 25%), no personal DTI check; qualifies
  on the property's projected cash flow alone. Slight rate premium.
- **Second home** — 10% min down, requires the property to be a reasonable distance
  from your primary and used as a vacation home. Many lenders restrict short-term
  rental on second-home loans, so verify.
- **FHA** — 3.5% down but ONLY for primary residence. Works for house-hack scenarios
  where you live in part of the property and STR the rest.

## How to use this calculator

1. Enter the **purchase price**.
2. Pick a **loan type**.
3. Read off the down payment dollars, loan amount, and a monthly P&I estimate.

## What this calculator doesn't include

PITIA — your full monthly payment also includes property taxes, insurance, and
HOA dues if applicable. Use the [DSCR calculator](/dscr-calculator) for the full
debt-service picture.

## FAQ

### Are these rates real?

The estimated rates are placeholders pinned to typical market levels (June 2026).
Your actual rate depends on credit score, LTV, property location, loan-level price
adjustments (LLPAs), and current market conditions. Get a real quote before relying
on the monthly P&I figure.

### Why does conventional require more down than DSCR?

Conventional investment loans are sold to Fannie/Freddie, which have stricter
LTV caps for non-owner-occupied. DSCR lenders are mostly portfolio lenders who can
write to their own guidelines.
