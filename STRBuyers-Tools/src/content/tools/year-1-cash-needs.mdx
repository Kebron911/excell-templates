## Why year-1 cash matters

Most STR buyers underestimate how much cash they need to land a property and operate
it through the slow first months. The headline down payment is only the first bucket.
This calculator captures the four real ones:

1. **Down payment** — 10–25% of purchase price depending on loan type
2. **Closing costs** — typically 2.5–4% of price (title, lender fees, prepaids)
3. **Furnishing** — bigger than buyers expect; see [furnishing budget](/furnishing-budget)
4. **Operating reserves** — most lenders want 6 months of PITIA on hand at close

Add them up; that's the all-in cash you need.

## How to use this calculator

1. Enter the **purchase price**.
2. Set the **down payment %** based on loan type (use the [down payment calculator](/down-payment-calculator) if you're not sure).
3. Default closing at 3% of price; bump to 4% in high-cost markets.
4. Use the [furnishing budget calculator](/furnishing-budget) to land a real number.
5. Set **reserve months** to whatever your lender requires + a buffer for surprises.
6. Estimate **monthly operating expense** as PITIA + utilities + property mgmt fees.

## What's typical

For a $425k purchase with DSCR financing:

- Down (20%): $85,000
- Closing (3%): $12,750
- Furnishing (2br mid): $21,000
- Reserves (6 months × $3,500/mo): $21,000
- **Total: ~$140,000** before bookings start

That's a real number that catches buyers off guard. The more accurate the model
upfront, the fewer surprises post-closing.

## FAQ

### Are reserves the lender's reserves or operating reserves?

Both, sometimes the same money. Lenders require 6 months of PITIA in liquid assets
at close. After close, that money becomes your operating reserve. Plan for the
higher of the two.

### Can I count my retirement accounts as reserves?

Most lenders allow 60–70% of retirement accounts toward reserves. Some don't allow
any. Ask before assuming.

### What if I'm using a HELOC for the down payment?

The HELOC draw counts as cash invested for cash-on-cash purposes. Reserves still
need to be liquid (not the HELOC line). Many lenders also have seasoning requirements
on borrowed-for-down-payment funds.
