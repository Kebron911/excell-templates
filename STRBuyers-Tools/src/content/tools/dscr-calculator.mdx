## How DSCR works

DSCR — Debt Service Coverage Ratio — is the metric DSCR lenders use to qualify a loan
based on the property's cash flow rather than the borrower's personal income. The
math is simple:

```
DSCR = monthly rent / monthly PITIA
```

Where PITIA = Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance + Association dues.

A DSCR of 1.0 means the property exactly covers its debt service. 1.25+ is what
lenders consider a strong asset; below 1.0 means the property doesn't fully cover
itself, and the borrower has to make up the gap with a larger down payment or accept
a rate hit.

## How to use this calculator

1. Enter your **expected monthly gross rent**. For STRs, lenders typically accept either
   the appraiser's 1007 form (long-term comparable rent) or an STR data source like
   AirDNA. Use whichever matches your lender's underwriting policy.
2. Enter your **monthly PITIA payment**. If you haven't locked a rate, model two
   scenarios — a base rate and a rate 50 bps higher — to see how sensitive the DSCR
   is to financing terms.
3. The calculator returns the ratio + a lender-tier label.

## What lenders actually do

Most STR DSCR lenders we've worked with publish minimum DSCRs in the 0.75–1.0 range
but adjust pricing based on tier:

- **DSCR ≥ 1.25** — best rate, full 75–80% LTV
- **DSCR 1.00–1.24** — qualifying, expect 25–50 bps add-on, sometimes 5% LTV haircut
- **DSCR 0.75–1.00** — case-by-case, larger down required, full rate add-ons
- **DSCR < 0.75** — pass

## FAQ

### Should I use long-term or STR projections for "rent"?

Depends on the lender. The most STR-friendly lenders (Visio, Kiavi, Lima One) accept
short-term-rental projections from AirDNA or similar — but they discount the projection
~10–20% for underwriting. Other lenders only use the 1007 form's long-term rent.
Ask up front; the answer drives whether the property pencils.

### What goes into PITIA?

Principal + Interest from the loan amortization schedule, plus annual property taxes
divided by 12, plus annual insurance divided by 12, plus monthly HOA/Association dues
if applicable. Get a real quote from your lender before relying on the number.

### Does AirCover or platform fees count?

No. PITIA is mortgage-side only. Booking platform fees, cleaning, supplies, and
property management are operating expenses tracked separately in cash-on-cash and
NOI calculations.
