## How comp analysis works

Three comparable listings is the minimum sample size that gives you a real signal.
One comp is anecdote. Two comps could both be wrong in the same direction. Three
comps with a tight spread give you confidence; three comps with a wide spread tell
you there's hidden variance worth investigating.

The math is simple per-listing:

```
booked nights = 365 × occupancy %
annual rent   = booked nights × nightly rate
cleaning rev  = (booked nights / 3) × cleaning fee   ← assumes 3-night avg stay
annual gross  = annual rent + cleaning rev
```

The summary shows average, max-min spread, and a label.

## How to read the spread

- **Tight (under 15%)** — comps agree, the projected revenue is reliable
- **Moderate (15–30%)** — average is a reasonable midpoint, but understand what
  drives the difference (location, amenities, season)
- **Wide (over 30%)** — at least one outlier. Don't trust the average until you've
  identified what's different about the high or low listing

## How to use this calculator

1. Find 3 comparable listings on Airbnb/Vrbo (same bed count, same area).
2. Pull each listing's nightly rate, occupancy estimate (AirDNA / PriceLabs), and
   cleaning fee.
3. Enter all three. The shareable URL captures every input.

## FAQ

### How do I get occupancy data?

AirDNA and PriceLabs publish occupancy by ZIP and listing. AirDNA has a free comp
viewer with rough numbers; their Pro tier is needed for serious comp work.

### What if my comps have very different sizes?

Don't compare a 2-bed to a 4-bed and expect the spread to be tight. Match comps on
bedroom count, bathroom count, and amenity tier (hot tub, pool, view) before running
the calculator.

### Why divide booked nights by 3 for cleaning?

US average stay length is ~3 nights. If your market trends shorter (urban) or longer
(beach/mountain), adjust your cleaning-fee inputs accordingly — bump the cleaning
fee for shorter stays or scale down occupancy effect.
