The most common Google search for first-time STR buyers is some flavor of *"how much down payment for Airbnb?"* — and the most common answer online is wrong, because it ignores the loan product and the occupancy classification.

The truthful answer: **3.5% to 30%+, depending on what you're buying and how you're buying it.** Here's the breakdown.

## The five down-payment tiers

| Scenario | Down payment | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| FHA owner-occupied 1–4 unit | 3.5% | Must occupy 12+ months. STR income from non-occupied units allowed. |
| VA owner-occupied 1–4 unit | 0% | Eligible veterans only. Same occupancy rule. |
| Conventional 2nd home | 10% | Limited STR usage allowed; lender will scrutinize. |
| Conventional investment SFH | 20–25% | Non-owner-occupied. Reserves required. |
| Conventional investment 2–4 unit | 25–30% | Higher risk class. |
| DSCR investment | 15–25% | Property-based qualification. |
| Hard money / bridge | 10–30% | Short-term. Used for rehab or fast-close situations. |

So if you're a buyer with no W-2 issues, no veteran status, and no plans to live in the property, you're realistically planning for **20–25% down**. On a $450,000 STR property that's $90,000–$112,500 just for the down payment — before closing costs, reserves, and furnishing.

## What the down-payment calculator won't tell you

The cash you actually need to close is meaningfully more than the down payment line. A defensible STR cash-to-close estimate includes:

1. **Down payment** — 20–25% of purchase price for most investment scenarios.
2. **Closing costs** — 2–4% of purchase price (lender fees, title, escrow, recording, prepaid taxes & insurance, transfer tax).
3. **Reserves** — 6 months of PITI minimum, often required by the lender. Don't model this as optional; the lender will hold the loan if you don't show it.
4. **Furniture, fixtures & equipment (FF&E)** — $15,000–$60,000 depending on property size and design tier. STR-grade furniture, durable bedding, dishware, smart locks, cameras, signage, art.
5. **Photography + listing setup** — $500–$2,500 for professional photos and copy. Don't skip this.
6. **First two months of operating cash** — utilities, internet, cleaning, supplies, software (PMS, dynamic pricing, lock platform). Most STRs don't hit stabilized occupancy until month 3–4.

## Why "10% down for a second home" is a trap

You'll see a lot of forum posts pitching the second-home loan as a hack: 10% down, conventional rate, lower than investment pricing. It's real, but it has hard limits.

- The property must be **at least 50 miles from your primary residence**.
- It must be **available for your personal use** at all times — meaning no full-time rental management contract, no rental management fee in your loan file.
- Lenders require an **annual signed occupancy certification** that you used the property as a second home.
- Fannie Mae's 2022 second-home loan-level price adjustment (LLPA) raised pricing on these loans materially — it's no longer the deal it was in 2019.

If you treat a 10%-down second home loan as a sneak path to a full-time STR, you're committing occupancy fraud. Lenders audit this with phone calls, utility bills, and tax-return cross-checks, and an audit hit can trigger a "due on sale" call. Do not do this.

## Down payment vs. reserves vs. cash-to-close

A clean way to think about it on a $450K STR purchase:

- **Down payment (25%):** $112,500
- **Closing costs (3%):** $13,500
- **Reserves (6 months PITI):** ~$18,000
- **FF&E + setup:** $35,000
- **Operating cash (60 days):** $5,000
- **Total cash needed at the closing table or shortly after:** ~$184,000

That's the gap most first-time STR buyers don't see in the down payment number. The deal that pencils with $112K down does not pencil with $184K of cash deployed — your cash-on-cash return changes meaningfully.

## Pitfalls

- **Confusing list price with appraised value.** Lenders fund off the lower number. If the appraisal comes in $20K under list, you owe the difference in additional down payment or you renegotiate.
- **Underestimating reserves.** Some DSCR lenders ask for 12 months of reserves on a vacation/STR property. Verify before you spend down your liquidity.
- **Ignoring the seasonal cash gap.** A property that nets $4K/month at stabilized occupancy may net $0 in shoulder season month one. Two months of operating cash is a floor, not a goal.
- **Forgetting the LLC formation cost.** If you're titling the property in an LLC (recommended for most investors), budget $300–$1,500 for entity formation, EIN, registered agent, and operating agreement.

The down payment is the headline number. The cash-to-close is the real number. Plan around the second one.
