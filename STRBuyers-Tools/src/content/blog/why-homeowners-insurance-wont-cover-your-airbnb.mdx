The story your insurance agent doesn't open the conversation with: standard homeowners insurance excludes commercial use, and listing your property on Airbnb is — in the eyes of most carriers — commercial use. A guest slip-and-fall claim on a homeowners-only policy gets denied. A fire caused by a guest gets denied or paid out at significant pushback. The carrier may cancel mid-policy when they discover the listing.

Airbnb's "Host Damage Protection" and "Host Liability Insurance" aren't insurance, despite the marketing. They are guarantee programs with claim-by-claim discretion. They're not what you build a coverage plan around.

Here's the actual coverage stack STR owners need.

## The four coverage layers

**1. Property coverage on a commercial / STR-endorsed policy**

This replaces your homeowners policy. The two structures most STR owners use:

- **Hybrid endorsement** — your existing homeowners carrier adds a commercial-use rider (Lloyd's of London, CBIZ, some regional carriers). Cheapest path if available, but rider availability varies sharply by state.
- **Commercial STR-specific policy** — Proper Insurance, Steadily, Foremost, others. Built ground-up for STR. Higher premium ($1,500–$4,000/year on a $500K property) but cleaner coverage and no surprise denial.

The hybrid path costs less. The commercial path almost never gets denied at claim time. For a property that's the centerpiece of an investment thesis, the commercial path is usually the right answer.

**2. Liability coverage — at least $1M, ideally $2M**

A guest fall, dog bite, hot tub burn, or pool accident can trigger a six- or seven-figure claim. Standard homeowners liability caps at $300K–$500K and excludes commercial guest activity. STR-specific liability runs $1M–$2M baseline and covers guest activity by definition.

**3. Loss of business income**

Your roof gets damaged and the property is uninhabitable for 4 months. The repair is covered, but the $40K of bookings you lose during repairs is only covered if you have business income coverage. Without it, you're paying mortgage on a property generating zero. Add this rider; it's typically $100–$400/year.

**4. Umbrella policy — $1M–$5M on top**

A personal umbrella sitting above your STR-specific liability and your auto/home liability is the cheapest dollar of insurance you'll ever buy — $300–$800/year for $1M of additional coverage. If you own multiple rentals or have personal assets to protect, this is non-negotiable.

## What Airbnb's "AirCover" actually covers

AirCover (Airbnb's host protection program) is real, but it's narrow and discretionary:

- **$1M Host Liability** — covers guest injuries / property damage **caused by the guest** during a stay booked on Airbnb. It does **not** cover injuries caused by hazards on the property, structural defects, or anything you would have insured against on a homeowners policy.
- **$3M Host Damage Protection** — covers property damage caused by a guest, with a long list of exclusions and a documentation-heavy claim process.
- **Pet damage coverage** — capped, with exclusions.

AirCover is **secondary**, not primary. If you have a primary STR insurance policy, that policy adjudicates first. Airbnb may pay above your policy limits or in scenarios your policy excludes — or they may decline. It's a backstop, not a coverage strategy.

Vrbo's equivalent (Booking Booster, Vrbo Liability Insurance) has a similar structure and similar limits.

## The five claim scenarios most homeowners policies deny

These are the scenarios I've seen denied or contested on standard homeowners policies that are listed on Airbnb:

1. **Guest slip-and-fall in the kitchen.** Commercial guest = excluded peril.
2. **Guest hot tub burn.** Commercial use of the hot tub.
3. **Guest dog bites another guest at the property.** Bites are already a fight on most homeowners policies; commercial guest activity removes the doubt.
4. **Fire from guest cooking.** Some carriers will pay; many will deny if they discover the property was listed on Airbnb at the time of loss.
5. **Theft of guest belongings.** Your policy doesn't cover guest belongings, period — but the guest's lawsuit against you for negligence is the part that hurts, and your liability coverage is the answer.

## What it actually costs

For a typical $450K STR (single-family, 3BR, no pool):

- Hybrid endorsement (where available): $1,200–$2,200/year
- Proper Insurance / Steadily commercial STR policy: $1,800–$3,500/year
- $1M umbrella: $300–$600/year
- Loss of business income rider: $100–$300/year

Total: **$2,200–$6,400/year**. Budget $3,000–$4,000 as a planning number for a non-pool property in a non-coastal market. Coastal Florida, California wildfire zone, or any property with a pool can push higher.

## Pitfalls

- **"My homeowners agent said it's fine."** Get the carrier's commercial-use exclusion in writing. Most agents don't know their own policy language well enough on this question.
- **Listing on Airbnb without telling your insurer.** Material misrepresentation. Carrier can cancel mid-policy and rescind any open claims.
- **Treating AirCover as your insurance.** It's a guarantee program, not a policy. There's no claim adjuster you can sue.
- **Underinsuring the structure.** Replacement cost on STR construction has gone up 30–50% since 2020. Pull a fresh replacement-cost quote, don't carry over the 2020 number.
- **Forgetting the LLC's name on the policy.** If you title in an LLC and the policy is in your personal name, the carrier may deny or require a re-write.

Insurance is the line item buyers want to skip and operators wish they hadn't. Get the right policy in place before the first guest checks in — preferably before close.
