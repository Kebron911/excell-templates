The pro forma you'll see in Bigger Pockets and most YouTube STR breakdowns shows down payment, closing costs, and "$15K furnishing budget." That's three line items. The reality is fifteen, and the gap between them is the reason 30%+ of new STR operators run out of cash before they hit stabilized occupancy.

Here's the full Year 1 stack for a typical $400–500K STR purchase.

## Pre-close: the cash you spend before the deal closes

| Line | Typical range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Earnest money | 1–3% of price | Held in escrow. Goes to closing or back to you. |
| Inspection | $400–$800 | More for septic/well/pool. |
| Appraisal | $500–$900 | Often higher in resort markets. |
| Loan application & lock fees | $0–$1,500 | Depends on lender. |
| Title and survey | $200–$2,000 | Survey only if required by lender or title. |
| LLC formation | $300–$1,500 | If you're titling in an entity. |

Subtotal: **$1,400 to $9,700** out of pocket before you ever sit at the closing table.

## Closing day

| Line | Typical range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Down payment | 20–25% of price | $80,000–$125,000 on a $500K deal. |
| Loan origination, points, processing | 0.5–2% of loan | Highly lender-dependent. |
| Title insurance, escrow, recording | 0.5–1.5% of price | Paid by buyer in most states. |
| Prepaid taxes, insurance, interest | 1–2% of price | Lender impound + per diem. |
| Transfer / recording tax | 0–1.5% of price | State-dependent. |

Subtotal: **20–30% of purchase price**, usually around 23–26%.

## Setup: the 90 days after closing

This is the bucket that buyers most consistently underestimate.

**Furniture, fixtures & equipment (FF&E)** — the single biggest line:

- **2BR property:** $18,000–$35,000
- **3BR property:** $28,000–$55,000
- **4BR+ property:** $45,000–$90,000+

A defensible STR-tier furnishing budget is roughly **$8,000–$14,000 per bedroom** for a property that needs to compete in a destination market. That covers durable beds and bedding, dining + living seating, dressers, bedside tables, lighting, art, kitchen-stocked-to-host-stays, dishware, cookware, towels, linens (in 3x sets per bed), and outdoor furniture if applicable.

Going below that range is possible — IKEA-everything builds cost less. They also generate more 4-star reviews and lower-tier ADR.

**Smart-home infrastructure:**
- Smart locks (one per door, $250–$500 each)
- Noise sensors (NoiseAware, Minut: $80–$150 per unit + $10/mo)
- Security cameras at exterior entry points (Ring, Reolink: $100–$300)
- Smart thermostat ($150–$250)
- Wi-Fi mesh or commercial-grade router ($200–$500)

Budget another **$1,500–$3,000** for smart-home setup.

**Photography + listing setup:**
- Professional STR photography: $500–$2,500
- Listing copy (DIY or hire): $0–$800
- Floor plan + 3D tour: $200–$700
- OTA setup: free, but ~10 hours of your time

Budget **$1,000–$3,500** for getting the listing live.

## Operating: the recurring cost stack

Once you're live, the monthly burn is heavier than most pro formas show:

| Category | Monthly | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Mortgage (PI) | $2,000–$3,500 | Loan-dependent. |
| Property tax (escrowed) | $300–$1,500 | State/county-dependent. Resort areas often higher. |
| Insurance | $150–$500 | STR-specific policy, not standard homeowners. |
| HOA dues (if applicable) | $0–$800 | |
| Utilities (electric, gas, water/sewer) | $200–$500 | Higher than long-term rental — guests don't conserve. |
| Internet | $80–$120 | Reliable speed required. |
| Streaming subscriptions | $30–$60 | Netflix + one or two others. |
| Trash/recycling | $30–$80 | |
| Cleaning supplies + consumables | $100–$300 | Coffee, paper goods, soap, toiletries. |
| Cleaning labor | varies | Usually passed to guest as cleaning fee. |
| Lawn / pool / hot tub maintenance | $100–$600 | Property-dependent. |
| Pest control | $30–$80 | |
| PMS / channel manager (Hospitable, Hostfully) | $35–$80 | |
| Dynamic pricing (PriceLabs, Wheelhouse) | $20–$30 | |
| Lock platform (RemoteLock, August) | $5–$15 | |
| Bookkeeping software | $20–$60 | |

Realistic monthly operating cost (excluding mortgage and cleaning labor that's passed through to the guest): **$1,000–$2,500/month**.

## Year 1 cash totals

For a $450K STR at 25% down, here's a defensible Year 1 cash deployment:

| Bucket | Cash |
|---|---|
| Pre-close diligence | $5,000 |
| Closing day (down payment + closing costs) | $135,000 |
| FF&E (3BR mid-tier) | $40,000 |
| Smart-home + photography + listing setup | $5,000 |
| 6 months operating reserves (lender requirement) | $20,000 |
| First 60 days operating cash | $5,000 |
| LLC + insurance + setup misc | $3,000 |
| **Total Year 1 cash** | **~$213,000** |

That is the number to plan around. If you have $135,000 ready and you think you're ready to close, you're not — you're roughly $80,000 short of a comfortable Year 1.

## Pitfalls

- **The "I'll furnish it as I go" plan.** Doesn't work. The listing needs to be photo-ready on day one or you eat 2–3 months of zero bookings while you finish setup.
- **Skipping STR-specific insurance.** Standard homeowners voids commercial use. Get a Proper or Steadily policy in the loan file before close.
- **Furnishing on credit cards.** $40K of FF&E at 24% APR is a $9,600/year drag on year 1 cash flow before you even host a guest.
- **Not budgeting for the slow month.** Every market has 1–3 weak months a year. Run the math with one month of zero net revenue and verify you survive.

The number you should plan around is total Year 1 cash, not down payment.
