# STRManuals — Conversion & Commerce Audit
**Date:** 2026-05-13
**Scope:** strmanuals.com (Path B static + Stripe payment links + n8n fulfillment)
**Method:** Read homepage, PDP template, free, thank-you, refund, EmailCapture, BuyButton, seo.ts, DEPLOY-STATUS.md.
**Verdict:** Strong foundation. **Two revenue-blocking gaps**, **three high-leverage conversion lifts**, and a clear path to organic growth.

---

## Critical — fix before driving paid traffic

### C1. Thank-you page can't deliver the download
**File:** `site/src/pages/thank-you.astro:18-23`

```astro
<h3 class="mb-4" id="manual-title">Loading...</h3>
<a href="#" class="btn-primary" id="download-link">Download PDF →</a>
```

The page sits there with "Loading..." forever — no client JS reads the Stripe success-URL params to fill in the manual title or download link. Customer pays, lands here, sees nothing actionable, has to wait for the email.

**Fix:** Either
- (a) Inline a `<script>` that parses `?sku=...&order=...` from the Stripe success URL and populates the link (Stripe lets you template the success URL with `{CHECKOUT_SESSION_ID}` + custom metadata), OR
- (b) Drop the "Your manual" card entirely and lean into "check your email — download link is on its way" + a /downloads re-request mechanism (the page already references `/downloads` which doesn't exist).

Path (b) is simpler and matches Path B's "email is the channel" architecture. Pick one and ship.

### C2. Homepage email form still posts to `/api/subscribe`
**File:** `site/src/components/EmailCapture.astro:23`

```astro
<form action="/api/subscribe" method="POST" ...>
```

In Path B (static-only), `/api/subscribe` doesn't exist. The free-page form was migrated to the n8n webhook; this generic EmailCapture component on the homepage was not. **Every homepage email signup currently 404s.**

**Fix:** Either point at the same n8n webhook (`${PUBLIC_N8N_WEBHOOK_BASE}/webhook/lead-magnet-strmanuals-tax-explainer`) or take a different magnet via a prop. Otherwise the homepage and any other page rendering `<EmailCapture />` is silently broken.

### C3. Per-order download URLs are still build-time hashed, not signed
**File:** `STRManuals/docs/PDF-HOSTING.md` (referenced) + `scripts/copy-pdfs-to-dist.mjs`

The hash gate (`/dl/<HASH>/<slug>/v1.pdf`) is **the same for every customer**. The moment one buyer shares their email's link in a Facebook group, anyone can download for free until you rotate the hash (which then breaks every existing customer's email).

**Fix:** Move to per-order signed URLs — n8n W01b composes `?token=<HMAC(orderId+sku+exp)>` and a tiny Cloudflare Worker (or n8n itself) validates before serving. ~2 hours of n8n + worker work. Pirating a link won't help anyone past 7 days or whatever expiry you set.

Lower-effort interim: rotate `STRMANUALS_DOWNLOAD_HASH` monthly and re-send active customers a fresh link. Buys time, not great UX.

## High leverage — conversion lifts

### H1. Zero social proof on PDPs
**File:** `site/src/pages/manuals/[slug].astro`
**SEO field exists:** `aggregateRating` in `seo.ts:65-68` but never populated.

Paid PDF, $19–$39 price tag, "trust me, it's good" copy — the single biggest conversion lever you don't have. Nothing on the PDPs says "X people bought this" or "5★ from Y reviews."

**Fix sequence:**
1. **Week 1:** Add an honest "Launch reviews wanted — get 50% off in exchange for a public review" CTA to the first 20 customers via the post-purchase email. Free, ethical, and you get reviews fast.
2. **Week 2:** Wire a tiny review-display component (3 quotes + ★★★★★) on each PDP. Source the quotes from the `manuals/<sku>.reviews.json` file (gitops, no DB).
3. **Week 3:** Once a manual has ≥10 reviews, populate `aggregateRating` in the Product schema — eligible for star ratings in Google search results (massive SERP CTR lift).

**Don't fake it.** Schema.org rating without backing reviews is a structured-data policy violation that can get rich-results removed.

### H2. No sticky / re-pinned Buy button on long PDPs
PDPs are ~7 scrolling sections (cover → who-for → TOC → companion → MDX → disclaimer → FAQ → bottom buy → related). On mobile especially, the buy decision happens 60% of the way through, then the user has to scroll back to the top OR all the way down past FAQ to find a button.

**Fix:** Add a mobile-only sticky bottom bar (`fixed inset-x-0 bottom-0`) showing `{title} — ${price}` + Buy button. Hides on desktop where the sidebar is in view. Expected uplift on PDF e-commerce: 8–15%.

### H3. Free page promises a 4-email drip, but the drip workflow isn't confirmed built
**File:** `site/src/pages/free.astro` (confirmed state shows Day 0 / Day 2 / Day 4 / Day 7)

The Day-7 "48-hour subscriber-only price" is your highest-converting email — discount-driven, time-boxed, warm audience. If n8n W08 isn't shipping all four emails yet, this is leaving money on the table.

**Fix:** Verify n8n W08 exists, all 4 emails are written, and the Day 7 has a real discount code (e.g., `LAUNCH50` for 30% off, valid 48h after subscribe). Audit `DEPLOY-STATUS.md` says W08 was not built as of 2026-05-11 — confirm current state.

## Medium — polish & risk reduction

### M1. BuyButton silently degrades to `href="#"` if env var missing
**File:** `site/src/components/BuyButton.astro:14`

```ts
const href = paymentLink ?? (stripePriceId ? `/api/checkout?price=${stripePriceId}` : '#');
```

If `STRIPE_LINK_TAX_01` etc. aren't set at build time, the button renders looking normal but is pointer-events-disabled with `opacity: 0.5`. That's quiet enough that QA could miss it, and a customer would see a half-faded button and bounce.

**Fix:** Hard-fail the build if any required `STRIPE_LINK_*` env var is missing. Add a check to `astro.config.mjs` or a `scripts/validate-env.mjs` that runs in `prebuild`. Better: show an explicit "Coming soon — drop your email to be notified" state instead of disabled CTA.

### M2. Privacy & Terms pages are 33 / 47 lines — thin for a paid product handling tax/legal content
**Files:** `privacy.astro`, `terms.astro`

For paid content in a regulated genre (tax, legal), bare-minimum policies are a liability:
- Privacy must disclose: what data Stripe collects (PCI scope), what n8n holds (email), retention period, GDPR/CCPA rights, contact for deletion requests
- Terms must disclose: license scope ("you bought one PDF, not a redistribution license"), refund process pointer, governing law, dispute resolution

For tax/legal content specifically, a CYA disclaimer on every manual (already done via `legalDisclaimer: true`) is necessary but not sufficient — Terms should explicitly state "not a substitute for professional advice; no fiduciary relationship is created."

**Fix:** Bring privacy + terms up to ~150 lines each using a real template (Termly, GetTerms, or a $300 attorney pass). For a business projecting >$1k/mo, this is cheaper than a single compliance complaint.

### M3. No "Add to bundle and save" cross-sell on PDPs
The bundle saves $46. A buyer eyeing one $29 manual could be pushed to the $99 bundle with one well-placed card.

**Fix:** On every PDP, after the "Buy" section, add a "Get all five for $99 (save $46)" upsell card pointing at `/bundle`. Could also be a price modifier inline: "or $99 for all five →".

### M4. Disclosures page exists but no affiliate links seem present yet
**File:** `disclosures.astro` (not read — referenced by sitemap)

If you eventually add affiliate links (Turno, Touch Stay, etc. — sister sites use them), the disclosures page needs to actually disclose. Right now it's defensive coverage with nothing to defend against. Fine for now; revisit when affiliates land.

## Low — nice-to-have

### L1. No content marketing surface
STRManuals has no blog. That's an architectural decision (sister sites do the SEO heavy lifting and cluster-link to STRManuals). It's defensible if:
- Sister sites are actively pushing buyer-funnel traffic to STRManuals
- The free-page drip is converting subscribers efficiently

It's a risk if:
- Sister-site traffic is below expectations (no organic top-of-funnel)
- The free drip's open rate is mediocre (no warm channel)

**Optional add:** 4-6 "preview chapter" pages (`/preview/<slug>`) per manual — full chapter 1 published as web content, ungated, with a "want the other 9 chapters? $29" CTA at the end. Doubles as SEO surface + sales tool. ~4 hours per chapter to convert MDX.

### L2. No homepage hero image
Discussed in prior session. Cover grid IS the visual identity here. Adding a lifestyle photo would compete with the product art. **Skip.**

### L3. Bundle page hasn't been read in this audit
60 lines; assume passable. Worth verifying: does it surface all 5 covers + savings math + single CTA, or is it a generic "click here" page? Quick fix if needed.

## What this audit deliberately ignored

- **Visual design polish** — the site already has a deliberate, restrained editorial aesthetic. Not a problem.
- **Manual cover SVGs** — these are intentional product art; AI photos would hurt, not help.
- **Sister-site cluster funnel** — assumed working. Worth a separate test: how many strmanuals.com sessions arrive from each of the four sister sites? Tag the cross-promo links with UTM params and check GA4.
- **Pricing optimization** — current pricing ($19–$39, $99 bundle) is reasonable for the niche. Don't optimize before social proof exists; floor pricing only works with reviews to back it up.
- **Stripe Tax** — for a US-only PDF business under $X threshold per state, marketplace facilitator rules may make this a non-issue. Confirm with a CPA before turning it on (or not).
